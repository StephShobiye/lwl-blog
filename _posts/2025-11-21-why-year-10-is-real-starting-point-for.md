<h2 data-end="704" data-start="629"><strong data-end="704" data-start="632">A Parent Guide with Evidence from Welsh ALN Law and System Realities</strong></h2>
<p data-end="865" data-start="706">For years, families in <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">Wales</a> have been told that transition planning for <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+Further+Education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">Further Education</a> takes place in <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Year+11+age&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">Year 11</a>.<br data-end="823" data-start="820" />
On paper, it sounds simple and reassuring.</p>
<p data-end="1014" data-start="867">But for <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">ALN families</a>, waiting until Year 11 is often the single biggest factor behind <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=failed+transitions+further+education+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">failed transitions</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=broken+placements+further+education+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">broken placements</a>, and <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=delays+in+support+further+education+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">delays in support</a>.</p>
<p data-end="1034" data-start="1016">The truth is this:</p>
<h2 data-end="1137" data-start="1036">‚≠ê <strong data-end="1137" data-start="1041">Year 10 is the safest, most realistic, and legally aligned starting point for FE transition.</strong></h2>
<p data-end="1270" data-start="1139">This article explains why, what the law says, where the system falls down, and what parents can do to protect their child‚Äôs future.</p>

<h1 data-end="1530" data-start="1480">‚≠ê <strong data-end="1530" data-start="1484">What the ALN Law and ALN Code Actually Say</strong></h1>
<p data-end="1608" data-start="1532">A few key quotations from the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+Code+for+Wales+2021&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">ALN Code for Wales 2021</a> clarify the intention:</p>
<h3 data-end="1635" data-start="1610">üìò <strong data-end="1633" data-start="1617">Section 25.9</strong></h3>
<blockquote data-end="1743" data-start="1636">
<p data-end="1743" data-start="1638">‚ÄúTransition planning for a young person must begin at the earliest opportunity and no later than Year 9.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote>
<p data-end="1834" data-start="1745">This is a direct contradiction to the widely held belief that planning begins in Year 11.</p>
<h3 data-end="1861" data-start="1836">üìò <strong data-end="1859" data-start="1843">Section 26.6</strong></h3>
<blockquote data-end="1956" data-start="1862">
<p data-end="1956" data-start="1864">‚ÄúThe <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+IDP+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">IDP</a> must support a planned progression into further education, training or employment.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote>
<p data-end="2028" data-start="1958">A rushed meeting at the end of Year 11 cannot fulfil this requirement.</p>
<h3 data-end="2055" data-start="2030">üìò <strong data-end="2053" data-start="2037">Section 17.6</strong></h3>
<blockquote data-end="2175" data-start="2056">
<p data-end="2175" data-start="2058">‚ÄúA young person may continue to have an IDP until the age of 25 where it remains necessary for the plan to continue.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote>
<p data-end="2242" data-start="2177">Families need time to ensure that support <em data-end="2230" data-start="2219">continues</em> beyond CSA.</p>
<hr data-end="2247" data-start="2244" />
<h1 data-end="2302" data-start="2249">‚≠ê <strong data-end="2302" data-start="2253">The CSA Cliff Edge and Why It Matters So Much</strong></h1>
<p data-end="2373" data-start="2304"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+Compulsory+School+Age&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">Compulsory School Age</a> ends on the <strong data-end="2361" data-start="2338">last Friday in June</strong> of Year 11.</p>
<p data-end="2386" data-start="2375">After this:</p>
<ul data-end="2681" data-start="2388">
<li data-end="2415" data-start="2388">
<p data-end="2415" data-start="2390">school duties fall away</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2449" data-start="2416">
<p data-end="2449" data-start="2418">education legislation changes</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2483" data-start="2450">
<p data-end="2483" data-start="2452">monitoring of welfare reduces</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2553" data-start="2484">
<p data-end="2553" data-start="2486"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=LAs+Local+Authorities+Wales+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">LAs</a> prioritise younger children with higher statutory protections</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2623" data-start="2554">
<p data-end="2623" data-start="2556">no one sees the young person daily to monitor anxiety or distress</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2681" data-start="2624">
<p data-end="2681" data-start="2626">families lose the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+ALNCO+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">ALNCO</a> as a regular point of contact</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="2763" data-start="2683">This is one reason placements fail in <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+FE+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">FE</a> ‚Äî the scaffolding falls away overnight.</p>
<hr data-end="2768" data-start="2765" />
<h1 data-end="2819" data-start="2770">‚≠ê <strong data-end="2819" data-start="2774">Why Year 10 Is the Correct Starting Point</strong></h1>
<h3 data-end="2862" data-start="2821">‚úî Two full IDP reviews are required</h3>
<p data-end="2902" data-start="2863">One in Year 10<br data-end="2880" data-start="2877" />
One early in Year 11</p>
<p data-end="2990" data-start="2904">This gives time for assessments, NHS input, college liaison and multi agency planning.</p>
<h3 data-end="3029" data-start="2992">‚úî FE Colleges need early notice</h3>
<p data-end="3139" data-start="3030">They must plan staffing, access needs, pastoral support, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=sensory+regulation&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">sensory regulation</a>, and 1 to 1 support months ahead.</p>
<h3 data-end="3191" data-start="3141">‚úî EOTAS and EHE learners need even more time</h3>
<p data-end="3373" data-start="3192">Elective Home Educators (EHE) do not have daily school observation data or ALNCO oversight.<br data-end="3286" data-start="3283" />
Their evidence base is thinner, and professionals often misunderstand their trajectory.</p>
<p data-end="3392" data-start="3375">For EHE families:</p>
<h2 data-end="3471" data-start="3394">‚≠ê <strong data-end="3469" data-start="3399">‚ÄúStart in Year 10‚Äù means ‚Äúbegin planning at age 14 at the latest.‚Äù</strong></h2>
<p data-end="3629" data-start="3472">Many EHE learners transition at 16, 17, 18, or 19 when developmentally ready ‚Äî not by chronological year group ‚Äî so using age, not year labels, is essential.</p>
<p data-end="3655" data-start="3631">This is crucial because:</p>
<ul data-end="3893" data-start="3657">
<li data-end="3730" data-start="3657">
<p data-end="3730" data-start="3659">colleges need to understand developmental not chronological readiness</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3776" data-start="3731">
<p data-end="3776" data-start="3733">NHS waiting lists create gaps in evidence</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3840" data-start="3777">
<p data-end="3840" data-start="3779"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Careers+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">Careers Wales</a> cannot contact EHE families first due to <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+GDPR&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6095214323524225430" target="_blank">GDPR</a></p>
</li>
<li data-end="3893" data-start="3841">
<p data-end="3893" data-start="3843">EHE parents often must initiate everything alone</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="3972" data-start="3895">Starting late increases the risk of a failed or unsuitable college placement.</p>

<p data-end="4088" data-start="4061">Families frequently report:</p>

</li>
<li data-end="4233" data-start="4184">
<p data-end="4233" data-start="4186">college ALN teams receiving IDPs far too late</p></li>
<li data-end="4349" data-start="4305">
<p data-end="4349" data-start="4307">LAs prioritising compulsory school cases</p>

<h1 data-end="4590" data-start="4534">‚≠ê <strong data-end="4590" data-start="4538">Parent Action Plan for Year 10 and Age 14+ (EHE)</strong></h1>
<h2 data-end="4660" data-start="4592">‚úî Request a Year 10 IDP review with FE preparation as the focus</h2>
<p data-end="4701" data-start="4661">Use your legal right under the ALN Code.</p>
<h2 data-end="4740" data-start="4703">‚úî Contact Careers Wales yourself</h2>
<p data-end="4780" data-start="4741">They cannot contact EHE families first due to GDPR.</p>
<h2 data-end="4836" data-start="4782">‚úî Ask for written contributions from every agency</h2>
<p data-end="4856" data-start="4837">No verbal promises.</p>
<h2 data-end="4923" data-start="4858">‚úî Follow every call with an email confirming what was agreed</h2>
<blockquote data-end="4980" data-start="4924">
<p data-end="4980" data-start="4926">‚ÄúIt is my understanding that on X date, Y was agreed.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote>
<h2 data-end="5017" data-start="4982">‚úî Start gathering evidence now</h2>
<p data-end="5144" data-start="5018">Assessments<br data-end="5032" data-start="5029" />
Behaviour patterns<br data-end="5053" data-start="5050" />
Sensory triggers<br data-end="5072" data-start="5069" />
Emotional regulation notes<br data-end="5101" data-start="5098" />
Home school diary<br data-end="5121" data-start="5118" />
Screenshots<br data-end="5135" data-start="5132" />
Reports</p>
<h2 data-end="5192" data-start="5146">‚úî Make a Subject Access Request if needed</h2>
<p data-end="5248" data-start="5193">This reveals what is on file and corrects inaccuracies.</p>
<h2 data-end="5310" data-start="5250">‚úî Start visiting colleges from Year 10 or age 14 onward</h2>
<p data-end="5375" data-start="5311">Different campuses have different cultures and provision levels.</p>
