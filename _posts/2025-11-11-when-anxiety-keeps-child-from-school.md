In Wales, the law and local support systems recognise that emotionally based school avoidance (EBSA) is not defiance or ‚Äúnaughtiness,‚Äù but a sign that something deeper needs to be understood and addressed with care. Many families feel overwhelmed, especially when school staff seem unsure or when anxiety escalates at home. This guide is for you, a gentle roadmap through a tough moment, filled with practical steps and reassurance.</p><hr data-end="850" data-start="847" /><h2 data-end="907" data-start="852">üõë How Do I Get My Child Referred to CAMHS in Wales?</h2><p data-end="1065" data-start="909">This is one of the most common questions parents ask, and the answer depends slightly on where you live and what your child's current education setting is.</p><p data-end="1158" data-start="1067">In Wales, <strong data-end="1157" data-start="1077">CAMHS (Child and Adolescent Mental Health Services) referrals can be made by</strong>:</p><ul data-end="1432" data-start="1159">
<li data-end="1210" data-start="1159">
<p data-end="1210" data-start="1161">Your <strong data-end="1172" data-start="1166"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=GP+services+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">GP</a></strong> (this is still the most common route)</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1273" data-start="1211">
<p data-end="1273" data-start="1213">Your <strong data-end="1236" data-start="1218">child‚Äôs school</strong> (via the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+ALNCo+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">ALNCo</a> or school counsellor)</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1304" data-start="1274">
<p data-end="1304" data-start="1276">Your <strong data-end="1304" data-start="1281"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=school+nursing+team+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">school nursing team</a></strong></p>
</li>
<li data-end="1371" data-start="1305">
<p data-end="1371" data-start="1307">A <strong data-end="1326" data-start="1309"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=social+worker+role+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">social worker</a></strong>, if your family is involved with social care</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1432" data-start="1372">
<p data-end="1432" data-start="1374">Occasionally, by <strong data-end="1414" data-start="1390"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=hospital+consultants+role+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">hospital consultants</a></strong> or paediatricians</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="1672" data-start="1434">Some Health Boards (like <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Aneurin+Bevan+University+Health+Board&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">Aneurin Bevan</a>) have a <strong data-end="1507" data-start="1481"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+Single+Point+of+Access+mental+health+services+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">Single Point of Access</a></strong> system (e.g. <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=SPACE-Wellbeing+Aneurin+Bevan&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">SPACE-Wellbeing</a>), where professionals, and sometimes parents, can complete a shared referral form. We‚Äôll cover area-specific routes in a future post.</p><p data-end="2105" data-start="1674"><strong data-end="1701" data-start="1674">Can parents self-refer?</strong><br data-end="1704" data-start="1701" />
In most parts of Wales, direct parent self-referral is <strong data-end="1775" data-start="1759">not accepted</strong>. A professional must make the referral, but you can and should ask your school and/or GP to do this.<br data-end="1880" data-start="1877" />
We strongly recommend asking <strong data-end="1917" data-start="1909">both</strong>, and doing so in writing (email is fine). This gives you a paper trail in case school attendance issues arise later ‚Äî and protects you if you‚Äôre contacted by an <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Education+Welfare+Officer+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">Education Welfare Officer</a>.</p><h3 data-end="2144" data-start="2107">CAMHS referral routes by setting:</h3><ul data-end="2493" data-start="2145">
<li data-end="2226" data-start="2145">
<p data-end="2226" data-start="2147"><strong data-end="2175" data-start="2147">Mainstream school pupils</strong>: Ask the school‚Äôs ALNCo or pastoral lead to refer.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2316" data-start="2227">
<p data-end="2316" data-start="2229"><strong data-end="2252" data-start="2229">FE college students</strong>: Most colleges have mental health teams. If not, consult your GP.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2392" data-start="2317">
<p data-end="2392" data-start="2319"><strong data-end="2353" data-start="2319">Electively Home Educated (EHE)</strong>: Contact your GP or school nurse team.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2493" data-start="2393">
<p data-end="2493" data-start="2395"><strong data-end="2413" data-start="2395"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+EOTAS+learners&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">EOTAS learners</a></strong>: Your LA‚Äôs EOTAS team or linked therapist can initiate a referral with your GP.</p>
</li>
</ul><h3 data-end="2522" data-start="2495">What happens at age 18?</h3><p data-end="2811" data-start="2523">At 18, care transfers to <strong data-end="2587" data-start="2548">Adult Mental Health Services (AMHS)</strong>. If your child has an Individual Development Plan (IDP), transition support should be built into it. For students entering university, a <strong data-end="2763" data-start="2725">Disabled Students‚Äô Allowance (DSA)</strong> may be available to fund mental health support.</p><hr data-end="2816" data-start="2813" /><h2 data-end="2857" data-start="2818">CAMHS Support: What Does It Involve?</h2><p data-end="2879" data-start="2859">CAMHS teams provide:</p><ul data-end="2995" data-start="2880">
<li data-end="2897" data-start="2880">
<p data-end="2897" data-start="2882"><strong data-end="2897" data-start="2882">Assessments</strong></p>
</li>
<li data-end="2939" data-start="2898">
<p data-end="2939" data-start="2900"><strong data-end="2939" data-start="2900">Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)</strong></p>
</li>
<li data-end="2960" data-start="2940">
<p data-end="2960" data-start="2942"><strong data-end="2960" data-start="2942"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+Family+therapy&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">Family therapy</a></strong></p>
</li>
<li data-end="2995" data-start="2961">
<p data-end="2995" data-start="2963"><strong data-end="2995" data-start="2963">Medication where appropriate</strong></p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="3267" data-start="2997">If your child struggles to attend school, CAMHS may coordinate with staff to ease transitions or offer in-reach support in the classroom. They also help with strategies to manage anxiety at home. Sessions may take place in the clinic, by video call, or occasionally at home.</p><p data-end="3630" data-start="3269">If medication is prescribed, schools must follow a <strong data-end="3339" data-start="3320">healthcare plan</strong>. This is called an <strong data-end="3395" data-start="3359">Individual Healthcare Plan (IHP)</strong> and is usually coordinated by the school nurse, ALNCo, or pastoral lead. Parents remain closely involved. If your child has an <strong data-end="3559" data-start="3522">Individual Development Plan (IDP)</strong> for ALN, this healthcare plan can be attached or referenced within it.</p><hr data-end="3635" data-start="3632" /><h2 data-end="3675" data-start="3637">üå≥ What If Your Child Is in Crisis?</h2><p data-end="3902" data-start="3677">If your child is in <strong data-end="3717" data-start="3697">immediate danger</strong> (for example, has taken an overdose, is missing or actively attempting to self-harm), call <strong data-end="3816" data-start="3809">999</strong> or go straight to A&amp;E. You are not overreacting. Getting help quickly can save lives.</p><p data-end="3958" data-start="3904">For urgent mental health help that isn‚Äôt an emergency:</p><ul data-end="4217" data-start="3959">
<li data-end="4032" data-start="3959">
<p data-end="4032" data-start="3961"><strong data-end="3989" data-start="3961">NHS 111 Wales (Option 2)</strong> ‚Äì 24/7 crisis line: dial 111, then press 2</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4101" data-start="4033">
<p data-end="4101" data-start="4035"><strong data-end="4057" data-start="4035"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Papyrus+HOPELINEUK&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">Papyrus HOPELINEUK</a></strong> ‚Äì Call 0800 068 4141 or text 07860 039967</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4158" data-start="4102">
<p data-end="4158" data-start="4104"><strong data-end="4117" data-start="4104"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Childline&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">Childline</a></strong> ‚Äì 0800 1111 (confidential for under-19s)</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4217" data-start="4159">
<p data-end="4217" data-start="4161"><strong data-end="4184" data-start="4161"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=CALL+Helpline+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">CALL Helpline Wales</a></strong> ‚Äì 0800 132 737 (bilingual, 24/7)</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="4425" data-start="4219">You can also reach out to your CAMHS duty worker if your child is already under their care, or ask your GP for an urgent appointment. If you‚Äôre unsure what to do, start by calling 111 and asking for advice.</p><hr data-end="4430" data-start="4427" /><h2 data-end="4462" data-start="4432">üè´ What About School Fines?</h2><p data-end="4727" data-start="4464">This part worries many families. Under the Education Act 1996, parents are responsible for ensuring their child receives a suitable education. That‚Äôs why local authorities can issue <strong data-end="4665" data-start="4646"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=penalty+notices+school+attendance+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=7796261056151903717" target="_blank">penalty notices</a></strong> (fines) or even initiate <strong data-end="4706" data-start="4691">prosecution</strong> if attendance drops.</p><p data-end="4892" data-start="4729">But, and this is key, if a child‚Äôs anxiety is recognised as part of a medical or <strong data-end="4846" data-start="4812">Additional Learning Need (ALN)</strong>, this becomes a <strong data-end="4891" data-start="4863">valid reason for absence</strong>.</p><p data-end="4914" data-start="4894">To protect yourself:</p><ul data-end="5167" data-start="4915">
<li data-end="4975" data-start="4915">
<p data-end="4975" data-start="4917">Keep a <strong data-end="4942" data-start="4924">written record</strong> of all anxiety-related absences.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5025" data-start="4976">
<p data-end="5025" data-start="4978">Request CAMHS or ALN assessment <strong data-end="5024" data-start="5010">in writing</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5105" data-start="5026">
<p data-end="5105" data-start="5028">Ask the school to document steps taken (e.g., reduced timetable, counselling).</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5167" data-start="5106">
<p data-end="5167" data-start="5108">Keep copies of GP letters, therapist input, and all emails.</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="5381" data-start="5169">These documents provide evidence that you are actively engaging with services and doing everything you can to secure your child‚Äôs education ‚Äî even if that education cannot currently take place within a classroom.</p><hr data-end="5386" data-start="5383" /><h2 data-end="5433" data-start="5388">üéí How Schools Support Pupils with Anxiety</h2><p data-end="5694" data-start="5435">If your child‚Äôs anxiety makes learning difficult, the school must consider whether they have <strong data-end="5563" data-start="5528">Additional Learning Needs (ALN)</strong>. Under the ALN Code for Wales (2021), emotional and mental health needs are recognised as ALN when they impact access to learning.</p><p data-end="5754" data-start="5696">Once ALN is confirmed, the school or local authority must:</p><ul data-end="6023" data-start="5755">
<li data-end="5804" data-start="5755">
<p data-end="5804" data-start="5757">Create an <strong data-end="5804" data-start="5767">Individual Development Plan (IDP)</strong></p>
</li>
<li data-end="5955" data-start="5805">
<p data-end="5955" data-start="5807">Specify <strong data-end="5854" data-start="5815">Additional Learning Provision (ALP)</strong>&nbsp;- such as emotional regulation support, sensory adaptations, counselling, or exam access arrangements</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6023" data-start="5956">
<p data-end="6023" data-start="5958">Work with <strong data-end="5993" data-start="5968">parents and the child</strong> as equal partners in planning</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="6211" data-start="6025">Even if your child stops attending school, the local authority remains responsible for meeting their needs. That might mean EOTAS provision or moving the IDP from school to LA oversight.</p><hr data-end="6216" data-start="6213" /><h2 data-end="6256" data-start="6218">üíä Medication &amp; Therapies in School</h2><p data-end="6566" data-start="6258">If CAMHS prescribes medication, the school will obtain your consent and follow the guidance outlined in an <strong data-end="6393" data-start="6357">Individual Healthcare Plan (IHP)</strong>. This document outlines who gives the medication, where, and when. Most often, it‚Äôs a trained member of staff, not an untrained class teacher, and only with written agreement.</p><p data-end="6872" data-start="6568">If your child is receiving <strong data-end="6606" data-start="6595">therapy</strong> (e.g. CBT), it may not appear directly in the IHP unless sessions are on-site.
