<p data-end="711" data-start="561"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Health+and+social+care+support+Wales+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">Health and social care support</a> are two of the most inconsistently applied parts of the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+system+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">ALN system in Wales</a> — yet the legal duties are extremely clear.</p>
<p data-end="951" data-start="713">This article explains what MUST go into an <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+IDP&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">IDP</a>, based on the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Additional+Learning+Needs+and+Education+Tribunal+%28Wales%29+Act+2018&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">Additional Learning Needs and Education Tribunal (Wales) Act 2018</a> (<a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+ALN+Act+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">ALN Act</a>), the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+Code+for+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">ALN Code for Wales</a> (statutory), and the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Social+Services+and+Well-Being+%28Wales%29+Act+2014&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">Social Services and Well-Being (Wales) Act 2014</a> (<a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+SSWBA+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">SSWBA</a>).</p>
<hr data-end="956" data-start="953" />
<h2 data-end="1023" data-start="958"><strong data-end="1023" data-start="961">1. The Law: Health and Social Care MUST Contribute to IDPs</strong></h2>
<h3 data-end="1067" data-start="1025"><strong data-end="1067" data-start="1029">A. Duties Under the ALN Act (2018)</strong></h3>
<p data-end="1253" data-start="1069"><strong data-end="1107" data-start="1069">Health advice (ALN Act 2018, s.20)</strong><br data-end="1110" data-start="1107" />
A <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Local+Health+Board+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">Local Health Board</a> must provide the local authority or school with whatever advice and information they reasonably require to support an IDP.</p>
<p data-end="1464" data-start="1255"><strong data-end="1301" data-start="1255"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Health-related+ALP+Wales+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">Health-related ALP</a> (ALN Act 2018, s.18–19)</strong><br data-end="1304" data-start="1301" />
If a Health Board agrees that something is <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=health+related+Additional+Learning+Provision+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">health-related Additional Learning Provision</a>, then they must secure that provision. This is a binding statutory duty.</p>
<p data-end="1630" data-start="1466"><strong data-end="1511" data-start="1466"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Multi-agency+working+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">Multi-agency working</a> (ALN Act 2018, s.77)</strong><br data-end="1514" data-start="1511" />
Local authorities, Health Boards and social services must cooperate with each other in supporting children with ALN.</p>
<hr data-end="1635" data-start="1632" />
<h3 data-end="1695" data-start="1637"><strong data-end="1695" data-start="1641">B. Duties Under the ALN Code (Statutory Code 2021)</strong></h3>
<p data-end="1820" data-start="1697"><strong data-end="1744" data-start="1697">Evidence must be considered (ALN Code 20.2)</strong><br data-end="1747" data-start="1744" />
Evidence may come from other services, the child, or the child’s parents.</p>
<p data-end="1986" data-start="1822"><strong data-end="1882" data-start="1822">Evidence can include specialist services (ALN Code 20.3)</strong><br data-end="1885" data-start="1882" />
This includes <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=NHS+specialist+services+ALN+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">NHS</a> and private specialists (<a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=EP+education+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">EP</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=SALT+therapy+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">SALT</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=OT+therapy+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">OT</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=physio+therapy+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">physio</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=CAMHS+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">CAMHS</a>, paediatrics, audiology etc.).</p>
<p data-end="2133" data-start="1988"><strong data-end="2048" data-start="1988">Schools/LAs must seek specialist advice (ALN Code 20.20)</strong><br data-end="2051" data-start="2048" />
Authorities must request advice from appropriate specialist services where needed.</p>
<p data-end="2250" data-start="2135"><strong data-end="2181" data-start="2135">Health must provide advice (ALN Code 21.6)</strong><br data-end="2184" data-start="2181" />
Health Boards must respond to requests for advice and information.</p>
<p data-end="2407" data-start="2252"><strong data-end="2300" data-start="2252">Social care must contribute (ALN Code 21.10)</strong><br data-end="2303" data-start="2300" />
Local authorities must ensure social services provide advice and information to support IDP development.</p>
<p data-end="2541" data-start="2409"><strong data-end="2457" data-start="2409">Health ALP in the IDP (ALN Code 23.55–23.63)</strong><br data-end="2460" data-start="2457" />
Medical, sensory and therapeutic needs and provision must be recorded in the IDP.</p>
<p data-end="2711" data-start="2543"><strong data-end="2590" data-start="2543">When the LA must take over (ALN Code 23.67)</strong><br data-end="2593" data-start="2590" />
If the ALP required cannot be delivered in full at any maintained school, the LA must take responsibility for the IDP.</p>
<p data-end="2865" data-start="2713"><strong data-end="2759" data-start="2713">Right to request a review (ALN Code 25.21)</strong><br data-end="2762" data-start="2759" />
Parents may request an IDP review at any time, and the school or LA must arrange it unless unnecessary.</p>
<hr data-end="2870" data-start="2867" />
<h3 data-end="2945" data-start="2872"><strong data-end="2945" data-start="2876">C. Duties Under the Social Services &amp; Well-Being (Wales) Act 2014</strong></h3>
<p data-end="3076" data-start="2947"><strong data-end="2998" data-start="2947">Disability duty to assess (SSWBA 2014, s.21(5))</strong><br data-end="3001" data-start="2998" />
If a child may need care and support, the local authority must assess them.</p>
<p data-end="3253" data-start="3078"><strong data-end="3120" data-start="3078">Rights regardless of education setting</strong><br data-end="3123" data-start="3120" />
Disabled children retain their rights to social care even if they are:<br data-end="3196" data-start="3193" />
• in school<br data-end="3210" data-start="3207" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=EOTAS+education+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">EOTAS</a><br data-end="3220" data-start="3217" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=EHE+education+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">EHE</a><br data-end="3228" data-start="3225" />
• not in education at all</p>
<p data-end="3485" data-start="3255"><strong data-end="3301" data-start="3255">Social care support must appear in the IDP</strong><br data-end="3304" data-start="3301" />
Any disability-related need that affects learning must be recorded in the IDP, including <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=home+adaptations+for+disability&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">home adaptations</a>, equipment, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=handling+plans+disability+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">handling plans</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=PA+personal+assistant+disability&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">PAs</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=short+breaks+disability+respite+care&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">short breaks</a>, or safeguarding involvement.</p>
<hr data-end="3490" data-start="3487" />
<h2 data-end="3537" data-start="3492"><strong data-end="3537" data-start="3495">2. What Health MUST Provide for an IDP</strong></h2>
<p data-end="3611" data-start="3539">Based on the ALN Act s.20 and ALN Code 21.6, health bodies must provide:</p>
<p data-end="3838" data-start="3613">• written advice<br data-end="3632" data-start="3629" />
• medical information<br data-end="3656" data-start="3653" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=care+plans+disability+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">care plans</a><br data-end="3671" data-start="3668" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=therapy+recommendations+for+disability&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">therapy recommendations</a><br data-end="3699" data-start="3696" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=sensory+profiles+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">sensory profiles</a><br data-end="3720" data-start="3717" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=positioning+handling+plans+disability&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">positioning/handling plans</a><br data-end="3751" data-start="3748" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=risk+assessments+examples+disability&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">risk assessments</a><br data-end="3772" data-start="3769" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=feeding+swallowing+guidance+disability&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">feeding/ swallowing guidance</a><br data-end="3805" data-start="3802" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=fatigue+pacing+strategies+disability&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">fatigue and pacing strategies</a></p>
<p data-end="4043" data-start="3840">This is required even if:<br data-end="3868" data-start="3865" />
• the child is not on a waiting list<br data-end="3907" data-start="3904" />
• the service has “no capacity”<br data-end="3941" data-start="3938" />
• the child is on a diagnostic pathway<br data-end="3982" data-start="3979" />
• the child is EHE or EOTAS<br data-end="4012" data-start="4009" />
• the child has been discharged</p>
<hr data-end="4048" data-start="4045" />
<h2 data-end="4104" data-start="4050"><strong data-end="4104" data-start="4053">3. What MUST Appear in the IDP (Health Section)</strong></h2>
<p data-end="4155" data-start="4106">Under ALN Code 23.55–23.63, the IDP must include:</p>
<p data-end="4422" data-start="4157">• medical diagnoses<br data-end="4179" data-start="4176" />
• sensory and motor needs<br data-end="4207" data-start="4204" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=therapy+guidance+for+disability&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">therapy guidance</a><br data-end="4228" data-start="4225" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=physical+disability+requirements+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">physical disability requirements</a><br data-end="4265" data-start="4262" />
• mental health needs<br data-end="4289" data-start="4286" />
• safety plans<br data-end="4306" data-start="4303" />
• seizure plans<br data-end="4324" data-start="4321" />
• feeding/swallowing risks<br data-end="4353" data-start="4350" />
• fatigue and pain<br data-end="4374" data-start="4371" />
• specialist equipment<br data-end="4399" data-start="4396" />
• personal care needs</p>
<p data-end="4502" data-start="4424">These must be written in even if NHS staff do not supply full recommendations.</p>
<hr data-end="4507" data-start="4504" />
<h2 data-end="4548" data-start="4509"><strong data-end="4548" data-start="4512">4. What Social Care MUST Provide</strong></h2>
<p data-end="4693" data-start="4550">Under ALN Code 21.10 and SSWBA 2014, social care must provide advice and support where a child’s disability or home situation impacts learning.</p>
<p data-end="4712" data-start="4695">This may include:</p>
<p data-end="4908" data-start="4714">• disability assessments<br data-end="4741" data-start="4738" />
• home adaptations<br data-end="4762" data-start="4759" />
• equipment<br data-end="4776" data-start="4773" />
• manual handling plans<br data-end="4802" data-start="4799" />
• PA support<br data-end="4817" data-start="4814" />
• short breaks<br data-end="4834" data-start="4831" />
• risk management<br data-end="4854" data-start="4851" />
• safeguarding advice<br data-end="4878" data-start="4875" />
• support for family wellbeing</p>
<p data-end="4979" data-start="4910">These must appear in the IDP whenever they affect access to learning.</p>
<hr data-end="4984" data-start="4981" />

<p data-end="5170" data-start="5078">• sensory diet<br data-end="5095" data-start="5092" />

<h2 data-end="5634" data-start="5593"><strong data-end="5634" data-start="5596">6. What If Health Will Not Engage?</strong></h2>
<p data-end="5720" data-start="5636">If health refuses, delays, or says “lack of capacity,” parents can use this wording:</p>
<p data-end="5886" data-start="5722"><strong data-end="5886" data-start="5722">“ALN Act s.20 and ALN Code 21.6 require the Health Board to provide advice when requested.<br data-end="5817" data-start="5814" />
Could you confirm in writing whether this advice will be provided?”</strong></p>
<p data-end="5914" data-start="5888">The IDP must still record:</p>
<p data-end="5989" data-start="5916">• the need<br data-end="5929" data-start="5926" />
• the provision required<br data-end="5956" data-start="5953" />
• that health advice is pending</p>
<p data-end="6056" data-start="5991">The ALN system cannot leave gaps because a service did not reply.</p>
<p data-end="6198" data-start="6058">Parents can escalate through:<br data-end="6090" data-start="6087" />
• ALN Lead Officer<br data-end="6111" data-start="6108" />
• Health Board complaints<br data-end="6139" data-start="6136" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Welsh+Ombudsman+contact+details&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=4398662967261655579" target="_blank">Welsh Ombudsman</a><br data-end="6159" data-start="6156" />
• their MS<br data-end="6172" data-start="6169" />
• safeguarding if relevant</p>
<hr data-end="6203" data-start="6200" />
<h2 data-end="6261" data-start="6205"><strong data-end="6261" data-start="6208">7. When Health Needs Trigger an LA-Maintained IDP</strong></h2>
<p data-end="6305" data-start="6263">Under ALN Code 23.67, if the required ALP:</p>
<p data-end="6561" data-start="6307">• involves daily therapy programmes<br data-end="6345" data-start="6342" />
• requires medical oversight<br data-end="6376" data-start="6373" />
• involves multi-agency input<br data-end="6408" data-start="6405" />
• needs specialist equipment or training<br data-end="6451" data-start="6448" />
• is beyond school staff expertise<br data-end="6488" data-start="6485" />
• poses significant risk<br data-end="6515" data-start="6512" />
• cannot be delivered consistently in school</p>
<p data-end="6607" data-start="6563">…the Local Authority must take over the IDP.</p>
<p data-end="6680" data-start="6609">Schools cannot legally continue to maintain an IDP they cannot deliver.</p>
<hr data-end="6685" data-start="6682" />
<h2 data-end="6730" data-start="6687"><strong data-end="6730" data-start="6690">8. Parent Scripts (Legally Grounded)</strong></h2>
<p data-end="6901" data-start="6732"><strong data-end="6777" data-start="6732">When school tries to remove health needs:</strong><br data-end="6780" data-start="6777" />
“ALN Code 23.55–23.63 requires health-related needs and ALP to be included in the IDP. Could we ensure this is recorded?”</p>
<p data-end="7097" data-start="6903"><strong data-end="6940" data-start="6903">When health claims ‘no capacity’:</strong><br data-end="6943" data-start="6940" />
“Under ALN Act s.20 and ALN Code 21.6, the Health Board must provide advice when requested. Could you confirm in writing whether advice will be provided?”</p>
<p data-end="7218" data-start="7099"><strong data-end="7128" data-start="7099">When requesting a review:</strong><br data-end="7131" data-start="7128" />
“I am requesting a review under ALN Code 25.21 due to new evidence or changes in need.”</p>

<h1 data-end="7238" data-start="7225"><strong data-end="7238" data-start="7227">Summary</strong></h1>
<p data-end="7799" data-start="7240">✔ Health must provide advice (ALN Act s.20; ALN Code 21.6)<br data-end="7301" data-start="7298" />
✔ Social care must contribute where relevant (ALN Code 21.10)<br data-end="7365" data-start="7362" />
✔ Needs must be written into the IDP even if services do not engage<br data-end="7435" data-start="7432" />
✔ Health ALP must be included (ALN Code 23.55–23.63)<br data-end="7490" data-start="7487" />
✔ LA must take over the IDP when ALP is beyond school capacity (ALN Code 23.67)<br data-end="7572" data-start="7569" />
✔ Parents can request a review at any time (ALN Code 25.21)<br data-end="7634" data-start="7631" />
✔ The Social Services &amp; Well-Being Act guarantees disability support regardless of school attendance<br data-end="7737" data-start="7734" />
✔ All multi-agency needs must be recorded clearly in the IDP</p>
