<h2 data-end="1146" data-start="1020"><strong data-end="1144" data-start="1023">Health services, exam adjustments, attendance support, tuition, social care and practical steps for families in Wales</strong></h2>
<p data-end="1181" data-start="1147"><em data-end="1181" data-start="1147">A Learn Without Limits CIC Guide</em></p>

<p data-end="1480" data-start="1188">Part 1 explored how Long Covid affects children and why school becomes difficult.<br data-end="1272" data-start="1269" />
This second guide takes you through the systems that support your child.<br data-end="1347" data-start="1344" />
Families often face slow referrals, confusing attendance messages, uncertainty around exams and a lack of clarity about their rights.</p>

<h1 data-end="1631" data-start="1569"><strong data-end="1631" data-start="1571">1. Health services for children with Long Covid in Wales</strong></h1>
<p data-end="1805" data-start="1633">There is no single national paediatric Long Covid clinic. Provision varies across health boards. Some have structured pathways while others rely on general paediatric care.</p>
<h3 data-end="1849" data-start="1807"><strong data-end="1849" data-start="1811">1.1 What currently exists in Wales</strong></h3>
<p data-end="2152" data-start="1851"><strong data-end="1892" data-start="1851">Aneurin Bevan University Health Board</strong><br data-end="1895" data-start="1892" />
Provides the Paediatric Recovering from Illness Service, supporting children with persistent symptoms including fatigue and Long Covid. The team includes paediatric psychology, physiotherapy, occupational therapy and rehabilitation support.<br data-end="2138" data-start="2135" />
(See Source 1)</p>
<p data-end="2354" data-start="2154"><strong data-end="2193" data-start="2154">Swansea Bay University Health Board</strong><br data-end="2196" data-start="2193" />
Provides a recovery and rehabilitation pathway for people affected by Long Covid, including children referred for fatigue and pacing support.<br data-end="2340" data-start="2337" />
(See Source 2)</p>
<p data-end="2534" data-start="2356"><strong data-end="2388" data-start="2356">All Wales Covid Recovery App</strong><br data-end="2391" data-start="2388" />
A bilingual digital tool developed by clinical teams to support fatigue management, pacing, breathing and emotional wellbeing.<br data-end="2520" data-start="2517" />
(See Source 3)</p>
<h3 data-end="2572" data-start="2536"><strong data-end="2572" data-start="2540">1.2 When to request referral</strong></h3>
<p data-end="2603" data-start="2574">Request referral if symptoms:</p>
<p data-end="2749" data-start="2605">‚Ä¢ last beyond four to eight weeks<br data-end="2641" data-start="2638" />
‚Ä¢ affect your childs school attendance<br data-end="2682" data-start="2679" />
‚Ä¢ flare after activity<br data-end="2707" data-start="2704" />
‚Ä¢ affect learning or emotional wellbeing</p>
<p data-end="2811" data-start="2751">Ask for all information to be recorded in your childs notes.</p>

<h1 data-end="2848" data-start="2818"><strong data-end="2848" data-start="2820">2. Mental health support</strong></h1>
<p data-end="3030" data-start="2850">Children with Long Covid often experience emotional strain such as anxiety, sadness, frustration or loss of confidence.<br data-end="2972" data-start="2969" />
This is common and supported by research.<br data-end="3016" data-start="3013" />
(See Source 4)</p>
<p data-end="3063" data-start="3032">You can access support through:</p>
<p data-end="3252" data-start="3065">‚Ä¢ paediatric psychology<br data-end="3091" data-start="3088" />
‚Ä¢ GP linked mental health practitioners<br data-end="3133" data-start="3130" />
‚Ä¢ counselling through school<br data-end="3164" data-start="3161" />
‚Ä¢ youth counselling services<br data-end="3195" data-start="3192" />
‚Ä¢ the All Wales Covid Recovery App mental wellbeing tools</p>

<h1 data-end="3393" data-start="3329"><strong data-end="3391" data-start="3331">3. Exam access arrangements for children with Long Covid</strong></h1>
<p data-end="3438" data-start="3394"><em data-end="3438" data-start="3394">A clear explanation plus the legal context</em></p>
<p data-end="3638" data-start="3440">Many children with Long Covid struggle with fatigue, cognitive overload, slower processing and symptom flare during cognitive effort.<br data-end="3576" data-start="3573" />
This can make exams extremely challenging without adjustments.</p>
<p data-end="3807" data-start="3640">Under <strong data-end="3665" data-start="3646">JCQ regulations</strong>, any learner who experiences a substantial disadvantage in exams compared to their peers is entitled to Access Arrangements.<br data-end="3793" data-start="3790" />
(See Source 6)</p>
<h3 data-end="3854" data-start="3809"><strong data-end="3854" data-start="3813">3.1 Common adjustments for Long Covid</strong></h3>
<p data-end="4099" data-start="3856">‚Ä¢ supervised rest breaks<br data-end="3883" data-start="3880" />
‚Ä¢ extra time<br data-end="3898" data-start="3895" />
‚Ä¢ use of a word processor<br data-end="3926" data-start="3923" />
‚Ä¢ separate room<br data-end="3944" data-start="3941" />
‚Ä¢ modified paper format<br data-end="3970" data-start="3967" />
‚Ä¢ smaller chunks of assessment<br data-end="4003" data-start="4000" />
‚Ä¢ permission to pause and recover<br data-end="4039" data-start="4036" />
‚Ä¢ more than one exam session in a day avoided where possible</p>
<p data-end="4187" data-start="4101">Rest breaks are especially important because they allow recovery time without penalty.</p>
<h3 data-end="4234" data-start="4189"><strong data-end="4234" data-start="4193">3.2 The legal context in simple terms</strong></h3>
<p data-end="4271" data-start="4236">JCQ regulations make it clear that:</p>
<p data-end="4608" data-start="4273">‚Ä¢ adjustments must reflect the learners <strong data-end="4338" data-start="4313">normal way of working</strong><br data-end="4341" data-start="4338" />
‚Ä¢ rest breaks do <strong data-end="4365" data-start="4358">not</strong> require formal application (schools or exam centres can grant them directly)<br data-end="4445" data-start="4442" />
‚Ä¢ adjustments must be evidence based<br data-end="4484" data-start="4481" />
‚Ä¢ health conditions count as a valid need if they affect stamina, processing, memory, pain or concentration<br data-end="4594" data-start="4591" />
(See Source 6)</p>
<h3 data-end="4681" data-start="4610"><strong data-end="4681" data-start="4614">3.3 Who arranges exam adjustments if the child is not in school</strong></h3>
<p data-end="4750" data-start="4683">This is where many parents feel confused.<br data-end="4727" data-start="4724" />
Here is the clear rule:</p>
<h2 data-end="4840" data-start="4752">‚≠ê The <strong data-end="4776" data-start="4761">exam centre</strong> is always responsible for arranging exam access arrangements.</h2>
<p data-end="4913" data-start="4841">Not the tutor.<br data-end="4858" data-start="4855" />
Not the LA team.<br data-end="4877" data-start="4874" />
Not the GP.<br data-end="4891" data-start="4888" />
Not the EOTAS teacher.</p>
<p data-end="5034" data-start="4915">Every child must be registered with an exam centre. That centre handles the JCQ paperwork and approves the adjustments.</p>
<h3 data-end="5088" data-start="5036"><strong data-end="5088" data-start="5040">3.4 Who the exam centre is in each situation</strong></h3>
<p data-end="5246" data-start="5090"><strong data-end="5163" data-start="5090">If your child is still on roll at a school (even if flexi schooling):</strong><br data-end="5166" data-start="5163" />
The <strong data-end="5180" data-start="5170">school</strong> is the exam centre.<br data-end="5203" data-start="5200" />
They must arrange exam access arrangements.</p>
<p data-end="5505" data-start="5248"><strong data-end="5275" data-start="5248">If your child is EOTAS:</strong><br data-end="5278" data-start="5275" />
The <strong data-end="5341" data-start="5282">local authority must attach the child to an exam centre</strong>, usually:<br data-end="5354" data-start="5351" />
‚Ä¢ the previous school<br data-end="5378" data-start="5375" />
‚Ä¢ a PRU<br data-end="5388" data-start="5385" />
‚Ä¢ an EOTAS centre<br data-end="5408" data-start="5405" />
‚Ä¢ another school willing to accept candidates<br data-end="5456" data-start="5453" />
‚Ä¢ or a designated exam centre for medical needs</p>
<p data-end="5662" data-start="5507">The LA is legally responsible for ensuring this is done because the child is still entitled to suitable education under the Education Act.<br data-end="5648" data-start="5645" />
(See Source 5)</p>
<p data-end="5817" data-start="5664"><strong data-end="5699" data-start="5664">If your child is home educated:</strong><br data-end="5702" data-start="5699" />
The parent chooses the exam centre.<br data-end="5740" data-start="5737" />
The chosen private exam centre reviews the evidence and arranges adjustments.</p>
<h3 data-end="5864" data-start="5819"><strong data-end="5864" data-start="5823">3.5 Evidence accepted by exam centres</strong></h3>
<p data-end="6083" data-start="5866">‚Ä¢ GP letter<br data-end="5880" data-start="5877" />
‚Ä¢ paediatric letter<br data-end="5902" data-start="5899" />
‚Ä¢ records of symptoms affecting stamina or concentration<br data-end="5961" data-start="5958" />
‚Ä¢ school or tutor observations<br data-end="5994" data-start="5991" />
‚Ä¢ record of relapses after cognitive activity<br data-end="6042" data-start="6039" />
‚Ä¢ any formal ALN or medical documentation</p>

<h1 data-end="6154" data-start="6090"><strong data-end="6154" data-start="6092">4. School attendance guidance for children with Long Covid</strong></h1>
<p data-end="6327" data-start="6156">Families often receive mixed messages.<br data-end="6197" data-start="6194" />
Some are told to push attendance.<br data-end="6233" data-start="6230" />
Others feel pressured by attendance letters.<br data-end="6280" data-start="6277" />
Some children are incorrectly labelled anxious.</p>
<h3 data-end="6379" data-start="6329"><strong data-end="6379" data-start="6333">4.1 Absence should be medically authorised</strong></h3>
<p data-end="6535" data-start="6381">A child with a recognised health condition should not be marked as unauthorised when they are unwell.<br data-end="6485" data-start="6482" />
Schools must record health evidence appropriately.</p>
<h3 data-end="6588" data-start="6537"><strong data-end="6588" data-start="6541">4.2 Attendance pressure can worsen symptoms</strong></h3>
<p data-end="6738" data-start="6590">The Covid Inquiry confirms that Long Covid can cause long term activity limiting illness.<br data-end="6682" data-start="6679" />
Pushing attendance can trigger relapse.<br data-end="6724" data-start="6721" />
(See Source 4)</p>
<h3 data-end="6798" data-start="6740"><strong data-end="6798" data-start="6744">4.3 What supportive attendance practice looks like</strong></h3>
<p data-end="6994" data-start="6800">‚Ä¢ reduced timetable as a <strong data-end="6839" data-start="6825">short term</strong> support<br data-end="6850" data-start="6847" />
‚Ä¢ planned rest breaks<br data-end="6874" data-start="6871" />
‚Ä¢ flexible starts<br data-end="6894" data-start="6891" />
‚Ä¢ remote learning options<br data-end="6922" data-start="6919" />
‚Ä¢ reduced sensory load<br data-end="6947" data-start="6944" />
‚Ä¢ supportive communication rather than pressure</p>
<h3 data-end="7041" data-start="6996"><strong data-end="7041" data-start="7000">4.4 What attendance teams must not do</strong></h3>
<p data-end="7069" data-start="7043">Attendance teams must not:</p>
<p data-end="7216" data-start="7071">‚Ä¢ threaten fines for illness<br data-end="7102" data-start="7099" />
‚Ä¢ accuse the child of avoidance<br data-end="7136" data-start="7133" />
‚Ä¢ pressure families contrary to <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=medical+evidence+Long+Covid+school+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=767408107096959762" target="_blank">medical evidence</a><br data-end="7187" data-start="7184" />
‚Ä¢ ignore clinical information</p>

<h1 data-end="7343" data-start="7286"><strong data-end="7343" data-start="7288">5. How to evidence medical need for schools and LAs</strong></h1>
<p data-end="7353" data-start="7345">Provide:</p>
<p data-end="7548" data-start="7355">‚Ä¢ GP or paediatric letter<br data-end="7383" data-start="7380" />
‚Ä¢ notes showing daily impact<br data-end="7414" data-start="7411" />
‚Ä¢ evidence of symptom flare after activity<br data-end="7459" data-start="7456" />
‚Ä¢ record of fatigue patterns<br data-end="7490" data-start="7487" />
‚Ä¢ your own <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=write+parent+impact+statement+Long+Covid&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=767408107096959762" target="_blank">parent impact statement</a><br data-end="7527" data-start="7524" />
‚Ä¢ any ALN assessments</p>
<p data-end="7623" data-start="7550">Schools and local authorities must consider this when planning provision.</p>

<h1 data-end="7669" data-start="7630"><strong data-end="7669" data-start="7632">6. Home tuition for medical needs</strong></h1>
<p data-end="7817" data-start="7671">When a child cannot attend school for medical reasons, the local authority has a legal duty under the Education Act to provide suitable education.</p>
<h3 data-end="7867" data-start="7819"><strong data-end="7867" data-start="7823">6.1 What suitable tuition should include</strong></h3>
<p data-end="8022" data-start="7869">‚Ä¢ enough hours to maintain progress<br data-end="7907" data-start="7904" />
‚Ä¢ curriculum aligned teaching<br data-end="7939" data-start="7936" />
‚Ä¢ regular review<br data-end="7958" data-start="7955" />
‚Ä¢ coordination with health advice<br data-end="7994" data-start="7991" />
‚Ä¢ not merely token education</p>
<p data-end="8126" data-start="8024">The Children Commissioner for Wales has raised concerns about inconsistent provision.<br data-end="8112" data-start="8109" />
(See Source 5)</p>
<h3 data-end="8155" data-start="8128"><strong data-end="8155" data-start="8132">6.2 What to request</strong></h3>
<p data-end="8279" data-start="8157">‚Ä¢ clear plan for hours<br data-end="8182" data-start="8179" />
‚Ä¢ timeline for start<br data-end="8205" data-start="8202" />
‚Ä¢ mode of delivery<br data-end="8226" data-start="8223" />
‚Ä¢ link with ALN support<br data-end="8252" data-start="8249" />
‚Ä¢ regular review meetings</p>

<h1 data-end="8372" data-start="8338"><strong data-end="8372" data-start="8340">7. Hospital tuition in Wales</strong></h1>
<p data-end="8472" data-start="8374">Hospital tuition helps children with significant medical needs continue learning during treatment.</p>
<p data-end="8498" data-start="8474">Learning may take place:</p>
<p data-end="8596" data-start="8500">‚Ä¢ on the ward<br data-end="8516" data-start="8513" />
‚Ä¢ in a hospital learning room<br data-end="8548" data-start="8545" />
‚Ä¢ through personalised plans for outpatient care</p>
<p data-end="8670" data-start="8598">Ask your health board for details if your child spends time in hospital.</p>

<h1 data-end="8727" data-start="8677"><strong data-end="8727" data-start="8679">8. Social care support for disabled children</strong></h1>
<p data-end="8823" data-start="8729">Long Covid can meet the definition of disability when symptoms significantly limit daily life.</p>
<p data-end="8916" data-start="8825">Under the Social Services and Wellbeing Wales Act you can request a Child Needs Assessment.</p>
<p data-end="8935" data-start="8918">This can provide:</p>
<p data-end="9088" data-start="8937">‚Ä¢ short breaks<br data-end="8954" data-start="8951" />
‚Ä¢ equipment<br data-end="8968" data-start="8965" />
‚Ä¢ transport support<br data-end="8990" data-start="8987" />
‚Ä¢ care support<br data-end="9007" data-start="9004" />
‚Ä¢ help with routines<br data-end="9030" data-start="9027" />
‚Ä¢ support for siblings<br data-end="9055" data-start="9052" />
‚Ä¢ emotional and practical support</p>
