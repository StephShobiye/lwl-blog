What Long Covid is in children</strong></h1><p data-end="1826" data-start="1495"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Long+Covid&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2694722550628594206" target="_blank">Long Covid</a> describes symptoms that continue for many weeks or months after a Covid infection. It is not a lack of resilience.<br data-end="1898" data-start="1895" />
It is not a behaviour issue.<br data-end="1929" data-start="1926" />
It is a genuine health condition that requires understanding, patience and a clear plan.</p><hr data-end="2022" data-start="2019" /><h1 data-end="2072" data-start="2024"><strong data-end="2072" data-start="2026">2. â€¢ around thirteen percent of young people aged twelve to sixteen<br data-end="2769" data-start="2766" />

(See Source 2)</p><p data-end="3078" data-start="2837">Meta studies suggest that between <strong data-end="2899" data-start="2871">eight and twenty percent</strong> of children experience ongoing symptoms after infection, depending on the definition used.<br data-end="2993" data-start="2990" />
