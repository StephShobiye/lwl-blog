<h2 data-end="392" data-start="309"><strong data-end="392" data-start="312">The Hidden Reality of ALN Parenting in Wales and Why It Deserves Recognition</strong></h2>
<p data-end="500" data-start="394">Schools see a child for around six hours a day.<br data-end="444" data-start="441" />
Parents see every hour before that and every hour after.</p>
<p data-end="777" data-start="502">They see the morning anxiety, the exhaustion after school, the sensory overload, the masking, the meltdowns, the shutdowns, the scripts rehearsed at night, the rituals holding everything together, and the emotional cost that never makes it into a behaviour log or IDP review.</p>
<p data-end="878" data-start="779">This is the invisible workload that shapes the daily reality of thousands of ALN families in Wales.</p>
<p data-end="899" data-start="880">And it is enormous.</p>
<hr data-end="904" data-start="901" />
<h1 data-end="953" data-start="906">⭐ Schools See Behaviour. Parents See the Why.</h1>
<p data-end="1164" data-start="955">Our November deep research confirmed the same pattern found in Welsh Government mental health studies, the Guardian’s recent report on anxiety and bullying, and from the lived experiences of our own community:</p>
<blockquote data-end="1272" data-start="1166">
<p data-end="1272" data-start="1168">Children who cope at school often fall apart at home because they have been holding it together all day.</p>
</blockquote>
<p data-end="1343" data-start="1274">This is not a behaviour problem.<br data-end="1309" data-start="1306" />
It is a stress and energy problem.</p>
<p data-end="1423" data-start="1345">Teachers see a child trying their best.<br data-end="1387" data-start="1384" />
Parents see the cost of that effort.</p>
<hr data-end="1428" data-start="1425" />
