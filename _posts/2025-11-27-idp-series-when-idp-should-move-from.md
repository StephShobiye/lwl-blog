<p data-end="655" data-start="596">A Clear Explanation of the ALN Code for Families in Wales</p>
<p data-end="790" data-start="657">One of the most misunderstood areas of Wales’ ALN system is <strong data-end="748" data-start="717">who should maintain the IDP</strong> — the school or the Local Authority (LA).</p>
<p data-end="916" data-start="792">Families are often left unsure when escalation should happen, why it matters, and how the Code expects decisions to be made.</p>
<p data-end="956" data-start="918">This article explains, in clear terms:</p>
<ul data-end="1211" data-start="958">
<li data-end="995" data-start="958">
<p data-end="995" data-start="960">what the ALN Act and ALN Code say</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1043" data-start="996">
<p data-end="1043" data-start="998">the three legal triggers for LA involvement</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1117" data-start="1044">
<p data-end="1117" data-start="1046">how this applies to EOTAS and Electively Home Educated (EHE) children</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1152" data-start="1118">
<p data-end="1152" data-start="1120">why schools sometimes hesitate</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1211" data-start="1153">
<p data-end="1211" data-start="1155">what families can do when needs exceed school capacity</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="1280" data-start="1213">This is general information about the ALN system, not legal advice.</p>
<hr data-end="1285" data-start="1282" />
<h1 data-end="1351" data-start="1287"><strong data-end="1351" data-start="1289">1. What the ALN Code Says About When the LA Must Take Over</strong></h1>
<p data-end="1401" data-start="1353">The key guidance is in <strong data-end="1400" data-start="1376">ALN Code 23.63–23.67</strong>.</p>
<h3 data-end="1427" data-start="1403"><strong data-end="1425" data-start="1407">ALN Code 23.63</strong></h3>
<p data-end="1497" data-start="1428">If a school is <strong data-end="1473" data-start="1443">not able to secure the ALP</strong>, it must notify the LA.</p>
<h3 data-end="1523" data-start="1499"><strong data-end="1521" data-start="1503">ALN Code 23.64</strong></h3>
<p data-end="1599" data-start="1524">The school must set out the reasons why an LA-maintained IDP may be needed.</p>
<h3 data-end="1625" data-start="1601"><strong data-end="1623" data-start="1605">ALN Code 23.65</strong></h3>
<p data-end="1697" data-start="1626">The LA then considers whether it will take over maintenance of the IDP.</p>
<h3 data-end="1723" data-start="1699"><strong data-end="1721" data-start="1703">ALN Code 23.67</strong></h3>
<p data-end="1761" data-start="1724">The LA must maintain the IDP where:</p>
<ul data-end="1938" data-start="1762">
<li data-end="1801" data-start="1762">
<p data-end="1801" data-start="1764">the child’s needs are more complex,</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1876" data-start="1802">
<p data-end="1876" data-start="1804">provision required goes beyond what a school can reasonably secure, or</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1938" data-start="1877">
<p data-end="1938" data-start="1879">the child is <strong data-end="1901" data-start="1892">EOTAS</strong> (educated otherwise than at school).</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="1991" data-start="1940">These sections form the legal basis for escalation.</p>
<hr data-end="1996" data-start="1993" />
<h1 data-end="2054" data-start="1998"><strong data-end="2054" data-start="2000">2. The Three Clear Triggers for LA-Maintained IDPs</strong></h1>
<p data-end="2142" data-start="2056">Families often find it helpful to think about three broad triggers the Code describes.</p>
<hr data-end="2147" data-start="2144" />
<h2 data-end="2210" data-start="2149">⭐ <strong data-end="2210" data-start="2154">Trigger 1: Needs the School Cannot Reasonably Secure</strong></h2>
<p data-end="2290" data-start="2212">Some needs go beyond what mainstream settings can provide.<br data-end="2273" data-start="2270" />
Examples include:</p>
<ul data-end="2667" data-start="2292">
<li data-end="2348" data-start="2292">
<p data-end="2348" data-start="2294">specialist therapeutic programmes (SALT, OT, Physio)</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2401" data-start="2349">
<p data-end="2401" data-start="2351">multi-agency ALP requiring coordinated oversight</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2438" data-start="2402">
<p data-end="2438" data-start="2404">complex medical or sensory needs</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2482" data-start="2439">
<p data-end="2482" data-start="2441">specialist literacy/numeracy programmes</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2545" data-start="2483">
<p data-end="2545" data-start="2485">support requiring regular review by external professionals</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2606" data-start="2546">
<p data-end="2606" data-start="2548">ALP requiring costs or staffing beyond delegated budgets</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2667" data-start="2607">
<p data-end="2667" data-start="2609">needs requiring outreach tutors or alternative provision</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="2773" data-start="2669">Where needs exceed the school’s capacity, the Code is clear that the LA becomes responsible for the IDP.</p>
<hr data-end="2778" data-start="2775" />
<h2 data-end="2851" data-start="2780">⭐ <strong data-end="2851" data-start="2785">Trigger 2: The Child Is Unable to Attend School Because of ALN</strong></h2>
<p data-end="2884" data-start="2853">This includes children who are:</p>
<ul data-end="3106" data-start="2886">
<li data-end="2927" data-start="2886">
<p data-end="2927" data-start="2888">too anxious or dysregulated to attend</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2964" data-start="2928">
<p data-end="2964" data-start="2930">out of school due to unmet needs</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2985" data-start="2965">
<p data-end="2985" data-start="2967">on reduced hours</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2998" data-start="2986">
<p data-end="2998" data-start="2988">excluded</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3033" data-start="2999">
<p data-end="3033" data-start="3001">recovering from medical issues</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3062" data-start="3034">
<p data-end="3062" data-start="3036">awaiting a new placement</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3106" data-start="3063">
<p data-end="3106" data-start="3065">in a managed move that is breaking down</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="3243" data-start="3108">In these situations, the LA is responsible for securing suitable ALP and education.<br data-end="3194" data-start="3191" />
This usually means the IDP becomes LA-maintained.</p>
<hr data-end="3248" data-start="3245" />
<h2 data-end="3320" data-start="3250">⭐ <strong data-end="3320" data-start="3255">Trigger 3: The Child Is EOTAS (Educated Other Than at School)</strong></h2>
<p data-end="3341" data-start="3322">For EOTAS learners:</p>
<ul data-end="3502" data-start="3343">
<li data-end="3382" data-start="3343">
<p data-end="3382" data-start="3345">the IDP is <strong data-end="3366" data-start="3356">always</strong> LA-maintained</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3440" data-start="3383">
<p data-end="3440" data-start="3385">schools cannot legally maintain IDPs for EOTAS pupils</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3502" data-start="3441">
<p data-end="3502" data-start="3443">the LA is responsible for the ALP and educational package</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="3560" data-start="3504">This is automatic and does not depend on school opinion.</p>
<hr data-end="3565" data-start="3562" />
<h1 data-end="3624" data-start="3567"><strong data-end="3624" data-start="3569">3. Electively Home Educated (EHE) Children and IDPs</strong></h1>
<p data-end="3718" data-start="3626">Where a child is EHE and has ALN, parents may request an assessment.<br data-end="3697" data-start="3694" />
If an IDP is created:</p>
<ul data-end="3967" data-start="3720">
<li data-end="3759" data-start="3720">
<p data-end="3759" data-start="3722">it must always be <strong data-end="3757" data-start="3740">LA-maintained</strong></p>
</li>
<li data-end="3832" data-start="3760">
<p data-end="3832" data-start="3762">the parent delivers the educational element (as required by EHE law)</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3967" data-start="3833">
<p data-end="3967" data-start="3835">specialist ALP (therapy, assessments, sensory plans, equipment, home adaptations) remains the responsibility of NHS or Social Care</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="4112" data-start="3969">Parents may carry out day-to-day therapy activities at home,<br data-end="4032" data-start="4029" />
but programmes must still be <strong data-end="4111" data-start="4061">professionally designed, reviewed and overseen</strong>.</p>
<p data-end="4198" data-start="4114">The LA should not shift specialist NHS or Social Care responsibilities onto parents.</p>
<hr data-end="4203" data-start="4200" />
<h1 data-end="4281" data-start="4205"><strong data-end="4281" data-start="4207">4. Why Escalation Can Be Difficult for Schools (Without Blaming Staff)</strong></h1>
<p data-end="4380" data-start="4283">Families often see hesitation from schools, and this is usually due to system pressures, such as:</p>
<ul data-end="4631" data-start="4382">
<li data-end="4412" data-start="4382">
<p data-end="4412" data-start="4384">uncertainty about the Code</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4432" data-start="4413">
<p data-end="4432" data-start="4415">heavy workloads</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4478" data-start="4433">
<p data-end="4478" data-start="4435">worry about appearing to “fail” the child</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4516" data-start="4479">
<p data-end="4516" data-start="4481">fear of damaging LA relationships</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4565" data-start="4517">
<p data-end="4565" data-start="4519">wanting to try all internal strategies first</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4596" data-start="4566">
<p data-end="4596" data-start="4568">unclear internal processes</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4631" data-start="4597">
<p data-end="4631" data-start="4599">staffing or budget constraints</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="4728" data-start="4633">These pressures are real.<br data-end="4661" data-start="4658" />
But they do not change the requirements of the ALN Act or ALN Code.</p>
<hr data-end="4733" data-start="4730" />
<h1 data-end="4797" data-start="4735"><strong data-end="4797" data-start="4737">5. Families Can Request That the LA Consider Taking Over</strong></h1>
<p data-end="4885" data-start="4799">The Code allows families to ask the LA to consider whether it should maintain the IDP.</p>
<p data-end="4932" data-start="4887">Many families find wording like this helpful:</p>
<blockquote data-end="5126" data-start="4934">
<p data-end="5126" data-start="4936"><strong data-end="5126" data-start="4936">“We would be grateful if the Local Authority could consider maintaining the IDP under ALN Code 23.63–23.67, as aspects of the ALP appear to exceed what the school can reasonably secure.”</strong></p>
</blockquote>
<p data-end="5184" data-start="5128">This is polite, factual and firmly grounded in the Code.</p>
<p data-end="5267" data-start="5186">It avoids conflict while clearly signalling that escalation should be considered.</p>
<hr data-end="5272" data-start="5269" />
<h1 data-end="5339" data-start="5274"><strong data-end="5339" data-start="5276">6. Information Families Often Provide to Support Escalation</strong></h1>
<p data-end="5376" data-start="5341">Families sometimes choose to share:</p>
<ul data-end="5668" data-start="5378">
<li data-end="5402" data-start="5378">
<p data-end="5402" data-start="5380">professional reports</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5428" data-start="5403">
<p data-end="5428" data-start="5405">evidence of unmet ALP</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5463" data-start="5429">
<p data-end="5463" data-start="5431">attendance or anxiety patterns</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5495" data-start="5464">
<p data-end="5495" data-start="5466">sensory/medical information</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5518" data-start="5496">
<p data-end="5518" data-start="5498">dysregulation logs</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5552" data-start="5519">
<p data-end="5552" data-start="5521">details of reduced timetables</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5597" data-start="5553">
<p data-end="5597" data-start="5555">exclusion or managed-move correspondence</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5624" data-start="5598">
<p data-end="5624" data-start="5600">child voice statements</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5668" data-start="5625">
<p data-end="5668" data-start="5627">written examples of what is not working</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="5748" data-start="5670">This gives the LA a clearer picture when deciding who should maintain the IDP.</p>
<hr data-end="5753" data-start="5750" />
<h1 data-end="5798" data-start="5755"><strong data-end="5798" data-start="5757">7. What Happens If the LA Takes Over?</strong></h1>
<p data-end="5844" data-start="5800">When the LA maintains the IDP, it generally:</p>
<ul data-end="6143" data-start="5846">
<li data-end="5875" data-start="5846">
<p data-end="5875" data-start="5848">assigns an LA ALN Officer</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5907" data-start="5876">
<p data-end="5907" data-start="5878">gathers updated information</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5943" data-start="5908">
<p data-end="5943" data-start="5910">consults health and social care</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5972" data-start="5944">
<p data-end="5972" data-start="5946">reviews outcomes and ALP</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6032" data-start="5973">
<p data-end="6032" data-start="5975">considers EOTAS or alternative provision where relevant</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6070" data-start="6033">
<p data-end="6070" data-start="6035">coordinates multi-agency planning</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6102" data-start="6071">
<p data-end="6102" data-start="6073">rewrites or updates the IDP</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6143" data-start="6103">
<p data-end="6143" data-start="6105">sets the schedule for formal reviews</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="6235" data-start="6145">If the child is still on roll, the ALNCo remains involved as the school link professional.</p>
<hr data-end="6240" data-start="6237" />
<h1 data-end="6292" data-start="6242"><strong data-end="6292" data-start="6244">8. If the LA Decides Not to Maintain the IDP</strong></h1>
<p data-end="6368" data-start="6294">If the LA decides the IDP can remain school-based, families may choose to:</p>
<ul data-end="6537" data-start="6370">
<li data-end="6396" data-start="6370">
<p data-end="6396" data-start="6372">request another review</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6430" data-start="6397">
<p data-end="6430" data-start="6399">submit additional information</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6467" data-start="6431">
<p data-end="6467" data-start="6433">ask for multi-agency involvement</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6505" data-start="6468">
<p data-end="6505" data-start="6470">use the LA’s complaints procedure</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6537" data-start="6506">
<p data-end="6537" data-start="6508">use disagreement resolution</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="6600" data-start="6539">The best next step varies depending on the child’s situation.</p>
<hr data-end="6605" data-start="6602" />
<h1 data-end="6623" data-start="6607"><strong data-end="6623" data-start="6609">9. Summary</strong></h1>
<p data-end="6664" data-start="6625">An IDP may need to move to the LA when:</p>
<p data-end="6816" data-start="6666">✔ the school cannot reasonably secure the ALP<br data-end="6714" data-start="6711" />
✔ the child is out of school due to ALN<br data-end="6756" data-start="6753" />
✔ the child is EOTAS<br data-end="6779" data-start="6776" />
✔ the child is EHE and requires ALP</p>
<p data-end="6980" data-start="6818">The ALN Code provides a clear framework for these decisions.<br data-end="6881" data-start="6878" />
Families and schools both play important parts in identifying when escalation should be considered.</p>
