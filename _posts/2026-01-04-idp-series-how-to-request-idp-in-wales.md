<p data-end="549" data-start="498"><strong data-end="549" data-start="498">Part of the Learn Without Limits CIC IDP Series<br /><br /></strong></p>
<p data-end="755" data-start="551">Under the <strong data-end="630" data-start="561">Additional Learning Needs and Education Tribunal (Wales) Act 2018</strong>, every child or young person with Additional Learning Needs (ALN) is entitled to an <strong data-end="752" data-start="715">Individual Development Plan (IDP)</strong>.</p>
<p data-end="1035" data-start="757">But parents often tell us they feel confused about <em data-end="814" data-start="808">when</em> to request an IDP, <em data-end="839" data-start="834">how</em> to do it, and <em data-end="875" data-start="854">which support level</em> they should be asking for. Schools sometimes talk about ‚Äúuniversal provision‚Äù, ‚ÄúIEPs‚Äù or ‚ÄúIHPs‚Äù, and it isn‚Äôt always obvious how these fit into the ALN system.</p>
<p data-end="1277" data-start="1037">This guide sets out the legal position clearly ‚Äî including the critical point that <strong data-end="1181" data-start="1120">support must be based on educational needs, NOT diagnosis</strong> ‚Äî and includes a legally correct <strong data-end="1237" data-start="1215">IDP request letter</strong> for both schools and local authorities.</p>
<hr data-end="1282" data-start="1279" />
<h1 data-end="1358" data-start="1284"><strong data-end="1358" data-start="1286">1. Support Is Based on Needs, Not Diagnosis (The Law Is Unambiguous)</strong></h1>
<p data-end="1391" data-start="1360">Schools sometimes tell parents:</p>
<ul data-end="1562" data-start="1393">
<li data-end="1441" data-start="1393">
<p data-end="1441" data-start="1395">‚ÄúWe can‚Äôt give support without a diagnosis.‚Äù</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1511" data-start="1442">
<p data-end="1511" data-start="1444">‚ÄúWe are waiting for CAMHS/paediatrics before we can open an IDP.‚Äù</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1562" data-start="1512">
<p data-end="1562" data-start="1514">‚ÄúWe don‚Äôt have evidence until you get a report.‚Äù</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="1585" data-start="1564"><strong data-end="1585" data-start="1564">This is unlawful.</strong></p>
<h3 data-end="1619" data-start="1587"><strong data-end="1617" data-start="1591">Exact Legal Quotation:</strong></h3>
<p data-end="1655" data-start="1620">The ALN Code for Wales 2021 states:</p>
<blockquote data-end="1885" data-start="1657">
<p data-end="1885" data-start="1659"><strong data-end="1840" data-start="1659">‚ÄúA person has ALN if he or she has a learning difficulty or disability arising from a <em data-end="1785" data-start="1747">barrier to learning or participation</em>, <em data-end="1836" data-start="1787">regardless of whether a diagnosis has been made</em>.‚Äù</strong><br data-end="1843" data-start="1840" />
‚Äî <em data-end="1885" data-start="1845">ALN Code for Wales 2021, Paragraph 2.4</em></p>
</blockquote>
<p data-end="1952" data-start="1887">This is one of the most important legal protections parents have.</p>
<h3 data-end="1991" data-start="1954"><strong data-end="1989" data-start="1958">What this means in practice</strong></h3>
<ul data-end="2303" data-start="1992">
<li data-end="2040" data-start="1992">
<p data-end="2040" data-start="1994">A diagnosis is <strong data-end="2025" data-start="2009">not required</strong> for support.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2106" data-start="2041">
<p data-end="2106" data-start="2043">Waiting lists <strong data-end="2067" data-start="2057">cannot</strong> be used as a reason to delay an IDP.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2256" data-start="2107">
<p data-end="2256" data-start="2109">Schools must base decisions on <strong data-end="2253" data-start="2140">needs observed in the classroom, transitions, regulation, communication, sensory access, or learning progress</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2303" data-start="2257">
<p data-end="2303" data-start="2259">Parents‚Äô evidence carries full legal weight.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="2362" data-start="2305"><strong data-end="2362" data-start="2305">ALN is triggered by educational need ‚Äî not paperwork.</strong></p>
<hr data-end="2367" data-start="2364" />
<h1 data-end="2424" data-start="2369"><strong data-end="2424" data-start="2371">2. Universal Provision, IEPs, IHPs ‚Äî What‚Äôs What?</strong></h1>
<h2 data-end="2454" data-start="2426"><strong data-end="2452" data-start="2429">Universal Provision</strong></h2>
<p data-end="2534" data-start="2455">Support that <strong data-end="2497" data-start="2468">every school must provide</strong> from their own resources, including:</p>
<ul data-end="2733" data-start="2536">
<li data-end="2563" data-start="2536">
<p data-end="2563" data-start="2538">Differentiated teaching</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2610" data-start="2564">
<p data-end="2610" data-start="2566">Reasonable adjustments (Equality Act 2010)</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2647" data-start="2611">
<p data-end="2647" data-start="2613">Sensory/communication strategies</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2664" data-start="2648">
<p data-end="2664" data-start="2650">EBSA support</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2700" data-start="2665">
<p data-end="2700" data-start="2667">Behaviour/regulation approaches</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2733" data-start="2701">
<p data-end="2733" data-start="2703">Short-term intervention groups</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="2887" data-start="2735">Universal Provision <strong data-end="2769" data-start="2755">is not ALN</strong>. If needs persist despite it ‚Äî or if the needs are significant at the outset ‚Äî the child is likely to require an IDP.</p>
<hr data-end="2892" data-start="2889" />
<h2 data-end="2934" data-start="2894"><strong data-end="2932" data-start="2897">IEP ‚Äì Individual Education Plan</strong></h2>
<p data-end="3049" data-start="2935">Not a statutory document under the ALN Act.<br data-end="2981" data-start="2978" />
Schools may use them internally, but they <strong data-end="3033" data-start="3023">cannot</strong> replace an IDP.</p>
<hr data-end="3054" data-start="3051" />
<h2 data-end="3097" data-start="3056"><strong data-end="3095" data-start="3059">IHP ‚Äì Individual Healthcare Plan</strong></h2>
<p data-end="3268" data-start="3098">For medical needs only.<br data-end="3124" data-start="3121" />
Not educational.<br data-end="3143" data-start="3140" />
An IHP <strong data-end="3160" data-start="3150">cannot</strong> substitute for an IDP when the child also has learning, sensory, communication, or regulation difficulties.</p>
<hr data-end="3273" data-start="3270" />
<h1 data-end="3344" data-start="3275"><strong data-end="3344" data-start="3277">3. Home‚ÄìSchool Communication Book: A Critical Piece of Evidence</strong></h1>
<p data-end="3499" data-start="3346">A <strong data-end="3386" data-start="3348">daily or weekly communication book</strong> is one of the most effective ways to gather evidence for ALN decision-making. It is particularly important when:</p>
<ul data-end="3743" data-start="3501">
<li data-end="3546" data-start="3501">
<p data-end="3546" data-start="3503">Behaviour differs between home and school</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3574" data-start="3547">
<p data-end="3574" data-start="3549">A child masks at school</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3620" data-start="3575">
<p data-end="3620" data-start="3577">Regulation challenges emerge after school</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3680" data-start="3621">
<p data-end="3680" data-start="3623">Fatigue, meltdowns, or shutdowns occur later in the day</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3743" data-start="3681">
<p data-end="3743" data-start="3683">Homework, sleep, eating, or anxiety deteriorate after school</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="3830" data-start="3745">A communication book helps identify <strong data-end="3793" data-start="3781">patterns</strong>, <strong data-end="3807" data-start="3795">triggers</strong>, and <strong data-end="3829" data-start="3813">unseen needs</strong>.</p>
<h3 data-end="3859" data-start="3832">Use the book to record:</h3>
<ul data-end="4054" data-start="3860">
<li data-end="3890" data-start="3860">
<p data-end="3890" data-start="3862">Dysregulation after school</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3912" data-start="3891">
<p data-end="3912" data-start="3893">Emotional crashes</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3948" data-start="3913">
<p data-end="3948" data-start="3915">Behaviour linked to transitions</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3978" data-start="3949">
<p data-end="3978" data-start="3951">Sleep and eating patterns</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4003" data-start="3979">
<p data-end="4003" data-start="3981">Recovery time needed</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4054" data-start="4004">
<p data-end="4054" data-start="4006">Episodes pointing to sensory overload or masking</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="4254" data-start="4056">Schools must consider <strong data-end="4085" data-start="4078">all</strong> evidence, including that provided by parents. The ALN Code explicitly states that a child's needs must be understood <strong data-end="4226" data-start="4203">across environments</strong>, not just in the classroom.</p>

<p data-end="4315" data-start="4264"><b><a href="https://learnwithoutlimitscic.blogspot.com/2025/11/why-so-many-disabled-children-mask-at.html">https://learnwithoutlimitscic.blogspot.com/2025/11/why-so-many-disabled-children-mask-at.html</a></b></p>
<hr data-end="4386" data-start="4383" />
<h1 data-end="4420" data-start="4388"><strong data-end="4420" data-start="4390">4. Who Can Request an IDP?</strong></h1>
<p data-end="4489" data-start="4422">Under <strong data-end="4457" data-start="4428">Section 21 of the ALN Act</strong>, an IDP request can be made by:</p>
<ul data-end="4626" data-start="4491">
<li data-end="4503" data-start="4491">
<p data-end="4503" data-start="4493">A parent</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4532" data-start="4504">
<p data-end="4532" data-start="4506">A young person (over 16)</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4545" data-start="4533">
<p data-end="4545" data-start="4535">A school</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4567" data-start="4546">
<p data-end="4567" data-start="4548">A local authority</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4626" data-start="4568">
<p data-end="4626" data-start="4570">A health body (e.g., speech therapy, CAMHS, paediatrics)</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="4691" data-start="4628">The request must be considered <strong data-end="4690" data-start="4659">within statutory timeframes</strong>.</p>
<hr data-end="4696" data-start="4693" />
<h1 data-end="4757" data-start="4698"><strong data-end="4757" data-start="4700">5. When to Apply to the School vs the Local Authority</strong></h1>
<h2 data-end="4806" data-start="4759"><strong data-end="4804" data-start="4762">A. If the child is ON ROLL at a school</strong></h2>
<p data-end="4857" data-start="4807">‚û°Ô∏è Your request must go to the <strong data-end="4848" data-start="4838">school</strong> first.</p>
<p data-end="4941" data-start="4859">The school ALNCo decides whether the child has ALN and whether an IDP is required.</p>
<p data-end="5022" data-start="4943">If the school refuses or delays, parents then apply to the <strong data-end="5021" data-start="5002">local authority</strong>.</p>
<hr data-end="5027" data-start="5024" />
<h2 data-end="5090" data-start="5029"><strong data-end="5090" data-start="5032">B. You can apply DIRECTLY to the Local Authority when:</strong></h2>
<h3 data-end="5148" data-start="5092"><strong data-end="5146" data-start="5096">1. The child is Electively Home Educated (EHE)</strong></h3>
<p data-end="5185" data-start="5149">LA is responsible for ALN decisions.</p>
<h3 data-end="5234" data-start="5187"><strong data-end="5232" data-start="5191">2. The child is not currently on roll</strong></h3>
<p data-end="5247" data-start="5235">For example:</p>
<ul data-end="5343" data-start="5248">
<li data-end="5271" data-start="5248">
<p data-end="5271" data-start="5250">Placement breakdown</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5288" data-start="5272">
<p data-end="5288" data-start="5274">Deregistered</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5313" data-start="5289">
<p data-end="5313" data-start="5291">Permanently excluded</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5343" data-start="5314">
<p data-end="5343" data-start="5316">Transition between settings</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3 data-end="5386" data-start="5345"><strong data-end="5384" data-start="5349">3. The learner is in FE college</strong></h3>
<p data-end="5424" data-start="5387">LAs handle IDPs for post-16 learners.</p>
<h3 data-end="5503" data-start="5426"><strong data-end="5501" data-start="5430">4. The school has refused, delayed, or stated they cannot meet need</strong></h3>
<p data-end="5563" data-start="5504">Parents may go directly to the LA for a statutory decision.</p>
<hr data-end="5568" data-start="5565" />
<h1 data-end="5612" data-start="5570"><strong data-end="5612" data-start="5572">6. What to Include in an IDP Request</strong></h1>
<p data-end="5720" data-start="5614">You do not need a diagnosis.<br data-end="5645" data-start="5642" />
You do not need professional reports.<br data-end="5685" data-start="5682" />
You do not need proof beyond doubt.</p>
<p data-end="5799" data-start="5722">Under the Act, you only need to demonstrate that your child <strong data-end="5789" data-start="5782">may</strong> have ALN.</p>
<p data-end="5809" data-start="5801">Include:</p>
<ul data-end="5979" data-start="5811">
<li data-end="5839" data-start="5811">
<p data-end="5839" data-start="5813">Examples of difficulties</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5873" data-start="5840">
<p data-end="5873" data-start="5842">Universal Provision attempted</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5945" data-start="5874">
<p data-end="5945" data-start="5876">Home evidence (communication book, dysregulation patterns, masking)</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5979" data-start="5946">
<p data-end="5979" data-start="5948">Any reports you have (optional)</p>
</li>
</ul>
<hr data-end="5984" data-start="5981" />
<h1 data-end="6016" data-start="5986"><strong data-end="6016" data-start="5988">7. IDP Request Templates</strong></h1>
