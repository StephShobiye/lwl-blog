<h1 data-end="1122" data-start="1101"><strong data-end="1122" data-start="1103">1. Introduction</strong></h1>
<p data-end="1358" data-start="1124">Flexi schooling is one of the most misunderstood areas of education in Wales.<br data-end="1204" data-start="1201" />
Parents are told flexi schooling is illegal. Schools confuse it with <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+reduced+timetables+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2278906810955066571" target="_blank">reduced timetables</a>.<br data-end="1295" data-start="1292" />
Local authority officers sometimes misunderstand it completely.</p>
<p data-end="1466" data-start="1360">Yet flexi schooling is <strong data-end="1392" data-start="1383">legal</strong>, <strong data-end="1414" data-start="1394">well established</strong>, and used by many families across Wales every year.</p>
<p data-end="1676" data-start="1468">Learn Without Limits CIC has supported parents with flexi schooling for more than a decade.<br data-end="1562" data-start="1559" />
This guide sets the record straight with clear explanations, Welsh legal references, scripts and letter templates.</p>
<hr data-end="1681" data-start="1678" />
<h1 data-end="1724" data-start="1683"><strong data-end="1724" data-start="1685">2. What flexi schooling actually is</strong></h1>
<p data-end="1766" data-start="1726">Flexi schooling is an arrangement where:</p>
<p data-end="2054" data-start="1768">✔ the parent chooses to educate the child part time at home<br data-end="1830" data-start="1827" />
✔ the child remains registered at the school<br data-end="1877" data-start="1874" />
✔ the Head Teacher agrees to the arrangement<br data-end="1924" data-start="1921" />
✔ the school provides part of the education<br data-end="1970" data-start="1967" />
✔ the parent provides the rest<br data-end="2003" data-start="2000" />
✔ attendance for home learning days is authorised</p>
<p data-end="2183" data-start="2056">Flexi schooling is a lawful and flexible educational choice.<br data-end="2119" data-start="2116" />
It is not deregistration, not EOTAS and not a reduced timetable.</p>
<hr data-end="2188" data-start="2185" />
<h1 data-end="2226" data-start="2190"><strong data-end="2226" data-start="2192">3. What flexi schooling is not</strong></h1>
<p data-end="2296" data-start="2228">Understanding what flexi schooling is <em data-end="2271" data-start="2266">not</em> helps prevent confusion.</p>
<h3 data-end="2331" data-start="2298">3.1 Not a reduced timetable</h3>
<p data-end="2355" data-start="2332">A reduced timetable is:</p>
<p data-end="2495" data-start="2357">• initiated by the school<br data-end="2385" data-start="2382" />
• temporary only<br data-end="2404" data-start="2401" />
• used for short term support<br data-end="2436" data-start="2433" />
• reviewed every few weeks<br data-end="2465" data-start="2462" />
• intended for reintegration</p>
<p data-end="2625" data-start="2497">Reduced timetables must <strong data-end="2530" data-start="2521">never</strong> be used long term.<br data-end="2552" data-start="2549" />
And they must <strong data-end="2575" data-start="2566">never</strong> be presented as flexi schooling.<br data-end="2611" data-start="2608" />
(See Source 4)</p>
<hr data-end="2630" data-start="2627" />
<h3 data-end="2651" data-start="2632">3.2 Not EOTAS</h3>
<p data-end="2826" data-start="2652">EOTAS is education arranged by the local authority for a child who <strong data-end="2729" data-start="2719">cannot</strong> attend school.<br data-end="2747" data-start="2744" />
Flexi schooling is a parental choice for children who <strong data-end="2808" data-start="2801">can</strong> attend part time.</p>
<hr data-end="2831" data-start="2828" />
<h3 data-end="2883" data-start="2833">3.3 Not school directed part time attendance</h3>
<p data-end="3000" data-start="2884">A school cannot direct a parent to educate their child at home.<br data-end="2950" data-start="2947" />
This is <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+off-rolling+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2278906810955066571" target="_blank">off-rolling</a> and unlawful.<br data-end="2986" data-start="2983" />
(See Source 5)</p>
<hr data-end="3005" data-start="3002" />
<h3 data-end="3035" data-start="3007">3.4 Not home education</h3>
<p data-end="3067" data-start="3036">With flexi schooling the child:</p>
<p data-end="3180" data-start="3069">• stays on roll<br data-end="3087" data-start="3084" />
• keeps their school place<br data-end="3116" data-start="3113" />
• keeps ALN support<br data-end="3138" data-start="3135" />
• remains within the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=safeguarding+system+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2278906810955066571" target="_blank">safeguarding system</a></p>
<p data-end="3234" data-start="3182">This is very different from <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+elective+home+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2278906810955066571" target="_blank">elective home education</a>.</p>
<hr data-end="3239" data-start="3236" />
<h1 data-end="3278" data-start="3241"><strong data-end="3278" data-start="3243">4. The legal framework in Wales</strong></h1>
<h3 data-end="3319" data-start="3280">4.1 Education Act 1996, Section 7</h3>
<p data-end="3446" data-start="3320">Parents must provide education “at school or otherwise.”<br data-end="3379" data-start="3376" />
Flexi schooling sits within the “otherwise” category and is lawful.</p>
<hr data-end="3451" data-start="3448" />
<h3 data-end="3519" data-start="3453">4.2 Welsh Government Elective Home Education Guidance (2017)</h3>
<p data-end="3534" data-start="3520">This guidance:</p>
<p data-end="3705" data-start="3536">✔ confirms flexi schooling is legal<br data-end="3574" data-start="3571" />
✔ states it can only happen with Head Teacher agreement<br data-end="3632" data-start="3629" />
✔ explains parents take responsibility for home learning<br data-end="3691" data-start="3688" />
(See Source 1)</p>
<hr data-end="3710" data-start="3707" />
<h3 data-end="3765" data-start="3712">4.3 Welsh Government Attendance Guidance (2010)</h3>
<p data-end="3886" data-start="3766">This guidance states that part time attendance arrangements must be authorised and coded appropriately.<br data-end="3872" data-start="3869" />
(See Source 2)</p>
<hr data-end="3891" data-start="3888" />
<h3 data-end="3931" data-start="3893">4.4 Keeping Learners Safe (2023)</h3>
<p data-end="4027" data-start="3932">Schools retain safeguarding responsibility for pupils on roll, including flexi schooled pupils.</p>
<hr data-end="4032" data-start="4029" />
<h3 data-end="4051" data-start="4034">4.5 ALN law</h3>
<p data-end="4177" data-start="4052">Flexi schooling does not remove schools duties under the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALNET+Act+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2278906810955066571" target="_blank">ALNET Act</a>.<br data-end="4122" data-start="4119" />
IDP provision must still be delivered.<br data-end="4163" data-start="4160" />
(See Source 3)</p>
<hr data-end="4182" data-start="4179" />
<h1 data-end="4249" data-start="4184">⭐ <strong data-end="4249" data-start="4188">5. Crucial clarity: A school cannot offer flexi schooling</strong></h1>
<p data-end="4353" data-start="4251">Only <strong data-end="4267" data-start="4256">parents</strong> can initiate flexi schooling.<br data-end="4300" data-start="4297" />
A school may agree or refuse.<br data-end="4332" data-start="4329" />
But a school may not:</p>
<p data-end="4576" data-start="4355">✘ suggest flexi schooling to reduce attendance<br data-end="4404" data-start="4401" />
✘ offer it as an alternative to ALN provision<br data-end="4452" data-start="4449" />
✘ encourage parents to educate part time at home<br data-end="4503" data-start="4500" />
✘ pressure families into agreeing<br data-end="4539" data-start="4536" />
✘ use it to avoid providing support</p>
<p data-end="4699" data-start="4578">If a school suggests flexi schooling, this is a safeguarding red flag and may be classed as off-rolling.<br data-end="4685" data-start="4682" />
(See Source 5)</p>

<h1 data-end="5549" data-start="5505"><strong data-end="5549" data-start="5507">7. When flexi schooling is appropriate</strong></h1>
<h3 data-end="5589" data-start="5551">Case Study 1: Long Covid fatigue</h3>
<p data-end="5746" data-start="5590">Child suffers severe fatigue and relapses after full days.<br data-end="5651" data-start="5648" />
Morning school attendance plus home learning afternoons reduces crashes and maintains progress.</p>
<h3 data-end="5793" data-start="5748">Case Study 2: Autistic sensory overload</h3>
<p data-end="5925" data-start="5794">Child becomes distressed after lunch due to sensory overload.<br data-end="5858" data-start="5855" />
Afternoon home learning protects wellbeing and supports engagement.</p>
<h3 data-end="5967" data-start="5927">Case Study 3: PDA demand avoidance</h3>
<p data-end="6080" data-start="5968">Full school days trigger shutdowns.<br data-end="6006" data-start="6003" />
Flexi schooling reduces demand pressure and increases emotional stability.</p>
<h3 data-end="6127" data-start="6082">Case Study 4: ADHD child needing pacing</h3>
<p data-end="6222" data-start="6128">High focus mornings in school plus structured afternoons at home support learning consistency.</p>
<hr data-end="6227" data-start="6224" />
<h1 data-end="6281" data-start="6229"><strong data-end="6281" data-start="6231">8. Flexi schooling does not have to be forever</strong></h1>
<p data-end="6350" data-start="6283">Flexi schooling is a tool, not a lifelong identity.<br data-end="6337" data-start="6334" />
Families may:</p>
<p data-end="6571" data-start="6352">• flexi school in primary but choose full time in secondary<br data-end="6414" data-start="6411" />
• flexi school during a health recovery<br data-end="6456" data-start="6453" />
• use flexi schooling while waiting for ALN assessments<br data-end="6514" data-start="6511" />
• return to full time school when the child feels ready</p>
<p data-end="6698" data-start="6573">Because the child remains <strong data-end="6610" data-start="6599">on roll</strong>, returning to full time schooling is straightforward and does not require re-admission.</p>
<p data-end="6751" data-start="6700">Flexibility is the entire point of flexi schooling.</p>
<hr data-end="6756" data-start="6753" />
<h1 data-end="6806" data-start="6758"><strong data-end="6806" data-start="6760">9. When flexi schooling is not appropriate</strong></h1>
<h3 data-end="6864" data-start="6808">Case Study 1: Child unable to attend school at all</h3>
<p data-end="6929" data-start="6865">This is not flexi schooling.<br data-end="6896" data-start="6893" />
This is medical tuition or EOTAS.</p>
<h3 data-end="6984" data-start="6931">Case Study 2: School initiating flexi schooling</h3>
<p data-end="7028" data-start="6985">This is unlawful and a form of off-rolling.</p>
<h3 data-end="7091" data-start="7030">Case Study 3: Unsafe or traumatising school environment</h3>
<p data-end="7204" data-start="7092">Flexi schooling hides safeguarding issues.<br data-end="7137" data-start="7134" />
The correct route is an urgent IDP review or alternative placement.</p>
<h3 data-end="7260" data-start="7206">Case Study 4: Year 11 without structured support</h3>
<p data-end="7336" data-start="7261">Without a strong home framework, flexi schooling can reduce exam readiness.</p>
<hr data-end="7341" data-start="7338" />
<h1 data-end="7394" data-start="7343"><strong data-end="7394" data-start="7345">10. How flexi schooling benefits ALN children</strong></h1>
<p data-end="7591" data-start="7396">• supports pacing<br data-end="7416" data-start="7413" />
• reduces burnout<br data-end="7436" data-start="7433" />
• protects emotional wellbeing<br data-end="7469" data-start="7466" />
• enhances attendance quality<br data-end="7501" data-start="7498" />
• allows therapy routines<br data-end="7529" data-start="7526" />
• improves learning consistency<br data-end="7563" data-start="7560" />
• reduces sensory overload</p>
<hr data-end="7596" data-start="7593" />
<h1 data-end="7638" data-start="7598"><strong data-end="7638" data-start="7600">11. How to request flexi schooling</strong></h1>
<p data-end="7688" data-start="7640">To persuade a Head Teacher, your request should:</p>
<p data-end="7917" data-start="7690">✔ explain how flexi schooling helps your child access education<br data-end="7756" data-start="7753" />
✔ link to ALN needs or health needs<br data-end="7794" data-start="7791" />
✔ provide structure and reassurance<br data-end="7832" data-start="7829" />
✔ clarify safeguarding arrangement<br data-end="7869" data-start="7866" />
✔ demonstrate the child will continue learning</p>
<p data-end="7938" data-start="7919">What does not work:</p>
<p data-end="8030" data-start="7940">✘ vague requests<br data-end="7959" data-start="7956" />
✘ confrontational approaches<br data-end="7990" data-start="7987" />
✘ framing flexi schooling as avoidance</p>
<p data-end="8104" data-start="8032">Schools respond best to structured, confident, evidence backed requests.</p>
<hr data-end="8109" data-start="8106" />
<h1 data-end="8147" data-start="8111"><strong data-end="8147" data-start="8113">12. How schools should respond</strong></h1>
<p data-end="8168" data-start="8149">Head Teachers must:</p>
<p data-end="8364" data-start="8170">✔ consider the request reasonably<br data-end="8206" data-start="8203" />
✔ give a written response<br data-end="8234" data-start="8231" />
✔ decide based on the child’s needs<br data-end="8272" data-start="8269" />
✔ provide a clear attendance plan<br data-end="8308" data-start="8305" />
✔ confirm review dates<br data-end="8333" data-start="8330" />
✔ update the IDP if necessary</p>
<p data-end="8380" data-start="8366">They must not:</p>
<p data-end="8476" data-start="8382">✘ pressure a parent to deregister<br data-end="8418" data-start="8415" />
✘ misstate the law<br data-end="8439" data-start="8436" />
✘ refuse for blanket policy reasons</p>
<hr data-end="8481" data-start="8478" />
<h1 data-end="8510" data-start="8483"><strong data-end="8510" data-start="8485">13. Attendance coding</strong></h1>
<h3 data-end="8547" data-start="8512">✔ Code C – authorised absence</h3>
<p data-end="8599" data-start="8548">Used for home learning portions of flexi schooling.</p>
<h3 data-end="8645" data-start="8601">✔ Code B – approved off-site education</h3>
<p data-end="8690" data-start="8646">Sometimes used for structured home learning.</p>
<h3 data-end="8729" data-start="8692">✘ Code O – unauthorised absence</h3>
<p data-end="8764" data-start="8730">Must not be used.<br data-end="8750" data-start="8747" />
(See Source 2)</p>
<p data-end="8781" data-start="8766">Why it matters:</p>
<p data-end="8891" data-start="8783">• prevents false attendance concerns<br data-end="8822" data-start="8819" />
• avoids triggering LA involvement<br data-end="8859" data-start="8856" />
• ensures safeguarding clarity</p>
<hr data-end="8896" data-start="8893" />
<h1 data-end="8938" data-start="8898"><strong data-end="8938" data-start="8900">14. Flexi schooling and ALN / IDPs</strong></h1>
<p data-end="9001" data-start="8940">Flexi schooling does not reduce any ALN duty.<br data-end="8988" data-start="8985" />
Schools must:</p>
<p data-end="9126" data-start="9003">✔ continue IDP provision<br data-end="9030" data-start="9027" />
✔ evidence progress<br data-end="9052" data-start="9049" />
✔ review regularly<br data-end="9073" data-start="9070" />
✔ include flexi schooling in the IDP if appropriate</p>
<p data-end="9167" data-start="9128">LA oversight remains for complex needs.</p>

Flexi schooling is lawful under <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Section+7+Education+Act&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2278906810955066571" target="_blank">Section 7 of the Education Act</a>, which allows parents to provide education at school or otherwise.</p>
<p data-end="10258" data-start="10073">This request is based on my child’s individual needs. Flexi schooling will support their access to education by ensuring that they can learn safely, consistently and without distress.</p>
<p data-end="10364" data-start="10265">I propose the following pattern of attendance:<br data-end="10314" data-start="10311" />
• Days in school:<br data-end="10336" data-start="10333" />
• Days learning at home:</p>
<p data-end="10475" data-start="10371">I will provide structured home learning, focused on literacy, numeracy and the subjects you recommend.</p>
<p data-end="10595" data-start="10482">I am happy to meet to discuss how we can make this work smoothly, including attendance coding and safeguarding.</p>
<p data-end="10654" data-start="10602">Please could you confirm your decision in writing.</p>
<p data-end="10684" data-start="10661">Kind regards<br data-end="10676" data-start="10673" />
[Name]</p>
</blockquote>
