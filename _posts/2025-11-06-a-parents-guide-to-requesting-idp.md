## 💬 Why Requesting a Review Matters

An Individual Development Plan (IDP) should evolve with your child.  
If their needs change, progress stalls, or current support isn’t working, you have the right to ask for a review.  
It’s not confrontation — it’s collaboration.

> “An IDP is a living document. If your child’s situation changes, so should their plan.”

---

## 1️⃣ When Can You Ask for a Review?

You can request an IDP review at any time, but especially if:

- Your child’s needs have changed or new assessments have been done  
- Support strategies aren’t helping  
- Attendance or wellbeing are declining  
- There’s been a change in staff, setting, or provision  

The ALN Code for Wales 2021 encourages schools and local authorities to review plans regularly and respond quickly to parent concerns.

---

## 2️⃣ How to Ask for a Review (Step-by-Step)

### Put it in writing  
Use clear, calm wording. The Learn Without Limits App includes a free printable Request for IDP Review Letter you can personalise.

### Keep a record  
Save a copy of your request, along with any recent reports, meeting notes, or email threads.

### Be specific  
Explain what has changed — e.g. new diagnosis, anxiety, missed targets, or recent exclusion/refusal patterns.

### Request a meeting  
Ask for a convenient date to discuss progress, new strategies, or outside-agency input.

---

## 3️⃣ What Happens Next

Once you’ve made a written request, the school or local authority must consider it.  
If they agree that the plan needs updating, a review meeting will be arranged.  

You can invite other professionals (such as a speech therapist, counsellor, or advocate).

If they decline to review the IDP, they must give you reasons in writing — and you have the right to appeal or request mediation.

---

## 4️⃣ Tips for a Calm, Productive Meeting

- Bring notes — use the Tier 2 Journal in our app to record examples of what’s working or not  
- Focus on the goal: what helps your child learn and feel safe  
- Keep tone collaborative, not defensive — you’re the expert on your child  
- After the meeting, ask for updated copies of the IDP and meeting notes

> “Asking for a review isn’t a complaint — it’s a commitment to getting support right.”
