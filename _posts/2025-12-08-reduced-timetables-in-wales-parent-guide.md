<p data-end="567" data-start="283">When a child is struggling to manage a full school day because of anxiety, medical needs, sensory overwhelm, chronic illness or unmet ALN needs, schools sometimes suggest a reduced timetable. This is sometimes described as a phased return and is meant to be short term and supportive.</p>
<p data-end="794" data-start="569">But parents are rarely told the legal rules or what good practice looks like.<br data-end="649" data-start="646" />
This guide explains your rights in Wales and offers practical steps to protect your child and ensure they continue to receive suitable education.</p>

<h1 data-end="1111" data-start="1079">‚≠ê What Is a Reduced Timetable?</h1>
<p data-end="1238" data-start="1113">A reduced timetable means your child attends school for fewer hours or fewer days than expected for their age. This might be:</p>
<ul data-end="1360" data-start="1240">
<li data-end="1257" data-start="1240">
<p data-end="1257" data-start="1242">mornings only</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1292" data-start="1258">
<p data-end="1292" data-start="1260">arriving late or leaving early</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1319" data-start="1293">
<p data-end="1319" data-start="1295">specific subjects only</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1360" data-start="1320">
<p data-end="1360" data-start="1322">attending two or three days per week</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="1457" data-start="1362">A reduced timetable <strong data-end="1401" data-start="1382">does not remove</strong> the school‚Äôs legal responsibility to provide education.</p>
<hr data-end="1462" data-start="1459" />
<h1 data-end="1480" data-start="1464">‚≠ê Is It Legal?</h1>
<p data-end="1506" data-start="1482">Yes ‚Äî but <strong data-end="1500" data-start="1492">only</strong> when:</p>
<ol data-end="1716" data-start="1508">
<li data-end="1534" data-start="1508">
<p data-end="1534" data-start="1511">The parent has agreed</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1560" data-start="1535">
<p data-end="1560" data-start="1538">It is <strong data-end="1558" data-start="1544">short term</strong></p>
</li>
<li data-end="1589" data-start="1561">
<p data-end="1589" data-start="1564">There is a written plan</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1630" data-start="1590">
<p data-end="1630" data-start="1593">It is in the child‚Äôs best interests</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1668" data-start="1631">
<p data-end="1668" data-start="1634">The school reviews it frequently</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1716" data-start="1669">
<p data-end="1716" data-start="1672">It is never used as a disciplinary measure</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p data-end="1824" data-start="1718">If these rules are not followed, the timetable may amount to an <strong data-end="1804" data-start="1782">informal exclusion</strong>, which is unlawful.</p>

<h1 data-end="2454" data-start="2404">‚≠ê When a Reduced Timetable <em data-end="2454" data-start="2433">Is Most Appropriate</em></h1>
<h2 data-end="2501" data-start="2456">‚≠ê 1. Medical Recovery or Chronic Illness</h2>
<p data-end="2514" data-start="2502">For example:</p>
<ul data-end="2619" data-start="2516">
<li data-end="2530" data-start="2516">
<p data-end="2530" data-start="2518">Long Covid</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2552" data-start="2531">
<p data-end="2552" data-start="2533">fatigue disorders</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2576" data-start="2553">
<p data-end="2576" data-start="2555">post viral symptoms</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2602" data-start="2577">
<p data-end="2602" data-start="2579">post surgery recovery</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2619" data-start="2603">
<p data-end="2619" data-start="2605">chronic pain</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="2695" data-start="2621">A reduced timetable may help build stamina without overwhelming the child.</p>
<h2 data-end="2752" data-start="2697">‚≠ê 2. Anxiety or Emotionally Based School Avoidance</h2>
<p data-end="2831" data-start="2753">When the child is willing to attempt small steps but cannot manage a full day.</p>
<p data-end="2857" data-start="2833">It should sit alongside:</p>
<ul data-end="2953" data-start="2859">
<li data-end="2874" data-start="2859">
<p data-end="2874" data-start="2861">counselling</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2897" data-start="2875">
<p data-end="2897" data-start="2877">graduated exposure</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2925" data-start="2898">
<p data-end="2925" data-start="2900">trauma informed support</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2953" data-start="2926">
<p data-end="2953" data-start="2928">CAMHS or GP involvement</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h2 data-end="3001" data-start="2955">‚≠ê 3. Sensory Overload or Autistic Burnout</h2>
<p data-end="3033" data-start="3002">For children who struggle with:</p>
<ul data-end="3116" data-start="3035">
<li data-end="3056" data-start="3035">
<p data-end="3056" data-start="3037">crowded corridors</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3072" data-start="3057">

</li>
<li data-end="3089" data-start="3073">
<p data-end="3089" data-start="3075">noise levels</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3116" data-start="3090">
<p data-end="3116" data-start="3092">unpredictable routines</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="3190" data-start="3118">The timetable should be paired with <strong data-end="3173" data-start="3154">sensory support</strong>, not replace it.</p>
<h2 data-end="3239" data-start="3192">‚≠ê 4. A Planned Phased Return After Absence</h2>

<ul data-end="3356" data-start="3260">

</li>

</li>
<li data-end="3336" data-start="3304">

</li>
<li data-end="3356" data-start="3337">
<p data-end="3356" data-start="3339">review meetings</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h1 data-end="3414" data-start="3363">‚≠ê When a Reduced Timetable Is <em data-end="3400" data-start="3395">Not</em> Appropriate</h1>
<h2 data-end="3452" data-start="3416">‚ùå 1. As a long term arrangement</h2>
<p data-end="3600" data-start="3453">Reduced timetables must be temporary.<br data-end="3493" data-start="3490" />
If your child still cannot cope, the school must escalate support through the IDP process or request EOTAS.</p>
<h2 data-end="3642" data-start="3602">‚ùå 2. As a behaviour management tool</h2>
<p data-end="3749" data-start="3643">If used to ‚Äúsend a child home early‚Äù due to behaviour, it is an <strong data-end="3729" data-start="3707">informal exclusion</strong>, which is unlawful.</p>
<h2 data-end="3792" data-start="3751">‚ùå 3. When the child is given no work</h2>
<p data-end="3877" data-start="3793">Schools must ensure learning continues during the hours your child is not attending.</p>
<h2 data-end="3921" data-start="3879">‚ùå 4. When a child has unmet ALN needs</h2>
<p data-end="3951" data-start="3922">Reduced hours cannot replace:</p>
<ul data-end="4022" data-start="3953">
<li data-end="3968" data-start="3953">
<p data-end="3968" data-start="3955">assessments</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3985" data-start="3969">
<p data-end="3985" data-start="3971">intervention</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4001" data-start="3986">
<p data-end="4001" data-start="3988">ALN support</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4022" data-start="4002">
<p data-end="4022" data-start="4004">specialist input</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h2 data-end="4062" data-start="4024">‚ùå 5. When the plan is verbal only</h2>
<p data-end="4139" data-start="4063">Anything not written down tends to drift.<br data-end="4107" data-start="4104" />
Parents must see a written plan.</p>
<h2 data-end="4200" data-start="4141">‚ùå 6. When the reduced timetable masks a deeper concern</h2>
<p data-end="4213" data-start="4201">For example:</p>
<ul data-end="4295" data-start="4215">
<li data-end="4227" data-start="4215">
<p data-end="4227" data-start="4217">bullying</p>
</li>

<p data-end="4238" data-start="4230">trauma</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4264" data-start="4239">
<p data-end="4264" data-start="4241">medical deterioration</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4295" data-start="4265">
<p data-end="4295" data-start="4267">school placement breakdown</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="4329" data-start="4297">These require proper assessment.</p>

</li>
<li data-end="4521" data-start="4493">

</li>
<li data-end="4558" data-start="4522">

</li>
<li data-end="4580" data-start="4559">

</li>
<li data-end="4605" data-start="4581">

</li>
</ul>

<h1 data-end="4732" data-start="4692">‚≠ê Should the School Nurse Be Involved?</h1>
<p data-end="4990" data-start="4734">The school nursing team should be involved <strong data-end="4856" data-start="4777">whenever a reduced timetable relates to the child‚Äôs health or medical needs</strong>. Schools cannot make clinical decisions about stamina, recovery or fitness for full time attendance without appropriate health input.</p>
<h3 data-end="5031" data-start="4992">‚≠ê When Involvement <em data-end="5031" data-start="5015">Is Appropriate</em></h3>
<p data-end="5106" data-start="5033">School nurses should be involved when the reduced timetable is linked to:</p>
<ul data-end="5370" data-start="5108">
<li data-end="5122" data-start="5108">
<p data-end="5122" data-start="5110">Long Covid</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5165" data-start="5123">
<p data-end="5165" data-start="5125">chronic fatigue or post viral symptoms</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5195" data-start="5166">
<p data-end="5195" data-start="5168">ongoing pain or migraines</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5242" data-start="5196">
<p data-end="5242" data-start="5198">asthma or long term respiratory conditions</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5275" data-start="5243">
<p data-end="5275" data-start="5245">epilepsy or seizure recovery</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5299" data-start="5276">
<p data-end="5299" data-start="5278">diabetes management</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5325" data-start="5300">
<p data-end="5325" data-start="5302">recovery from surgery</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5370" data-start="5326">
<p data-end="5370" data-start="5328">unexplained illness requiring assessment</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="5393" data-start="5372">Nurses can advise on:</p>
<ul data-end="5586" data-start="5395">
<li data-end="5424" data-start="5395">
<p data-end="5424" data-start="5397">safe levels of attendance</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5451" data-start="5425">
<p data-end="5451" data-start="5427">phased return planning</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5479" data-start="5452">
<p data-end="5479" data-start="5454">medical evidence needed</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5534" data-start="5480">
<p data-end="5534" data-start="5482">referral routes to paediatrics, CAMHS or therapies</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5586" data-start="5535">
<p data-end="5586" data-start="5537">when medical needs tuition should be considered</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3 data-end="5625" data-start="5588">‚≠ê When Involvement <em data-end="5625" data-start="5611">Is Essential</em></h3>
<p data-end="5676" data-start="5627">You should request school nurse involvement when:</p>
<ul data-end="6010" data-start="5678">
<li data-end="5772" data-start="5678">
<p data-end="5772" data-start="5680">a reduced timetable is suggested because of health but no clinician has assessed the child</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5848" data-start="5773">
<p data-end="5848" data-start="5775">the child cannot manage school due to fatigue or physical deterioration</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5888" data-start="5849">
<p data-end="5888" data-start="5851">the timetable is becoming long term</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5951" data-start="5889">
<p data-end="5951" data-start="5891">there is a possibility of needing home or hospital tuition</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6010" data-start="5952">
<p data-end="6010" data-start="5954">attendance is being challenged despite ongoing illness</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3 data-end="6045" data-start="6012">‚≠ê When It May Not Be Required</h3>
<p data-end="6093" data-start="6047">If the reduced timetable is related solely to:</p>
<ul data-end="6186" data-start="6095">
<li data-end="6115" data-start="6095">
<p data-end="6115" data-start="6097">sensory overload</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6127" data-start="6116">
<p data-end="6127" data-start="6118">anxiety</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6166" data-start="6128">
<p data-end="6166" data-start="6130">emotionally based school avoidance</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6186" data-start="6167">
<p data-end="6186" data-start="6169">unmet ALN needs</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="6315" data-start="6188">then the ALNCo usually leads.<br data-end="6220" data-start="6217" />
However, if distress presents with physical symptoms, nurses can still support onward referral.</p>
<h3 data-end="6355" data-start="6317">‚≠ê How to Request Their Involvement</h3>
<p data-end="6373" data-start="6357">Parents can say:</p>
<blockquote data-end="6593" data-start="6375">
<p data-end="6593" data-start="6377">‚ÄúBecause this reduced timetable relates to [health issue], I would like the school nursing team to be involved so that we can ensure the plan meets my child‚Äôs medical needs and follows appropriate clinical guidance.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote>
<p data-end="6661" data-start="6595">Schools cannot refuse a reasonable request for health involvement.</p>

<h1 data-end="6691" data-start="6668">‚≠ê Attendance Coding</h1>
<p data-end="6712" data-start="6693">Schools should use:</p>
<ul data-end="6792" data-start="6714">
<li data-end="6751" data-start="6714">
<p data-end="6751" data-start="6716"><strong data-end="6726" data-start="6716">C code</strong> for authorised absence</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6792" data-start="6752">
<p data-end="6792" data-start="6754"><strong data-end="6764" data-start="6754">I code</strong> for illness when relevant</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="6812" data-start="6794">They must <strong data-end="6811" data-start="6804">not</strong>:</p>
<ul data-end="6947" data-start="6814">
<li data-end="6848" data-start="6814">

</li>
</ul>

<h1 data-end="6980" data-start="6954">‚≠ê If It Is Not Working</h1>
<p data-end="7002" data-start="6982">Ask immediately for:</p>
<ul data-end="7141" data-start="7004">
<li data-end="7039" data-start="7004">
<p data-end="7039" data-start="7006">an IDP assessment or IDP review</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7066" data-start="7040">
<p data-end="7066" data-start="7042">a multi agency meeting</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7088" data-start="7067">
<p data-end="7088" data-start="7069">ALNCo involvement</p>
