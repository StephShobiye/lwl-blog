<p data-end="404" data-start="343"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+ALN+SEND&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">ALN SEND</a> Support and Employment Reform Are the Real Answers**</p>
<p data-end="450" data-start="406"><strong data-end="450" data-start="406"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Learn+Without+Limits+CIC&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">Learn Without Limits CIC — December 2025</a></strong></p>
<p data-end="795" data-start="452">This week, the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Health+Secretary+Wes+Streeting&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">Health Secretary Wes Streeting</a> announced a review into rising <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=mental+health+diagnoses+trends+UK&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">mental health diagnoses in the UK</a>, linking it to the growing benefits bill.<br data-end="607" data-start="604" />
It has sparked fear among <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+disabled+and+neurodivergent&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">disabled and neurodivergent people</a> including thousands of families here in <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=disability+employment+rates+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">Wales</a> that the Government may be preparing to question whether people are truly unwell.</p>
<p data-end="850" data-start="797">But the Government is looking in the wrong direction.</p>
<p data-end="1003" data-start="852">If ministers want to understand why so many disabled and neurodivergent adults end up claiming benefits, they must start with the real upstream causes:</p>
<p data-end="1141" data-start="1005"><strong data-end="1047" data-start="1005"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+SEND+support+failures+in+childhood+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">ALN SEND support failures in childhood</a></strong><br data-end="1050" data-start="1047" />
and<br data-end="1056" data-start="1053" />
<strong data-end="1141" data-start="1056"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=broken+employment+support+system+UK+critiques&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">a broken employment support system</a> that absorbs millions and delivers very little</strong></p>
<p data-end="1402" data-start="1143">Tomorrow, we will be meeting with <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Torsten+Bell+MP+Swansea+West&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">Torsten Bell MP for Swansea West</a> and a <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Junior+Treasury+Minister+UK&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">Junior Treasury Minister</a> to outline a set of reforms that would genuinely reduce long-term benefit dependency. Not by blaming individuals but by fixing the systems that have failed them.</p>
<p data-end="1439" data-start="1404">This is what we will be asking for.</p>
<hr data-end="1444" data-start="1441" />
<h2 data-end="1544" data-start="1446"><strong data-end="1544" data-start="1449">1. ALN SEND failures in childhood create adult dependency and Wales is the clearest example</strong></h2>
<p data-end="1686" data-start="1546">In Wales, the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+Act+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">ALN Act</a> promised early identification and joined up support.<br data-end="1623" data-start="1620" />
Families were told the system would be simpler, fairer, faster.</p>
<p data-end="1699" data-start="1688">The reality</p>
<p data-end="2144" data-start="1701">• children waiting years for ALN SEND and <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=neurodevelopmental+assessments+process&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">neurodevelopmental assessments</a><br data-end="1776" data-start="1773" />
• mainstream schools unable to meet specialist needs<br data-end="1831" data-start="1828" />
• panels repeatedly refusing <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=specialist+placements+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">specialist placements</a><br data-end="1884" data-start="1881" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+CAMHS&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">CAMHS</a> turning away complex cases<br data-end="1921" data-start="1918" />
• parents feeling forced into <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Elective+Home+Education+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">Elective Home Education</a> because support was not available<br data-end="2011" data-start="2008" />
• many <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+SEND+learners+resources&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">ALN SEND learners</a> missing months or even years of education<br data-end="2080" data-start="2077" />
• children internalising the message that they are the problem</p>
<p data-end="2183" data-start="2146">By adulthood, these young people face</p>
<p data-end="2460" data-start="2185">• gaps in basic qualifications<br data-end="2218" data-start="2215" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=chronic+anxiety+support+resources&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">chronic anxiety</a> from years of unmet need<br data-end="2263" data-start="2260" />
• no <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=supported+internships+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">supported internships</a> or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=vocational+pathways+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">vocational pathways</a><br data-end="2315" data-start="2312" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=unresolved+trauma+support+resources&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">unresolved trauma</a> linked to <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=exclusion+isolation+school+based+distress+effects&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">exclusion isolation and school based distress</a><br data-end="2393" data-start="2390" />
• very little understanding from employment agencies or employers</p>
<p data-end="2524" data-start="2462">None of this is the fault of the young person or their family.</p>
<p data-end="2620" data-start="2526">It is the result of structural failures across education health and local authority processes.</p>
<p data-end="2821" data-start="2622"><strong data-end="2821" data-start="2622">When ALN SEND needs go unidentified or unsupported in childhood the result is predictable.<br data-end="2717" data-start="2714" />
You get a generation of adults who were set up to struggle long before they reached the labour market.</strong></p>
<hr data-end="2826" data-start="2823" />
<h2 data-end="2920" data-start="2828"><strong data-end="2920" data-start="2831">2. Employment support in Wales is uniquely failing disabled and neurodivergent people</strong></h2>
<p data-end="3064" data-start="2922">Wales has one of the lowest disabled employment rates in the UK.<br data-end="2989" data-start="2986" />
But the narrative that disabled people do not want to work is simply false.</p>
<p data-end="3088" data-start="3066">The real story is this</p>
<p data-end="3467" data-start="3090">• fewer suitable vacancies especially in rural and deprived areas<br data-end="3158" data-start="3155" />
• employers with very limited understanding of <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=autism+definition+resources&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">autism</a> <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ADHD+definition+resources&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">ADHD</a> <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=trauma+definition+resources&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">trauma</a> or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=PDA+autism+definition&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">PDA</a><br data-end="3233" data-start="3230" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=workplace+adjustments+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">workplace adjustments</a> applied inconsistently or not at all<br data-end="3296" data-start="3293" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=FE+colleges+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">FE colleges</a> delaying ALN SEND support until well after enrolment<br data-end="3365" data-start="3362" />
• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=DWP+contractors+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">DWP contractors</a> routinely misunderstanding ALN SEND profiles especially for women and PDA learners</p>
<p data-end="3523" data-start="3469">Meanwhile employment programmes offer little more than</p>
<p data-end="3681" data-start="3525">• <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=alternatives+to+generic+CV+workshops&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">generic CV workshops</a><br data-end="3550" data-start="3547" />
• endless cycles of reviews and goal setting<br data-end="3597" data-start="3594" />
• no specialist support for <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=job+carving+definition+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">job carving</a> workplace adjustments or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=employer+advocacy+for+disabled+employees&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">employer advocacy</a></p>
<p data-end="3793" data-start="3683">Disabled people are not resisting work.<br data-end="3725" data-start="3722" />
They are being failed by the agencies that are paid to support them.</p>
<hr data-end="3798" data-start="3795" />
<h2 data-end="3880" data-start="3800">**3. The biggest inefficiency in the benefits system is not disabled people</h2>
<p data-end="3938" data-start="3881">It is the employment contractors being paid for failure**</p>
<p data-end="3990" data-start="3940">Employment support agencies are paid primarily for</p>
<p data-end="4081" data-start="3992">• signing people up<br data-end="4014" data-start="4011" />
• keeping them attending<br data-end="4041" data-start="4038" />
• completing administrative milestones</p>
<p data-end="4104" data-start="4083">They are not paid for</p>
<p data-end="4324" data-start="4106">• getting disabled people into work<br data-end="4144" data-start="4141" />
• ensuring adjustments are actually implemented<br data-end="4194" data-start="4191" />
• helping people stay employed<br data-end="4227" data-start="4224" />
• matching ND profiles to meaningful jobs<br data-end="4271" data-start="4268" />
• supporting transitions beyond the first few weeks</p>
<p data-end="4365" data-start="4326">The incentives are the wrong way round.</p>
<p data-end="4485" data-start="4367">Providers earn more money by keeping people active on their books for long periods rather than helping them into work.</p>
<p data-end="4566" data-start="4487">So people are repeatedly recycled through programmes even when nothing changes.</p>
<p data-end="4609" data-start="4568"><strong data-end="4609" data-start="4568">People are strung along not supported</strong></p>
<p data-end="4721" data-start="4611">Reviewing mental health diagnoses does nothing to solve this.<br data-end="4675" data-start="4672" />
Reforming the employment support market would.</p>
<hr data-end="4726" data-start="4723" />
<h1 data-end="4800" data-start="4728"><strong data-end="4800" data-start="4730">4. What Learn Without Limits CIC will be asking the Treasury to do</strong></h1>
<p data-end="4975" data-start="4802">We believe there are five reforms that the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Treasury+UK+Government&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">Treasury</a> can lead which would reduce long term benefit dependency while improving outcomes for disabled and neurodivergent people.</p>
<hr data-end="4980" data-start="4977" />
<h2 data-end="4997" data-start="4982">**ACTION 1</h2>
<p data-end="5100" data-start="4998">Reform the way employment support providers are paid so they are rewarded for outcomes not paperwork**</p>
<p data-end="5153" data-start="5102">At least half of provider payments should depend on</p>
<p data-end="5284" data-start="5155">• securing paid work<br data-end="5178" data-start="5175" />
• keeping someone in work for 13 and 26 weeks<br data-end="5226" data-start="5223" />
• reducing support need<br data-end="5252" data-start="5249" />
• improving wellbeing outcomes</p>
<p data-end="5390" data-start="5286">This removes the current incentive for providers to keep people “engaged” without helping them progress.</p>
<hr data-end="5395" data-start="5392" />
<h2 data-end="5412" data-start="5397">**ACTION 2</h2>
<p data-end="5500" data-start="5413">Launch a Treasury led review of <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+SEND+educational+and+employment+pathways+in+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">ALN SEND educational and employment pathways in Wales</a>**</p>
<p data-end="5528" data-start="5502">This review should examine</p>
<p data-end="5888" data-start="5530">• the economic cost of delayed ALN SEND identification<br data-end="5587" data-start="5584" />
• the long term impact of school absence exclusions and EOTAS patterns<br data-end="5660" data-start="5657" />
• outcomes for ALN SEND learners entering adulthood<br data-end="5714" data-start="5711" />
• performance data for DWP contractors working with neurodivergent learners<br data-end="5792" data-start="5789" />
• the cost savings achieved when early support is provided instead of late crisis intervention</p>
<p data-end="5953" data-start="5890">This places the Treasury in the lead on evidence driven reform.</p>
<hr data-end="5958" data-start="5955" />
<h2 data-end="5975" data-start="5960">**ACTION 3</h2>
<p data-end="6034" data-start="5976">Pilot a <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Supported+ALN+SEND+Employment+Guarantee+in+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">Supported ALN SEND Employment Guarantee in Wales</a>**</p>
<p data-end="6079" data-start="6036">Wales is ideal for a national pilot because</p>
<p data-end="6231" data-start="6081">• population size is manageable<br data-end="6115" data-start="6112" />
• ALN SEND needs are clearly recorded<br data-end="6155" data-start="6152" />
• employment inactivity is high<br data-end="6189" data-start="6186" />
• measurable change would appear quickly</p>
<p data-end="6260" data-start="6233">The Guarantee would provide</p>
<p data-end="6486" data-start="6262">• supported internships<br data-end="6288" data-start="6285" />
• specialist <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=neurodiversity+job+coaches&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2797857511026792012" target="_blank">neurodiversity job coaches</a><br data-end="6330" data-start="6327" />
• incentives for employers who provide sustained placements<br data-end="6392" data-start="6389" />
• enforcement of workplace adjustment duties<br data-end="6439" data-start="6436" />
• supported transitions from FE to employment</p>
<hr data-end="6491" data-start="6488" />
<h2 data-end="6508" data-start="6493">**ACTION 4</h2>
<p data-end="6578" data-start="6509">Require ALN SEND expertise in every DWP contract operating in Wales**</p>
<p data-end="6613" data-start="6580">Funding agreements should require</p>
<p data-end="6808" data-start="6615">• mandatory ALN SEND competency training<br data-end="6658" data-start="6655" />
• specialist ND advisers in each Welsh region<br data-end="6706" data-start="6703" />
• clear performance reporting by disability type<br data-end="6757" data-start="6754" />
• audited compliance with adjustment requirements</p>
<p data-end="6894" data-start="6810">Public money should not go to providers who cannot support disabled people properly.</p>
<hr data-end="6899" data-start="6896" />
<h2 data-end="6916" data-start="6901">**ACTION 5</h2>
<p data-end="6982" data-start="6917">Champion a Treasury wide message that early support saves money**</p>
<p data-end="7068" data-start="6984">If the Government wants to reduce adult benefit dependency it must invest earlier in</p>
<p data-end="7202" data-start="7070">• ALN SEND identification<br data-end="7098" data-start="7095" />
• specialist placements<br data-end="7124" data-start="7121" />
• therapeutic support<br data-end="7148" data-start="7145" />
• stable transitions from school to FE to employment</p>
<p data-end="7268" data-start="7204">Every pound spent early saves several pounds later in adulthood.</p>
<hr data-end="7273" data-start="7270" />
<h1 data-end="7291" data-start="7275">**Conclusion</h1>
<p data-end="7353" data-start="7292">Stop blaming individuals<br data-end="7319" data-start="7316" />
Fix the systems that failed them**</p>
<p data-end="7538" data-start="7355">Rising mental health claims do not indicate widespread over diagnosis.<br data-end="7428" data-start="7425" />
They reflect the cost of a generation whose needs went unsupported for years during childhood and adolescence.</p>
<p data-end="7626" data-start="7540">Instead of questioning whether people are truly unwell the Government should be asking</p>
<p data-end="7832" data-start="7628">• why ALN SEND children wait years for assessments<br data-end="7681" data-start="7678" />
• why their education breaks down<br data-end="7717" data-start="7714" />
• why Wales has such low disabled employment rates<br data-end="7770" data-start="7767" />
• why contractors are not held accountable for poor outcomes</p>
<p data-end="7929" data-start="7834">The problem is never the individual.<br data-end="7873" data-start="7870" />
The problem is the structure of the systems around them.</p>
<p data-end="8012" data-start="7931">We will be asking the Treasury to take the lead in fixing those systems.</p>
<p data-end="8094" data-start="8014">If Ministers want fewer disabled people stuck on benefits the solution is simple</p>
<p data-end="8261" data-start="8096"><strong data-end="8261" data-start="8096">Support ALN SEND children properly<br data-end="8135" data-start="8132" />
Rebuild employment support<br data-end="8164" data-start="8161" />
Reward genuine outcomes not box ticking<br data-end="8206" data-start="8203" />
Change the incentives and you change the entire story</strong></p>
