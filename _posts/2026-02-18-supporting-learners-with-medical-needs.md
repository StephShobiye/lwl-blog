<h2 data-end="607" data-start="555">Supporting Learners with Medical Needs in Wales</h2><h3 data-end="693" data-start="608">A practical guide for ALN families on health plans, school duties, and your rights</h3><h1 data-end="826" data-start="771"><p data-end="821" data-start="695" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Children and young people with medical needs are protected under Welsh education law and the Additional Learning Needs system.</p><p data-end="1018" data-start="823" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Schools have clear duties to support learners with healthcare needs, whether those needs are short-term, long-term, fluctuating, mental health-related, or linked to neurodevelopmental conditions.</p><p data-end="1208" data-start="1020" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">This guide explains what schools must provide, how Individual Healthcare Plans work, and how education and health services are expected to work together to keep children safe and included.</p><p data-end="1354" data-start="1210" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">It also includes guidance for electively home-educated families, children with chronic illness, and those experiencing slow post-viral recovery.</p><hr data-end="1359" data-start="1356" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;" /></h1><h2 data-end="1406" data-start="1361">1. What counts as a medical need in Wales?</h2><h1 data-end="826" data-start="771"><p data-end="1504" data-start="1408" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">A medical need is <strong data-end="1469" data-start="1426">any physical or mental health condition</strong> that affects a child’s ability to:</p><ul data-end="1573" data-start="1506" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="1526" data-start="1506"><p data-end="1526" data-start="1508">access education</p></li><li data-end="1547" data-start="1527"><p data-end="1547" data-start="1529">attend regularly</p></li><li data-end="1573" data-start="1548"><p data-end="1573" data-start="1550">remain safe in school</p></li></ul><p data-end="1612" data-start="1575" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">This includes, but is not limited to:</p><ul data-end="1981" data-start="1614" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="1624" data-start="1614"><p data-end="1624" data-start="1616">asthma</p></li><li data-end="1650" data-start="1625"><p data-end="1650" data-start="1627">ADHD medication needs</p></li><li data-end="1663" data-start="1651"><p data-end="1663" data-start="1653">epilepsy</p></li><li data-end="1676" data-start="1664"><p data-end="1676" data-start="1666">diabetes</p></li><li data-end="1719" data-start="1677"><p data-end="1719" data-start="1679">chronic fatigue or post-viral symptoms</p></li><li data-end="1748" data-start="1720"><p data-end="1748" data-start="1722">anxiety or panic attacks</p></li><li data-end="1780" data-start="1749"><p data-end="1780" data-start="1751">gastrointestinal conditions</p></li><li data-end="1818" data-start="1781"><p data-end="1818" data-start="1783">feeding or hydration difficulties</p></li><li data-end="1848" data-start="1819"><p data-end="1848" data-start="1821">allergies and anaphylaxis</p></li><li data-end="1863" data-start="1849"><p data-end="1863" data-start="1851">Long Covid</p></li><li data-end="1896" data-start="1864"><p data-end="1896" data-start="1866">mobility or continence needs</p></li><li data-end="1925" data-start="1897"><p data-end="1925" data-start="1899">mental health conditions</p></li><li data-end="1981" data-start="1926"><p data-end="1981" data-start="1928">medication that must be given during the school day</p></li></ul><p data-end="2110" data-start="1983" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><strong data-end="2044" data-start="1983">Medical needs do not need to meet a diagnostic threshold.</strong><br data-end="2047" data-start="2044" />If a condition affects safety, access or attendance, it counts.</p><p data-end="2231" data-start="2112" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><strong data-end="2143" data-start="2112">Welsh Government reference:</strong><br data-end="2146" data-start="2143" />Schools must follow <em data-end="2209" data-start="2166">Supporting Learners with Healthcare Needs</em> (statutory guidance).</p><p data-end="2291" data-start="2233" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><a class="decorated-link" data-end="2291" data-start="2233" href="https://www.gov.wales/supporting-learners-healthcare-needs" rel="noopener" target="_new">https://www.gov.wales/supporting-learners-healthcare-needs<span aria-hidden="true" class="ms-0.5 inline-block align-middle leading-none"><svg aria-hidden="true" class="block h-[0.75em] w-[0.75em] stroke-current stroke-[0.75]" data-rtl-flip="" height="20" width="20" xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg"><use fill="currentColor" href="/cdn/assets/sprites-core-5p0gevgh.svg#304883"></use></svg></span></a></p><hr data-end="2296" data-start="2293" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;" /></h1><h2 data-end="2359" data-start="2298">2. When a school must create an Individual Healthcare Plan</h2><h1 data-end="826" data-start="771"><p data-end="2441" data-start="2361" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Welsh Government guidance requires an <strong data-end="2435" data-start="2399">Individual Healthcare Plan (IHP)</strong> when:</p><ul data-end="2830" data-start="2443" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="2499" data-start="2443"><p data-end="2499" data-start="2445">a child has a long term or complex medical condition</p></li><li data-end="2538" data-start="2500"><p data-end="2538" data-start="2502">medication must be given in school</p></li><li data-end="2576" data-start="2539"><p data-end="2576" data-start="2541">emergency procedures are required</p></li><li data-end="2611" data-start="2577"><p data-end="2611" data-start="2579">a condition affects attendance</p></li><li data-end="2661" data-start="2612"><p data-end="2661" data-start="2614">a condition affects participation in learning</p></li><li data-end="2720" data-start="2662"><p data-end="2720" data-start="2664">adjustments, rest breaks or safety planning are needed</p></li><li data-end="2760" data-start="2721"><p data-end="2760" data-start="2723">symptoms fluctuate or are invisible</p></li><li data-end="2830" data-start="2761"><p data-end="2830" data-start="2763">a child cannot reliably self-manage symptoms, hydration or energy</p></li></ul><p data-end="2990" data-start="2832" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Schools cannot refuse to create a plan because they are busy, because a condition is described as anxiety, or because a child appears to be coping or masking.</p><p data-end="3077" data-start="2992" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">If a medical condition affects safety or access to education, <strong data-end="3076" data-start="3054">a plan is required</strong>.</p><hr data-end="3082" data-start="3079" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;" /></h1><h2 data-end="3142" data-start="3084">3. What should be in a good Individual Healthcare Plan?</h2><h1 data-end="826" data-start="771"><p data-end="3172" data-start="3144" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">A strong IHP should include:</p><ul data-end="3805" data-start="3174" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="3214" data-start="3174"><p data-end="3214" data-start="3176">a clear description of the condition</p></li><li data-end="3250" data-start="3215"><p data-end="3250" data-start="3217">signs and symptoms to watch for</p></li><li data-end="3292" data-start="3251"><p data-end="3292" data-start="3253">known triggers and how to reduce them</p></li><li data-end="3360" data-start="3293"><p data-end="3360" data-start="3295">baseline information on energy, eating, drinking and regulation</p></li><li data-end="3385" data-start="3361"><p data-end="3385" data-start="3363">emergency procedures</p></li><li data-end="3444" data-start="3386"><p data-end="3444" data-start="3388">medication details, including dose, timing and storage</p></li><li data-end="3479" data-start="3445"><p data-end="3479" data-start="3447">who is trained to deliver care</p></li><li data-end="3513" data-start="3480"><p data-end="3513" data-start="3482">rest breaks and sensory needs</p></li><li data-end="3551" data-start="3514"><p data-end="3551" data-start="3516">hydration or nutrition strategies</p></li><li data-end="3588" data-start="3552"><p data-end="3588" data-start="3554">when parents should be contacted</p></li><li data-end="3632" data-start="3589"><p data-end="3632" data-start="3591">when the child is too unwell for school</p></li><li data-end="3667" data-start="3633"><p data-end="3667" data-start="3635">steps for return after illness</p></li><li data-end="3707" data-start="3668"><p data-end="3707" data-start="3670">who is responsible for daily checks</p></li><li data-end="3742" data-start="3708"><p data-end="3742" data-start="3710">an agreed communication system</p></li><li data-end="3805" data-start="3743"><p data-end="3805" data-start="3745">reasonable adjustments during lessons, movement and breaks</p></li></ul><p data-end="3944" data-start="3807" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">The plan should be written <strong data-end="3868" data-start="3834">with full parental involvement</strong> and reviewed at least annually, or sooner if the child’s condition changes.</p><hr data-end="3949" data-start="3946" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;" /></h1><h2 data-end="3994" data-start="3951">4. Schools must involve the right people</h2><h1 data-end="826" data-start="771"><p data-end="4060" data-start="3996" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">An Individual Healthcare Plan should be written with input from:</p><ul data-end="4249" data-start="4062" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="4085" data-start="4062"><p data-end="4085" data-start="4064">the parent or carer</p></li><li data-end="4106" data-start="4086"><p data-end="4106" data-start="4088">the school nurse</p></li><li data-end="4150" data-start="4107"><p data-end="4150" data-start="4109">GP or specialist team where appropriate</p></li><li data-end="4171" data-start="4151"><p data-end="4171" data-start="4153">the school ALNCo</p></li><li data-end="4203" data-start="4172"><p data-end="4203" data-start="4174">pastoral or wellbeing staff</p></li><li data-end="4249" data-start="4204"><p data-end="4249" data-start="4206">the child or young person, where possible</p></li></ul><p data-end="4345" data-start="4251" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Schools must not write a health plan in isolation without consulting healthcare professionals.</p><p data-end="4461" data-start="4347" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">If a child has an <strong data-end="4402" data-start="4365">Individual Development Plan (IDP)</strong>, the IHP should sit alongside it and inform ALN provision.</p><hr data-end="4466" data-start="4463" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;" /></h1><h2 data-end="4507" data-start="4468">5. Medication in school: your rights</h2><h1 data-end="826" data-start="771"><p data-end="4522" data-start="4509" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Schools must:</p><ul data-end="4801" data-start="4524" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="4551" data-start="4524"><p data-end="4551" data-start="4526">store medication safely</p></li><li data-end="4584" data-start="4552"><p data-end="4584" data-start="4554">train staff to administer it</p></li><li data-end="4607" data-start="4585"><p data-end="4607" data-start="4587">keep clear records</p></li><li data-end="4662" data-start="4608"><p data-end="4662" data-start="4610">allow children to carry inhalers where appropriate</p></li><li data-end="4730" data-start="4663"><p data-end="4730" data-start="4665">make reasonable adjustments for sensory needs around medication</p></li><li data-end="4801" data-start="4731"><p data-end="4801" data-start="4733">support hydration and food needs linked to medication side effects</p></li></ul><p data-end="4914" data-start="4803" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Schools cannot require a parent to attend daily to administer medication unless this is <strong data-end="4913" data-start="4891">by parental choice</strong>.</p><hr data-end="4919" data-start="4916" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;" /></h1><h2 data-end="4963" data-start="4921">6. When medical needs affect attendance</h2><h1 data-end="826" data-start="771"><p data-end="5091" data-start="4965" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Where a condition causes fatigue, pain, anxiety, dysregulation or difficulty managing a full school day, families may request:</p><ul data-end="5266" data-start="5093" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="5116" data-start="5093"><p data-end="5116" data-start="5095">a reduced timetable</p></li><li data-end="5148" data-start="5117"><p data-end="5148" data-start="5119">a phased reintegration plan</p></li><li data-end="5175" data-start="5149"><p data-end="5175" data-start="5151">temporary home tuition</p></li><li data-end="5207" data-start="5176"><p data-end="5207" data-start="5178">rest breaks or quiet spaces</p></li><li data-end="5242" data-start="5208"><p data-end="5242" data-start="5210">adjusted start or finish times</p></li><li data-end="5266" data-start="5243"><p data-end="5266" data-start="5245">a review of the IDP</p></li></ul><p data-end="5335" data-start="5268" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">If a child is too unwell to attend long term, families may request:</p><ul data-end="5455" data-start="5337" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="5362" data-start="5337"><p data-end="5362" data-start="5339">medical needs tuition</p></li><li data-end="5455" data-start="5363"><p data-end="5455" data-start="5365">Education Otherwise Than At School (EOTAS) where attendance becomes medically impossible</p></li></ul><p data-end="5602" data-start="5457" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Parents are <strong data-end="5485" data-start="5469">not required</strong> to provide repeated GP notes. Schools must accept parental explanation unless there is a clear safeguarding concern.</p><hr data-end="5607" data-start="5604" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;" /></h1><h2 data-end="5666" data-start="5609">7. What happens for electively home-educated children?</h2><h1 data-end="826" data-start="771"><p data-end="5720" data-start="5668" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Health support does <strong data-end="5695" data-start="5688">not</strong> end with deregistration.</p><p data-end="5783" data-start="5722" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Electively home-educated children continue to have access to:</p><ul data-end="5931" data-start="5785" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="5812" data-start="5785"><p data-end="5812" data-start="5787">school nursing services</p></li><li data-end="5829" data-start="5813"><p data-end="5829" data-start="5815">vaccinations</p></li><li data-end="5847" data-start="5830"><p data-end="5847" data-start="5832">health checks</p></li><li data-end="5871" data-start="5848"><p data-end="5871" data-start="5850">continence services</p></li><li data-end="5896" data-start="5872"><p data-end="5896" data-start="5874">paediatric referrals</p></li><li data-end="5931" data-start="5897"><p data-end="5931" data-start="5899">long-term condition monitoring</p></li></ul><p data-end="5988" data-start="5933" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Families can contact their local health board directly.</p><hr data-end="5993" data-start="5990" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;" /></h1><h2 data-end="6051" data-start="5995">8. When you should request a review or change of plan</h2><h1 data-end="826" data-start="771"><p data-end="6085" data-start="6053" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Request an urgent IHP review if:</p><ul data-end="6295" data-start="6087" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="6119" data-start="6087"><p data-end="6119" data-start="6089">Symptoms worsen or fluctuate</p></li><li data-end="6142" data-start="6120"><p data-end="6142" data-start="6122">medication changes</p></li><li data-end="6163" data-start="6143"><p data-end="6163" data-start="6145">attendance drops</p></li><li data-end="6206" data-start="6164"><p data-end="6206" data-start="6166">fatigue or sensory overwhelm increases</p></li><li data-end="6251" data-start="6207"><p data-end="6251" data-start="6209">the child cannot regulate without breaks</p></li><li data-end="6295" data-start="6252"><p data-end="6295" data-start="6254">staff say they cannot meet health needs</p></li></ul><p data-end="6322" data-start="6297" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">Request an IDP review if:</p><ul data-end="6490" data-start="6324" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><li data-end="6357" data-start="6324"><p data-end="6357" data-start="6326">medical needs impact learning</p></li><li data-end="6407" data-start="6358"><p data-end="6407" data-start="6360">health and education provision need alignment</p></li><li data-end="6447" data-start="6408"><p data-end="6447" data-start="6410">reduced timetables become prolonged</p></li><li data-end="6490" data-start="6448"><p data-end="6490" data-start="6450">anxiety or EBSA develops due to health</p></li></ul><p data-end="6564" data-start="6492" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">If attendance becomes medically impossible, request EOTAS consideration.</p><hr data-end="6569" data-start="6566" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;" /></h1><h2 data-end="6629" data-start="6571">9. Where to find the official Welsh Government guidance</h2><h1 data-end="826" data-start="771"><p data-end="6667" data-start="6631" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">The statutory duties are set out in:</p><p data-end="6731" data-start="6669" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;"><a href="https://www.gov.wales/sites/default/files/publications/2018-12/supporting-learners-with-healthcare-needs.pdf" target="_blank"><em data-end="6712" data-start="6669">Supporting Learners with Healthcare Needs</em> (Welsh Government)</a></p><p data-end="6791" data-start="6733" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;">This document is essential for families and professionals.</p><hr data-end="6796" data-start="6793" style="font-size: medium; font-weight: 400;" /></h1><h2 data-end="6833" data-start="6798"><span style="color: #660000;">10.
