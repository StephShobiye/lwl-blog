<p data-end="436" data-start="376">Not everything that helps children grow needs to be therapy.</p>
<p data-end="752" data-start="438">For most of human history, wisdom was passed down through stories. Around fires. At the end of long days. Or at the start of new ones. Long before schools, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=psychology+definition&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">psychology</a> or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=parenting+manuals&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">parenting manuals</a>, humans used stories to teach children how to live in a world that could be hard, unfair, confusing and beautiful all at once.</p>
<p data-end="910" data-start="754">Stories did something very specific. They helped children regulate, make sense of difficulty, and understand boundaries without being shamed or overwhelmed.</p>
<p data-end="960" data-start="912">That is not a coincidence. It is how we evolved.</p>
<h3 data-end="1025" data-start="962">Stories are older than schools, therapy or the written word</h3>
<p data-end="1083" data-start="1027">Long before humans could read or write, we told stories.</p>
<p data-end="1288" data-start="1085">After a hard day of hunting, gathering or travelling, early humans sat together around a fire with their children and elders. In the flicker of firelight, they passed on what mattered most through story.</p>
<p data-end="1335" data-start="1290">Not facts.<br data-end="1303" data-start="1300" />
Not instructions.<br data-end="1323" data-start="1320" />
But meaning.</p>
<p data-end="1352" data-start="1337">Stories taught:</p>
<ul data-end="1511" data-start="1353">
<li data-end="1369" data-start="1353">
<p data-end="1369" data-start="1355">what to fear</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1389" data-start="1370">
<p data-end="1389" data-start="1372">what to respect</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1409" data-start="1390">
<p data-end="1409" data-start="1392">when to persist</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1426" data-start="1410">
<p data-end="1426" data-start="1412">when to stop</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1459" data-start="1427">
<p data-end="1459" data-start="1429">how to live alongside others</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1511" data-start="1460">
<p data-end="1511" data-start="1462">how to survive hardship without losing yourself</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="1570" data-start="1513">This is how humans evolved to teach <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+emotional+regulation&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">emotional regulation</a>.</p>
<p data-end="1683" data-start="1572">The rhythm of a familiar voice.<br data-end="1606" data-start="1603" />
The predictability of a known ending.<br data-end="1646" data-start="1643" />
The safety of being together at rest.</p>
<p data-end="1922" data-start="1685">Long before the written word existed, stories helped children make sense of a world that was uncertain and often dangerous. They learned about loss, courage, boundaries and responsibility through characters and metaphor, not explanation.</p>
<p data-end="2066" data-start="1924">That matters, because it means stories are not an optional extra or a cultural add on. They are part of our biological and social inheritance.</p>
<p data-end="2103" data-start="2068">They work because they always have.</p>
<h3 data-end="2154" data-start="2105">What replaced stories did not do the same job</h3>
<p data-end="2371" data-start="2156">In many households today, that shared space has been quietly replaced. Not by silence, but by screens. By fast moving content designed to provoke outrage, comparison and emotional spikes rather than calm or meaning.</p>
<p data-end="2479" data-start="2373">This is not a judgement of parents. It is a reflection of the environment we are all trying to survive in.</p>
<p data-end="2660" data-start="2481"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+algorithms&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">Algorithms</a> are not neutral. They are designed to keep attention by amplifying intensity. Over time, that intensity crowds out the quieter practices that help children feel steady.</p>
<p data-end="2724" data-start="2662">When stories disappear entirely, something else fills the gap.</p><p data-end="2724" data-start="2662"><br /></p><p data-end="2724" data-start="2662"></p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEhO92jlZ3zqYYIEUJmH8siwJutHxTx8Kv7ZcKRF86fnPpn0C7oqvwoOcuu6v_2FqPnyMJUjl38Jran6bv563pGg58irLOpci0xrvhs1EcqJTcLnE5x0EQX4dD5gZaxfaHt_wvHXzuyN6oOU4bgpQCerP5HI2AoRdEXwokHKOZLQvAXuTokOms11IJgVVdWQ" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img alt="" data-original-height="1536" data-original-width="1024" height="294" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEhO92jlZ3zqYYIEUJmH8siwJutHxTx8Kv7ZcKRF86fnPpn0C7oqvwoOcuu6v_2FqPnyMJUjl38Jran6bv563pGg58irLOpci0xrvhs1EcqJTcLnE5x0EQX4dD5gZaxfaHt_wvHXzuyN6oOU4bgpQCerP5HI2AoRdEXwokHKOZLQvAXuTokOms11IJgVVdWQ=w196-h294" width="196" /></a></div><br /><br /><p></p>
<h3 data-end="2781" data-start="2726">Stories are not nostalgia. They are infrastructure.</h3>
<p data-end="2891" data-start="2783">A story is not just entertainment. It is a handover point between the outside world and a child’s inner one.</p>
<p data-end="2901" data-start="2893">Stories:</p>
<ul data-end="3042" data-start="2902">
<li data-end="2929" data-start="2902">
<p data-end="2929" data-start="2904">slow the nervous system</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2955" data-start="2930">
<p data-end="2955" data-start="2932">create predictability</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2980" data-start="2956">
<p data-end="2980" data-start="2958">externalise problems</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3007" data-start="2981">
<p data-end="3007" data-start="2983">teach cause and effect</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3042" data-start="3008">
<p data-end="3042" data-start="3010">build meaning without pressure</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="3166" data-start="3044">That is why children ask for the same story again and again. Repetition is reassurance. Knowing what comes next is safety.</p>
<p data-end="3222" data-start="3168">Stories also do not have to happen at bedtime to work.</p>
<p data-end="3450" data-start="3224">In our house, bedtime was the worst possible time. By then, my son was exhausted and had no capacity for language at all. So we told stories at breakfast instead. Same stories. Same adult. Different timing. No cost. Big impact.</p>
<p data-end="3615" data-start="3452">That small adaptation is a good example of what inclusion actually looks like. It is not always about money. Often it is about timing, observation, and flexibility.</p><p data-end="3615" data-start="3452"><br /></p><p data-end="3615" data-start="3452"></p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEgSAulccVORWBChbXkQfpiaEU5mab7oa7yc4uUdUJVmo32N4s-egiZXREpOspwM_ipoxyILOjCNpiAKHkAHpvsCSvG5wJ1HfZCmjtO_yDbzInYTmRuf2zCkNGpLKr4esrX8OIv6rH3t_E73RmmEXKrR3aWX9ABWh05G59n5CgKhi1KH3Cmlrw_laidIi2tM" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img alt="" data-original-height="1024" data-original-width="1024" height="353" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEgSAulccVORWBChbXkQfpiaEU5mab7oa7yc4uUdUJVmo32N4s-egiZXREpOspwM_ipoxyILOjCNpiAKHkAHpvsCSvG5wJ1HfZCmjtO_yDbzInYTmRuf2zCkNGpLKr4esrX8OIv6rH3t_E73RmmEXKrR3aWX9ABWh05G59n5CgKhi1KH3Cmlrw_laidIi2tM=w353-h353" width="353" /></a></div><br /><br /><p></p>
<h3 data-end="3651" data-start="3617">Wales already has what we need</h3>
<p data-end="3734" data-start="3653">One of the quiet strengths of Wales is its <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=public+library+service+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">public library service</a>. Libraries are:</p>
<ul data-end="3799" data-start="3735">
<li data-end="3743" data-start="3735">
<p data-end="3743" data-start="3737">free</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3753" data-start="3744">
<p data-end="3753" data-start="3746">local</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3765" data-start="3754">
<p data-end="3765" data-start="3756">trusted</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3779" data-start="3766">
<p data-end="3779" data-start="3768">inclusive</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3799" data-start="3780">
<p data-end="3799" data-start="3782">non judgemental</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="3979" data-start="3801">This means story led approaches do not exclude families on low incomes. They do not require devices. They do not require specialist input. They simply require time and intention.</p>
<p data-end="4022" data-start="3981">In a <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=cost+of+living+crisis&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">cost of living crisis</a>, that matters.</p>
<h3 data-end="4062" data-start="4024">Librarians have secret superpowers</h3>
<p data-end="4173" data-start="4064">One of the most underappreciated assets in Wales is not just our libraries, but the people who work in them.</p>
<p data-end="4331" data-start="4175"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=what+do+librarians+do&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">Librarians</a> have a kind of professional intuition that is rarely talked about. They are exceptionally good at matching the <em data-end="4304" data-start="4297">right</em> book to the <em data-end="4324" data-start="4317">right</em> child.</p>
<p data-end="4363" data-start="4333">Not by age on a spine, but by:</p>
<ul data-end="4675" data-start="4364">
<li data-end="4417" data-start="4364">
<p data-end="4417" data-start="4366">developmental stage rather than chronological age</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4462" data-start="4418">
<p data-end="4462" data-start="4420">emotional need rather than reading level</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4533" data-start="4463">
<p data-end="4533" data-start="4465">visual accessibility for children with limited language processing</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4594" data-start="4534">
<p data-end="4594" data-start="4536">illustration quality for children who struggle with text</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4675" data-start="4595">
<p data-end="4675" data-start="4597">themes that can gently support a specific behavioural or emotional challenge</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="4935" data-start="4677">If you explain what you are trying to help a child understand, such as coping with frustration, respecting boundaries, managing anxiety, or feeling different, a good librarian can often suggest a story that does exactly that without turning it into a lesson.</p>
<p data-end="4993" data-start="4937">Even better, they are trained to think beyond one child.</p>
<p data-end="5031" data-start="4995">They can help you choose books that:</p>
<ul data-end="5210" data-start="5032">
<li data-end="5068" data-start="5032">
<p data-end="5068" data-start="5034">work for <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=neurodivergent+children+definition&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">neurodivergent children</a></p>
</li>
<li data-end="5108" data-start="5069">
<p data-end="5108" data-start="5071">also benefit siblings or classmates</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5152" data-start="5109">
<p data-end="5152" data-start="5111">are inclusive without being patronising</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5210" data-start="5153">
<p data-end="5210" data-start="5155">can be shared in group settings as well as one to one</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="5422" data-start="5212">And if the perfect book is not currently stocked by your local library, most councils will order it in so that you can borrow it. That means families are not limited to what happens to be on the shelf that day.</p>
<p data-end="5463" data-start="5424">This matters, because it keeps support:</p>
<ul data-end="5555" data-start="5464">
<li data-end="5472" data-start="5464">
<p data-end="5472" data-start="5466">free</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5482" data-start="5473">
<p data-end="5482" data-start="5475">local</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5503" data-start="5483">
<p data-end="5503" data-start="5485">non stigmatising</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5555" data-start="5504">
<p data-end="5555" data-start="5506">accessible to families under financial pressure</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="5717" data-start="5557">Libraries are not just buildings full of books. They are staffed by professionals whose job is to connect people with the right information at the right moment.</p>
<p data-end="5820" data-start="5719">Used well, they are one of the most inclusive and cost-effective early support tools we already have.</p><p data-end="5820" data-start="5719"><br /></p><p data-end="5820" data-start="5719"><br /></p>
<h3 data-end="5864" data-start="5822"><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEg5xILPMpCoyUUqP-oOvJ6dkin7PQUc9CAbBqsg8QfPAYAGZjOrF_qmcApCFvpIGFv41YKLnp7MK5dsisEzI0cYBTlyANlCJAS764vikxQDi1ymNpYAoXLiM_y91c1jfUAbSNeuUDurALYQW34J81I56u-BTMELQAcef4D9SkN6beUi-YBgX0S8pVLCHVEV" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img alt="" data-original-height="1024" data-original-width="1024" height="391" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEg5xILPMpCoyUUqP-oOvJ6dkin7PQUc9CAbBqsg8QfPAYAGZjOrF_qmcApCFvpIGFv41YKLnp7MK5dsisEzI0cYBTlyANlCJAS764vikxQDi1ymNpYAoXLiM_y91c1jfUAbSNeuUDurALYQW34J81I56u-BTMELQAcef4D9SkN6beUi-YBgX0S8pVLCHVEV=w391-h391" width="391" /></a></div><br /><br /></h3><h3 data-end="5864" data-start="5822">Stories that quietly teach life skills</h3>
<p data-end="6009" data-start="5866">Below are examples of traditional stories that help children build resilience, boundaries, and self-regulation. Not as therapy. Just as stories.</p>
<p data-end="6241" data-start="6011"><strong data-end="6040" data-start="6011"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=The+Hare+and+the+Tortoise+summary&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">The Hare and the Tortoise</a></strong><br data-end="6043" data-start="6040" />
Teaches persistence over speed and effort over comparison. Especially powerful for children who feel behind or discouraged.<br data-end="6169" data-start="6166" />
Simple message: You do not have to be fast. You just have to keep going.</p>
<p data-end="6439" data-start="6243"><strong data-end="6252" data-start="6243"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Atlas+story+summary&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">Atlas</a></strong><br data-end="6255" data-start="6252" />
Explains why adults carry responsibility so children do not have to yet. Strength as responsibility, not control.<br data-end="6371" data-start="6368" />
Simple message: Grown-ups carry the sky until you are strong enough.</p>
<p data-end="6602" data-start="6441"><strong data-end="6467" data-start="6441"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=The+Lion+and+the+Mouse+story+summary&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">The Lion and the Mouse</a></strong><br data-end="6470" data-start="6467" />
Shows that kindness matters and that even small people have value.<br data-end="6539" data-start="6536" />
Simple message: Being kind can matter more than being powerful.</p>
<p data-end="6790" data-start="6604"><strong data-end="6618" data-start="6604"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Goldilocks+story+summary&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">Goldilocks</a></strong><br data-end="6621" data-start="6618" />
Introduces boundaries, respect for other people’s spaces, and the idea that not everything is yours.<br data-end="6723" data-start="6720" />
Simple message: Some things are not for you, even if you want them.</p>
<p data-end="6974" data-start="6792"><strong data-end="6818" data-start="6792"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=The+Boy+Who+Cried+Wolf+story+summary&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">The Boy Who Cried Wolf</a></strong><br data-end="6821" data-start="6818" />
Teaches trust, honesty, and consequences in a way children understand.<br data-end="6893" data-start="6890" />
Simple message: If people stop believing you, help may not come when you need it.</p>
<p data-end="7126" data-start="6976"><strong data-end="7007" data-start="6976"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=The+Ant+and+the+Grasshopper+story+summary&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">The Ant and the Grasshopper</a></strong><br data-end="7010" data-start="7007" />
Encourages planning, balance, and thinking about the future you.<br data-end="7072" data-start="7069" />
Simple message: Enjoy yourself, but remember tomorrow.</p>
<p data-end="7283" data-start="7128"><strong data-end="7149" data-start="7128"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=The+Ugly+Duckling+story+summary&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">The Ugly Duckling</a></strong><br data-end="7152" data-start="7149" />
A powerful story for children who feel different or excluded.<br data-end="7216" data-start="7213" />
Simple message: Difference is not failure. Belonging can take time.</p>
<p data-end="7432" data-start="7285"><strong data-end="7310" data-start="7285"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=The+Three+Little+Pigs+story+summary&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">The Three Little Pigs</a></strong><br data-end="7313" data-start="7310" />
Shows why effort, preparation, and solid foundations matter.<br data-end="7375" data-start="7372" />
Simple message: Shortcuts feel good until they fall down.</p>
<p data-end="7617" data-start="7434"><strong data-end="7463" data-start="7434"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=The+Emperor%E2%80%99s+New+Clothes+story+summary&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3005267635055125968" target="_blank">The Emperor’s New Clothes</a></strong><br data-end="7466" data-start="7463" />
For older children and teens. Teaches critical thinking and speaking up.<br data-end="7541" data-start="7538" />
Simple message: If something does not make sense, you are allowed to say so.</p><p data-end="7617" data-start="7434"><br /></p>

<p data-end="7788" data-start="7657">Therapy has its place. It can be life-changing when used well. But it should supplement kind, boundaried parenting, not replace it.</p>
<p data-end="7952" data-start="7790">Stories are one of the oldest tools humans have for helping children grow steady. They cost nothing. They are flexible. They work across cultures and generations.</p>
<p data-end="8030" data-start="7954">And perhaps most importantly, they remind children of something very simple.</p>
