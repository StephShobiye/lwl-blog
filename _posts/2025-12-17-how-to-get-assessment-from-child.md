<p>&nbsp;</p><h1 data-end="440" data-start="357">How to get an assessment for your child from child disability social care (Wales)<br /><br /><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEhn-1g8iLmA_yWh-wVkbQr5VfGb9PzdiD_vXP26fjq1RToYdtlbmWJ9ZgrUiaHf58S0HdFfeqsoMUvM9BVyoT1QZ16Oz-Th3_bUZAARxUowrQ2XJ68IAAKHsqm2vGdEKyE8vJvpJD5-vEmmLNs6Lz9bvi6n8yJwHomOXRzY4S6yKsjqHP8hcTUegDu94RQb" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img alt="" data-original-height="700" data-original-width="970" height="231" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEhn-1g8iLmA_yWh-wVkbQr5VfGb9PzdiD_vXP26fjq1RToYdtlbmWJ9ZgrUiaHf58S0HdFfeqsoMUvM9BVyoT1QZ16Oz-Th3_bUZAARxUowrQ2XJ68IAAKHsqm2vGdEKyE8vJvpJD5-vEmmLNs6Lz9bvi6n8yJwHomOXRzY4S6yKsjqHP8hcTUegDu94RQb" width="320" /></a></div></h1><p data-end="739" data-start="442">Many parents know long before any service becomes involved that their child’s disability is affecting daily life. What is often unclear is <strong data-end="627" data-start="581">how to access <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=child+disability+social+care+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">child disability social care</a></strong>, what triggers an assessment, what support it can and cannot provide, and how long things are supposed to take.</p><p data-end="936" data-start="741">This guide explains how the system works in Wales, who can ask for an assessment, what ages are covered, how adaptations and support are arranged, and what happens as children approach adulthood.</p><p data-end="1007" data-start="938">The aim is clarity. Clear information early can prevent crisis later.</p><hr data-end="1012" data-start="1009" /><h2 data-end="1054" data-start="1014">What is child disability social care?</h2><p data-end="1289" data-start="1056">Child disability social care sits within your local authority’s children’s services. Its role is to assess whether a disabled child and their family need <strong data-end="1230" data-start="1210">care and support</strong> under the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Social+Services+and+Well-being+Wales+Act+2014&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">Social Services and Well-being (Wales) Act 2014</a>.</p><p data-end="1490" data-start="1291">It does not diagnose conditions and it does not replace education provision. Social care focuses on <strong data-end="1431" data-start="1391"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=how+disability+impacts+everyday+life+examples+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">how disability impacts everyday life</a></strong>, family wellbeing, safety, and the sustainability of care.</p><p data-end="1512" data-start="1492">Support may include:</p><ul data-end="1675" data-start="1513">
<li data-end="1538" data-start="1513">
<p data-end="1538" data-start="1515">short breaks or respite</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1567" data-start="1539">
<p data-end="1567" data-start="1541">practical help in the home</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1606" data-start="1568">
<p data-end="1606" data-start="1570">direct payments or a personal budget</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1632" data-start="1607">
<p data-end="1632" data-start="1609">family support services</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1675" data-start="1633">
<p data-end="1675" data-start="1635">planning for the transition to adulthood</p>
</li>
</ul><hr data-end="1680" data-start="1677" /><h2 data-end="1715" data-start="1682">Who can ask for an assessment?</h2><p data-end="1794" data-start="1717">In Wales, <strong data-end="1793" data-start="1727">any of the following can request a child disability assessment</strong>:</p><ul data-end="1957" data-start="1795">
<li data-end="1814" data-start="1795">
<p data-end="1814" data-start="1797">a parent or carer</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1861" data-start="1815">
<p data-end="1861" data-start="1817">a young person themselves, where appropriate</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1897" data-start="1862">
<p data-end="1897" data-start="1864">a <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=GP+health+professional+child+disability+assessment+Wales+role&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">GP or other health professional</a></p>
</li>
<li data-end="1917" data-start="1898">
<p data-end="1917" data-start="1900">a <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALNCO+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">school or ALNCO</a></p>
</li>
<li data-end="1957" data-start="1918">
<p data-end="1957" data-start="1920">another professional already involved</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="2093" data-start="1959">You do <strong data-end="1973" data-start="1966">not</strong> need a diagnosis first.<br data-end="2000" data-start="1997" />
You do <strong data-end="2014" data-start="2007">not</strong> need school permission.<br data-end="2041" data-start="2038" />
You do <strong data-end="2055" data-start="2048">not</strong> need to wait until things fall apart.</p><p data-end="2203" data-start="2095">If disability is affecting your child’s wellbeing or your family’s ability to cope, you are entitled to ask.</p><hr data-end="2208" data-start="2205" /><h2 data-end="2241" data-start="2210">How to request an assessment</h2><p data-end="2304" data-start="2243">Contact your local authority children’s services and ask for:</p><blockquote data-end="2415" data-start="2305">
<p data-end="2415" data-start="2307">a <strong data-end="2361" data-start="2309"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=care+and+support+assessment+disabled+child+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">care and support assessment for a disabled child</a></strong> under the Social Services and Well-being (Wales) Act.</p>
</blockquote><p data-end="2530" data-start="2417">You can do this by phone, online form, or email. If writing, keep it factual and focus on <strong data-end="2517" data-start="2507">impact</strong>, not labels.</p><p data-end="2557" data-start="2532">Helpful examples include:</p><ul data-end="2803" data-start="2558">
<li data-end="2598" data-start="2558">
<p data-end="2598" data-start="2560">constant supervision needed for safety</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2636" data-start="2599">
<p data-end="2636" data-start="2601">inability to leave the house safely</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2683" data-start="2637">
<p data-end="2683" data-start="2639">severe sleep disruption affecting the family</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2728" data-start="2684">
<p data-end="2728" data-start="2686">distress at school spilling into home life</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2761" data-start="2729">
<p data-end="2761" data-start="2731">significant impact on siblings</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2803" data-start="2762">
<p data-end="2803" data-start="2764">parental exhaustion affecting wellbeing</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="2863" data-start="2805">This is not the moment to minimise or put on a brave face.</p><hr data-end="2868" data-start="2865" /><h2 data-end="2891" data-start="2870">What happens next?</h2><p data-end="2917" data-start="2893">The local authority may:</p><ul data-end="3033" data-start="2918">
<li data-end="2947" data-start="2918">
<p data-end="2947" data-start="2920">carry out initial screening</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2973" data-start="2948">
<p data-end="2973" data-start="2950">ask follow-up questions</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2991" data-start="2974">
<p data-end="2991" data-start="2976">request reports</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3033" data-start="2992">
<p data-end="3033" data-start="2994">speak to school or health professionals</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="3106" data-start="3035">If needs appear significant, this should move to a <strong data-end="3105" data-start="3086">full assessment</strong>.</p><p data-end="3143" data-start="3108">A full assessment usually involves:</p><ul data-end="3302" data-start="3144">
<li data-end="3171" data-start="3144">
<p data-end="3171" data-start="3146">discussing daily routines</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3208" data-start="3172">
<p data-end="3208" data-start="3174">understanding what is hard and why</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3255" data-start="3209">
<p data-end="3255" data-start="3211">identifying what support is already in place</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3302" data-start="3256">
<p data-end="3302" data-start="3258">exploring what would reduce risk or pressure</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="3325" data-start="3304">Outcomes may include:</p><ul data-end="3463" data-start="3326">
<li data-end="3362" data-start="3326">
<p data-end="3362" data-start="3328">no further action with signposting</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3383" data-start="3363">
<p data-end="3383" data-start="3365">short-term support</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3402" data-start="3384">
<p data-end="3402" data-start="3386">ongoing services</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3441" data-start="3403">
<p data-end="3441" data-start="3405">direct payments or a personal budget</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3463" data-start="3442">
<p data-end="3463" data-start="3444">transition planning</p>
</li>
</ul><hr data-end="3468" data-start="3465" /><h2 data-end="3525" data-start="3470">Timescales: what parents should realistically expect</h2><p data-end="3561" data-start="3527">Waiting is often the hardest part.</p><p data-end="3814" data-start="3563">In Wales, there are <strong data-end="3615" data-start="3583"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=child+disability+social+care+assessment+deadlines+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">no fixed statutory deadlines</a></strong> for child disability social care assessments. The law requires local authorities to act <strong data-end="3716" data-start="3704">promptly</strong>, <strong data-end="3737" data-start="3718">proportionately</strong>, and <strong data-end="3776" data-start="3743">within a reasonable timescale</strong>, based on the level of need and risk.</p><p data-end="3944" data-start="3816">This flexibility exists so that urgent situations can be addressed quickly and more complex situations can be assessed properly.</p><p data-end="4018" data-start="3946">In practice, many local authorities work to internal benchmarks such as:</p><ul data-end="4126" data-start="4019">
<li data-end="4057" data-start="4019">
<p data-end="4057" data-start="4021">initial screening within a few weeks</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4126" data-start="4058">
<p data-end="4126" data-start="4060">full assessment completed over several further weeks once accepted</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="4228" data-start="4128">These are <strong data-end="4163" data-start="4138">practice expectations</strong>, not legal guarantees. What matters is whether the authority is:</p><ul data-end="4319" data-start="4229">
<li data-end="4266" data-start="4229">
<p data-end="4266" data-start="4231">actively progressing the assessment</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4291" data-start="4267">
<p data-end="4291" data-start="4269">communicating with you</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4319" data-start="4292">
<p data-end="4319" data-start="4294">able to explain any delay</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="4452" data-start="4321">Where needs are urgent or family wellbeing is deteriorating, support can be put in place <strong data-end="4451" data-start="4410">before a full assessment is completed</strong>.</p><hr data-end="4457" data-start="4454" /><h2 data-end="4521" data-start="4459">Personal budgets and direct payments: managing expectations</h2><p data-end="4647" data-start="4523">A personal budget is <strong data-end="4564" data-start="4544">not compensation</strong> and not an open-ended fund. It exists to meet <strong data-end="4646" data-start="4611">assessed care and support needs</strong>.</p><p data-end="4662" data-start="4649">It may cover:</p><ul data-end="4805" data-start="4663">
<li data-end="4688" data-start="4663">
<p data-end="4688" data-start="4665">short breaks or respite</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4721" data-start="4689">
<p data-end="4721" data-start="4691">support workers for care needs</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4764" data-start="4722">
<p data-end="4764" data-start="4724">activities linked to wellbeing or safety</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4805" data-start="4765">
<p data-end="4805" data-start="4767">support that helps sustain family life</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="4833" data-start="4807">It will not usually cover:</p><ul data-end="4983" data-start="4834">
<li data-end="4851" data-start="4834">
<p data-end="4851" data-start="4836">education costs</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4893" data-start="4852">
<p data-end="4893" data-start="4854">private therapy replacing NHS provision</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4920" data-start="4894">
<p data-end="4920" data-start="4896">school tuition or tutors</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4946" data-start="4921">
<p data-end="4946" data-start="4923">general household costs</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4983" data-start="4947">
<p data-end="4983" data-start="4949">support unrelated to assessed need</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="5171" data-start="4985">Social care must be able to show that any funded support meets identified need and represents appropriate use of public funds. Clear expectations early help prevent disappointment later.</p><hr data-end="5176" data-start="5173" /><h2 style="text-align: left;">What social care cannot do (and why this matters)</h2><h2 data-end="5222" data-start="5178">
<p data-end="558" data-start="487" style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">Being clear about limits can prevent frustration and conflict later on.</span></span></p>
<p data-end="600" data-start="560" style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">Child disability social care <span data-end="599" data-start="589">cannot</span>:</span></span></p>
<ul data-end="861" data-start="601" style="text-align: left;">
<li data-end="647" data-start="601">
<p data-end="647" data-start="603"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">diagnose medical or developmental conditions</span></span></p>
</li>
<li data-end="677" data-start="648">
<p data-end="677" data-start="650"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">replace NHS health services</span></span></p>
</li>
<li data-end="730" data-start="678">
<p data-end="730" data-start="680"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">fund education, tuition, or school-based provision</span></span></p>
</li>
<li data-end="781" data-start="731">
<p data-end="781" data-start="733"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">provide private therapy in place of NHS pathways</span></span></p>
</li>
<li data-end="813" data-start="782">
<p data-end="813" data-start="784"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">resolve disputes with schools</span></span></p>
</li>
<li data-end="861" data-start="814">
<p data-end="861" data-start="816"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">act as an enforcement body for other services</span></span></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="1008" data-start="863" style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">Social care’s role is to assess <span data-end="921" data-start="895">care and support needs</span> and consider what support may reduce risk, sustain family life, and promote wellbeing.</span></span></p>
<p data-end="1141" data-start="1010" style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">When expectations are aligned early, assessments are usually more constructive and less emotionally draining for everyone involved.</span></span></p>_______________________________________________________<br /><br />Home adaptations and specialist equipment</h2><p data-end="5330" data-start="5224">Home adaptations and specialist equipment are arranged through a <strong data-end="5329" data-start="5289"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=joint+health+and+social+care+process+child+disability+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">joint health and social care process</a></strong>.</p><p data-end="5508" data-start="5332">Children’s <strong data-end="5372" data-start="5343">Occupational Therapy (OT)</strong> assesses the child and identifies <strong data-end="5459" data-start="5407">what adaptations or equipment are needed and why</strong>, based on safety, access, and daily functioning.</p><p data-end="5646" data-start="5510">Once OT recommendations are made, <strong data-end="5600" data-start="5544"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=social+care+responsible+funding+arranging+equipment+adaptations+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">social care is responsible for funding and arranging</a></strong> the equipment, adaptations, or building work.</p><p data-end="5803" data-start="5648">Parents can usually <strong data-end="5699" data-start="5668"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=how+to+self+refer+children%27s+OT+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">self-refer to children’s OT</a></strong> via their local health board website. OT determines need. Social care delivers the practical provision.</p><hr data-end="5808" data-start="5805" /><h2 data-end="5863" data-start="5810">Education and transport: where responsibility sits</h2><p data-end="5907" data-start="5865">Transport is a common source of confusion.</p><ul data-end="6256" data-start="5909">
<li data-end="6009" data-start="5909">
<p data-end="6009" data-start="5911"><strong data-end="5927" data-start="5911">Up to age 16</strong>, school transport is usually managed by the local authority education department.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6043" data-start="6010">
<p data-end="6043" data-start="6012"><strong data-end="6023" data-start="6012">Post-16</strong>, arrangements vary.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6185" data-start="6044">
<p data-end="6185" data-start="6046">Where a young person has ongoing care needs, <strong data-end="6149" data-start="6091"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=adult+social+care+transport+disabled+young+adult+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">adult social care may become responsible for transport</a></strong> as part of a care and support plan.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6256" data-start="6186">
<p data-end="6256" data-start="6188">College transport is often assessed separately and is not automatic.</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="6311" data-start="6258">As your child approaches 16, it is reasonable to ask:</p><ul data-end="6430" data-start="6312">
<li data-end="6346" data-start="6312">
<p data-end="6346" data-start="6314">who is responsible for transport</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6376" data-start="6347">
<p data-end="6376" data-start="6349">when responsibility changes</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6430" data-start="6377">
<p data-end="6430" data-start="6379">how arrangements will be reviewed during transition</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="6492" data-start="6432">Do not assume provision will roll over without reassessment.</p><hr data-end="6497" data-start="6494" /><h2 data-end="6542" data-start="6499">Ages and transition to adult social care</h2><ul data-end="6729" data-start="6544">
<li data-end="6607" data-start="6544">
<p data-end="6607" data-start="6546">Child disability social care generally covers <strong data-end="6607" data-start="6592"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=child+disability+social+care+age+range+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">birth to 18</a></strong></p>
</li>
<li data-end="6660" data-start="6608">
<p data-end="6660" data-start="6610">Transition planning should begin <strong data-end="6660" data-start="6643"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=disability+services+Wales+transition+planning+age+14&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">around age 14</a></strong></p>
</li>
<li data-end="6729" data-start="6661">
<p data-end="6729" data-start="6663">Adult social care assessments usually happen <strong data-end="6729" data-start="6708"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=disability+services+Wales+transition+to+adult+services+age+16-18&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">between 16 and 18</a></strong></p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="6746" data-start="6731">Adult services:</p><ul data-end="6881" data-start="6747">
<li data-end="6793" data-start="6747">
<p data-end="6793" data-start="6749">focus more on the individual than the family</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6832" data-start="6794">
<p data-end="6832" data-start="6796">apply different eligibility criteria</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6881" data-start="6833">
<p data-end="6881" data-start="6835">often feel more functional and less relational</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="6917" data-start="6883">Early planning helps prevent gaps.</p><hr data-end="6922" data-start="6919" /><h2 data-end="6981" data-start="6924">Team Around the Family (TAF): what parents should know</h2><p data-end="7129" data-start="6983">A Team Around the Family meeting is intended to coordinate support and prevent escalation. It is <strong data-end="7128" data-start="7080"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Team+Around+the+Family+Wales+child+protection&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1456573195848058056" target="_blank">not automatically a child protection process</a></strong>.</p><p data-end="7188" data-start="7131">However, it is a point where parents should be attentive.</p><p data-end="7214" data-start="7190">Parents are entitled to:</p><ul data-end="7373" data-start="7215">
<li data-end="7247" data-start="7215">
<p data-end="7247" data-start="7217">know why a TAF is being called</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7264" data-start="7248">
<p data-end="7264" data-start="7250">see the agenda</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7311" data-start="7265">

</li>

</li>
</ul><p data-end="7564" data-start="7375">If the focus remains on support and unmet need, TAF is working as intended. If language shifts towards monitoring or parental blame, it is reasonable to ask for clarity and to keep records.</p><hr data-end="7569" data-start="7566" /><h2 data-end="7610" data-start="7571">If things stall: what parents can do</h2><p data-end="7733" data-start="7612">If progress feels slow, follow up calmly. <li data-end="7781" data-start="7748">
<p data-end="7781" data-start="7750">what stage the assessment is at</p>

<li data-end="7815" data-start="7782">
<p data-end="7815" data-start="7784">what information is outstanding</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7853" data-start="7816">
<p data-end="7853" data-start="7818">who the allocated worker or team is</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7891" data-start="7854">
<p data-end="7891" data-start="7856">when the situation will be reviewed</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="7949" data-start="7893">A brief written follow-up is often helpful. For example:</p><blockquote data-end="8166" data-start="7951">
<p data-end="8166" data-start="7953">I am checking in to understand where we are in the assessment process and what the next steps and likely timescales are. Our child’s needs remain significant and we want to ensure matters continue to move forward.</p>

assessments</h2><h2 data-end="9504" data-start="9484">
<p data-end="1386" data-start="1274" style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">When a child has significant additional needs, parents and carers are often carrying far more than they realise.</span></span></p>
<p data-end="1564" data-start="1388" style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">Under Welsh law, <span data-end="1481" data-start="1405">parents and carers are also entitled to an assessment in their own right</span> if caring is affecting their wellbeing. This is known as a <span data-end="1563" data-start="1541">carer’s assessment</span>.</span></span></p>
<p data-end="1596" data-start="1566" style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">A carer’s assessment looks at:</span></span></p>
<ul data-end="1737" data-start="1597" style="text-align: left;">
<li data-end="1625" data-start="1597">
<p data-end="1625" data-start="1599"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">physical and mental health</span></span></p>
</li>
<li data-end="1648" data-start="1626">
<p data-end="1648" data-start="1628"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">exhaustion and sleep</span></span></p>
</li>
<li data-end="1675" data-start="1649">
<p data-end="1675" data-start="1651"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">ability to work or study</span></span></p>
</li>
<li data-end="1701" data-start="1676">
<p data-end="1701" data-start="1678"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">impact on relationships</span></span></p>
</li>
<li data-end="1737" data-start="1702">
<p data-end="1737" data-start="1704"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">sustainability of the caring role</span></span></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="1835" data-start="1739" style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">You do not need to be at breaking point to ask. In fact, asking earlier can help prevent crisis.</span></span></p>
<p data-end="2020" data-start="1837" style="text-align: left;"><span style="font-weight: normal;"><span style="font-size: small;">Carer support may overlap with child support, but it can also stand alone. It is reasonable to ask whether a carer’s assessment would be appropriate alongside your child’s assessment.</span></span></p>______________________________________________________<br /><br /><br />Final reassurance</h2><p data-end="9724" data-start="9506">Requesting a child disability social care assessment is not a failure and not an accusation. It is about recognising when disability affects family life to the point where <strong data-end="9723" data-start="9678">early support can prevent crisis later on</strong>.</p><p data-end="8823" data-start="8729">
