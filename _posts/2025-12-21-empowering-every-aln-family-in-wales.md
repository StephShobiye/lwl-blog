dark:prose-invert w-full break-words light deep-research-result markdown-new-styling"><h1 data-end="62" data-start="0">Empowering Every ALN Family in Wales: Beyond Autism and ADHD</h1>
<p data-end="772" data-start="64">For families navigating <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Wales+ALN+Additional+Learning+Needs+system&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Wales’s ALN (Additional Learning Needs) system</a>, getting the right support can feel overwhelming – especially if your child’s needs are less understood.  Although the ALN reforms (Wales) emphasize a <em data-end="299" data-start="286">“needs led”</em> approach<span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://research.senedd.wales/research-articles/the-growing-demand-for-adhd-and-autism-assessments-in-wales/#:~:text=away%20from%20achieving%20a%20needs,2024%20came%20to%20the%20conclusion" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://research.senedd.wales/research-articles/the-growing-demand-for-adhd-and-autism-assessments-in-wales/#:~:text=away%20from%20achieving%20a%20needs,2024%20came%20to%20the%20conclusion" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">research.senedd.wales</span></a></span></span>, we often hear that help only comes after familiar diagnoses like <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=autism&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">autism</a> or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ADHD&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">ADHD</a> are given.  Many families of children with <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Dyspraxia&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Dyspraxia</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Cerebral+Palsy&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Cerebral Palsy</a> or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=rare+genetic+conditions+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">rare genetic conditions</a> find themselves overlooked.  Here at <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Learn+Without+Limits+CIC&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Learn Without Limits CIC</a>, we know how isolating that can feel.  We are a parent‑led community interest company, and our mission is to support <em data-end="709" data-start="702">every</em> ALN family in Wales, ensuring no one is left on the sidelines.</p>
<h2 data-end="819" data-start="774">Welsh ALN Reforms and Neurodiversity Focus</h2>
<p data-end="1564" data-start="821">In practice, however, autism and ADHD often receive the lion’s share of attention and resources.  For example, the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Welsh+Government&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Welsh Government</a> recently announced <strong data-end="1026" data-start="1009">£13.7 million</strong> to cut waiting times for autism and ADHD assessments<span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://www.gov.wales/137-million-transform-services-and-cut-adhd-and-autism-waiting-times#:~:text=A%20further%20%C2%A313,and%20ADHD%20assessments%20across%20Wales" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://www.gov.wales/137-million-transform-services-and-cut-adhd-and-autism-waiting-times#:~:text=A%20further%20%C2%A313,and%20ADHD%20assessments%20across%20Wales" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">gov.wales</span></a></span></span>.  A <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Welsh+Government+ALN+survey&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Welsh Government survey</a> also found that around half of ALN learners were reported as autistic and about one-third had ADHD, while only “smaller proportions” of families mentioned conditions like dyspraxia or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=physical+impairments+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">physical impairments</a><span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://www.gov.wales/sites/default/files/statistics-and-research/2025-10/evaluation-additional-learning-needs-system-survey-parents-and-carers.pdf#:~:text=%E2%80%A2%20Autism%20or%20Autistic%20Spectrum,was%20the%20second%20most%20frequently" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://www.gov.wales/sites/default/files/statistics-and-research/2025-10/evaluation-additional-learning-needs-system-survey-parents-and-carers.pdf#:~:text=%E2%80%A2%20Autism%20or%20Autistic%20Spectrum,was%20the%20second%20most%20frequently" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">gov.wales</span></a></span></span><span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://www.gov.wales/sites/default/files/statistics-and-research/2025-10/evaluation-additional-learning-needs-system-survey-parents-and-carers.pdf#:~:text=%E2%80%A2%20Smaller%20proportions%20of%20respondents,or%20visual%20impairments%2C%20and%20dyscalculia" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://www.gov.wales/sites/default/files/statistics-and-research/2025-10/evaluation-additional-learning-needs-system-survey-parents-and-carers.pdf#:~:text=%E2%80%A2%20Smaller%20proportions%20of%20respondents,or%20visual%20impairments%2C%20and%20dyscalculia" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">gov.wales</span></a></span></span>.  These figures underline how the system tends to centre on well‑known neurodevelopmental needs, leaving other conditions under the radar.</p>
<h2 data-end="1628" data-start="1566">Overlooked ALN Conditions: Dyspraxia, CP and Rare Disorders</h2>
<p data-end="2432" data-start="1630">For many parents, it feels like only certain labels matter.  Conditions that are more visible or widely publicised often attract more funding and sympathy.  For example, the Welsh Government’s recent <strong data-end="1850" data-start="1830">£20m ALN funding</strong> was ring‑fenced for building and updating <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=sensory+rooms+images&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">sensory rooms</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=specialist+equipment+ALN+children&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">specialist equipment</a> and classrooms<span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://www.childcomwales.org.uk/our-work/policy-positions/aln-policy-position/#:~:text=In%20May%202024%20the%20Welsh,through%20the%20medium%20of%20Welsh" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://www.childcomwales.org.uk/our-work/policy-positions/aln-policy-position/#:~:text=In%20May%202024%20the%20Welsh,through%20the%20medium%20of%20Welsh" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">childcomwales.org.uk</span></a></span></span> – upgrades that directly benefit pupils with sensory sensitivities or obvious physical disabilities.  Meanwhile, families of children with dyspraxia, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=dyscalculia&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">dyscalculia</a> or rare genetic syndromes often have little tailored support in school.  They may face long waits for a diagnosis or therapy in rural areas, and struggle to find resources geared to their child’s needs.  We believe every ALN need deserves attention – whether or not it makes the headlines.</p>
<h2 data-end="2468" data-start="2434">Diagnosis Delays and Rural Gaps</h2>
<p data-end="3502" data-start="2470">Long waits for assessment are a Wales‑wide problem.  For example, by mid‑2023 <strong data-end="2567" data-start="2548"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=67%25+children+autism+ADHD+waiting+lists+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">67% of children</a></strong> referred for an autism or ADHD assessment had already been waiting more than 26 weeks<span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://nation.cymru/news/long-waits-for-autism-assessments-in-wales-leave-thousands-without-answers/#:~:text=According%20to%20Senedd%20Research%2C%2067,been%20waiting%20over%20a%20year" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://nation.cymru/news/long-waits-for-autism-assessments-in-wales-leave-thousands-without-answers/#:~:text=According%20to%20Senedd%20Research%2C%2067,been%20waiting%20over%20a%20year" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">nation.cymru</span></a></span></span>.  In more rural health boards, it was even worse: <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Hywel+Dda+health+board+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Hywel Dda</a> (serving <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Carmarthenshire+ALN+support&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Carmarthenshire</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Pembrokeshire+ALN+support&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Pembrokeshire</a> and <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Ceredigion+ALN+support&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Ceredigion</a>) had an <em data-end="2840" data-start="2814">average wait of <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=75+weeks+ALN+assessment+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">75 weeks</a></em> for a neurodevelopmental assessment<span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://nation.cymru/news/long-waits-for-autism-assessments-in-wales-leave-thousands-without-answers/#:~:text=Waiting%20times%20vary%20dramatically%20between,of%20the%2031st%20March%202024" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://nation.cymru/news/long-waits-for-autism-assessments-in-wales-leave-thousands-without-answers/#:~:text=Waiting%20times%20vary%20dramatically%20between,of%20the%2031st%20March%202024" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">nation.cymru</span></a></span></span>.  This postcode‑by‑postcode variation can be stark.  The Senedd’s Disabled Children’s Committee described Welsh provision as <em data-end="3066" data-start="3039">“patchy and inconsistent”</em>, a <em data-end="3090" data-start="3070">“<a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=postcode+lottery+ALN+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">postcode lottery</a>”</em> for disabled children<span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://www.educationlawadvice.com/2024/09/02/children-aln-disabilities-missing-wales/#:~:text=young%20people%20and%20their%20families%2C,their%20families%20in%20their%20area%E2%80%A6" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://www.educationlawadvice.com/2024/09/02/children-aln-disabilities-missing-wales/#:~:text=young%20people%20and%20their%20families%2C,their%20families%20in%20their%20area%E2%80%A6" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">educationlawadvice.com</span></a></span></span>. The law says support should start based on need, not a label<span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://research.senedd.wales/research-articles/the-growing-demand-for-adhd-and-autism-assessments-in-wales/#:~:text=away%20from%20achieving%20a%20needs,2024%20came%20to%20the%20conclusion" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://research.senedd.wales/research-articles/the-growing-demand-for-adhd-and-autism-assessments-in-wales/#:~:text=away%20from%20achieving%20a%20needs,2024%20came%20to%20the%20conclusion" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">research.senedd.wales</span></a></span></span>, but in reality families often must fight for every bit of help.</p>
<h2 data-end="3561" data-start="3504">Learn Without Limits CIC: Support for Every ALN Family</h2>
<p data-end="4233" data-start="3563">At Learn Without Limits, we welcome <em data-end="3645" data-start="3638">every</em> ALN family and condition.  Our vision is that <strong data-end="3775" data-start="3692">“Wales becomes the best place to grow up if you’re autistic, ADHD, or disabled”</strong><span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://learnwithoutlimitscic.org/index.en.html#:~:text=Wales%20becomes%20the%20best%20place,decisions%2C%20and%20a%20strong%20community" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://learnwithoutlimitscic.org/index.en.html#:~:text=Wales%20becomes%20the%20best%20place,decisions%2C%20and%20a%20strong%20community" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">learnwithoutlimitscic.org</span></a></span></span> – and by “disabled” we include children with dyspraxia, cerebral palsy, rare syndromes or any condition that affects learning.  We understand families in these communities often feel left out, so we work to fill that gap.  We provide clear guidance on the ALN law and <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=IDPs+ALN+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">IDPs</a>, peer mentoring and support, plus friendly online Q&amp;As and local meet‑ups – anything that helps you and your child get the support you deserve.</p>
<p data-end="4728" data-start="4235">In addition, we’ve built a free <strong data-end="4287" data-start="4267"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Learn+Without+Limits+Parent+Guide+App&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Parent Guide App</a></strong> as a practical toolkit for ALN families.  It offers printable IDP and letter templates, straightforward FAQs on <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Welsh+ALN+law&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1889774197062418528" target="_blank">Welsh ALN law</a>, and links to community support and wellbeing resources<span data-state="closed"><span class="ms-1 inline-flex max-w-full items-center relative top-[-0.094rem] animate-[show_150ms_ease-in]" data-testid="webpage-citation-pill"><a alt="https://learnwithoutlimitscic.org/app.en.html#:~:text=What%E2%80%99s%20inside" class="flex h-4.5 overflow-hidden rounded-xl px-2 text-[9px] font-medium transition-colors duration-150 ease-in-out text-token-text-secondary! bg-[#F4F4F4]! dark:bg-[#303030]!" href="https://learnwithoutlimitscic.org/app.en.html#:~:text=What%E2%80%99s%20inside" rel="noopener" target="_blank"><span class="relative start-0 bottom-0 flex h-full w-full items-center">learnwithoutlimitscic.org</span></a></span></span>.  It’s built on the real questions parents ask us, and we keep improving it with your feedback.  Whether or not your child’s condition is well-known, Learn Without Limits is here for your family – every step of the way.</p>
