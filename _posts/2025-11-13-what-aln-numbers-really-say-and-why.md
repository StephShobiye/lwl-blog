<p data-end="268" data-start="79">In <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Welsh+government+ALN+funding+October+2025&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">October 2025</a>, the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Welsh+Government+ALN+policy&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Welsh Government</a> announced new funding and measures to strengthen support for children and young people with Additional Learning Needs (ALN). The announcement included:</p>
<ul data-end="546" data-start="270">
<li data-end="353" data-start="270">
<p data-end="353" data-start="272">¬£8.2 million in additional funding for local authorities, schools, and colleges</p>
</li>
<li data-end="409" data-start="354">
<p data-end="409" data-start="356">A new national <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Parent+and+Carer+Information+Toolkit+Wales+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Parent and Carer Information Toolkit</a></p>
</li>
<li data-end="472" data-start="410">
<p data-end="472" data-start="412">Guidance to make ALN delivery more consistent across Wales</p>
</li>
<li data-end="546" data-start="473">
<p data-end="546" data-start="475">Promises of closer working between education, health, and social care</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="751" data-start="548">At <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Learn+Without+Limits+CIC&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Learn Without Limits CIC</a>, we welcome any steps towards improving outcomes. But we also ask the tough questions: will this change anything for the families living on the sharp edge of the system today?</p>
<h2 data-end="777" data-start="753">The Numbers Don‚Äôt Lie</h2>
<p data-end="1133" data-start="779">When the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+Act+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">ALN Act</a> was introduced, projections suggested around 22% of Welsh pupils might be identified as having ALN. But in practice, identification under the new <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Individual+Development+Plan+IDP+system+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Individual Development Plan (IDP) system</a> has been far lower than expected. Many children with genuine needs have been labelled as being part of <em data-end="1107" data-start="1086"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+Universal+Provision+Wales+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Universal Provision</a></em> without tailored support.</p>
<p data-end="1452" data-start="1135">Under the old <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Special+Educational+Needs+SEN+system+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Special Educational Needs (SEN) system</a>, around 1.5% of learners had a <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Statement+SEN+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Statement</a>, but up to 20% more received help under <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=School+Action+Special+Educational+Needs&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">School Action</a> or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=School+Action+Plus+Special+Educational+Needs&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">School Action Plus</a>. Those categories were abolished in Wales. Today, if your child doesn‚Äôt meet the threshold for an IDP, they may fall through the cracks completely.</p>
<p data-end="1679" data-start="1454">Meanwhile, demand for <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=local+authority+funded+home+learning+programmes+Wales+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">local authority-funded home learning programmes</a> is rising rapidly. This is not because families want to leave mainstream provision, but because they feel forced out by the inadequacy of what‚Äôs available.</p>
<h2 data-end="1709" data-start="1681">Are Families Being Heard?</h2>
<p data-end="2092" data-start="1711">The proposed <strong data-end="1742" data-start="1724"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Parent+Toolkit+Wales+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Parent Toolkit</a></strong> is being framed as a new way to explain families‚Äô rights and what support to expect. However, few parents even knew the toolkit was being developed. At Learn Without Limits CIC, we only heard about it this month, despite being deeply embedded in the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Welsh+ALN+community+resources&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Welsh ALN community</a>. This suggests the toolkit was created without meaningful parental involvement.</p>
<p data-end="2500" data-start="2094">The truth is that families are not opting out of mainstream; they‚Äôre being pushed out. Children with <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=long+COVID&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">long COVID</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=rare+genetic+conditions+impact+on+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">rare genetic conditions</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=cerebral+palsy+support+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">cerebral palsy</a>, or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=dyspraxia+support+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">dyspraxia</a> often struggle to access specialist provision that fits their needs. Some young people never even make it through the door: <em data-end="2403" data-start="2385"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=fitness+to+study+policies+further+education+colleges&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">fitness to study</a></em> policies at some <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=further+education+FE+colleges+ALN+support&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">further education (FE) colleges</a> effectively bar them from continuing education.</p>
<h2 data-end="2539" data-start="2502">Mental Health and Missed Referrals</h2>
<p data-end="2877" data-start="2541">The <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=mental+health+crisis+young+people+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">mental health crisis</a> among young people in Wales is growing. Yet <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=CAMHS+Wales+waiting+times&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">CAMHS</a> waiting times remain untenable. Our own community has seen children referred to the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Integrated+Autism+Service+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Integrated Autism Service</a> at 16 still waiting at 21. <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=NHS+dental+waiting+lists+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">NHS dental waiting lists</a> have stretched so long that children wait until adulthood for care requested during primary school.</p>
<p data-end="3110" data-start="2879">In areas like <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Swansea+ambulance+waiting+times&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Swansea</a>, the situation is dire: ambulances reportedly take up to 16 hours to respond to medical emergencies. When health collapses, education follows. If we don‚Äôt fix the basics, no toolkit will paper over the cracks.</p>
<p data-end="3396" data-start="3112">The slow erosion of agreements, such as having school nurses offer a route to <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=neurodevelopmental+referrals+process+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">neurodevelopmental referrals</a>, is especially worrying. These ‚Äúmission creep‚Äù changes are happening piecemeal across local authorities and health boards, and without parent pushback, they will quietly spread.</p>
<h2 data-end="3436" data-start="3398">The Trap of the ‚ÄúForever Volunteer‚Äù</h2>
<p data-end="3827" data-start="3438">Meanwhile, young people with ALN and lifelong conditions are expected to be grateful for being endlessly <em data-end="3554" data-start="3543">supported</em>, yet are rarely empowered. Agencies like <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Maximus+employability+support+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">Maximus</a> and some further education (FE) colleges receive public funds to <em data-end="3678" data-start="3669">prepare</em> young people for work. However, many are kept in endless cycles of <em data-end="3769" data-start="3746"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=employability+support+programs+Wales+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">employability support</a></em> without actually being helped into meaningful employment.</p>
<p data-end="4129" data-start="3829">For disabled young people and long-term carers trying to re-enter the workforce, the reality is a life stuck in unpaid roles that benefit providers more than participants. This dependency model needs to be broken. ALN youth want autonomy and self-determination, not more paperwork and empty meetings.</p>
<h2 data-end="4176" data-start="4131">What Makes Learn Without Limits Different?</h2>
<p data-end="4361" data-start="4178">We‚Äôre not waiting for top-down consultations. We‚Äôre building tools now, with and for families, to give them what they need to navigate the ALN, health, and education systems in Wales.</p>
<p data-end="4918" data-start="4363">Our <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+Parent+Guide+App+Learn+Without+Limits&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">ALN Parent Guide App</a> is built by those who use the system, and it evolves with the lived experience of parents, carers, and young people. For example, the Welsh-language translation of our website was completed by a 19-year-old autistic team member, and our content is shaped weekly by the questions and concerns raised in our online community. We‚Äôre covering the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=25%2B+cliff+edge+disability+resources+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">25+ cliff edge</a>, Elective Home Education (EHE) re-entry, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=rare+and+invisible+conditions+support+groups&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">rare and invisible conditions</a>, and <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=realistic+mental+health+pathways+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3623321288885348753" target="_blank">realistic mental health pathways</a>. Every app release responds to what families tell us they need.</p>
<p data-end="5476" data-start="4920"><strong data-end="5012" data-start="4920">The Learn Without Limits ALN Parent Guide App is truly designed by parents, for parents.</strong> Some of our contributors are parents with ALN or disabilities themselves, bringing personal insight into the challenges families face. Teens and young adults with lived experience also contribute; notably, the Welsh-language translation of our website was completed by a 19-year-old autistic team member. This approach, grounded in lived experience, ensures that the app‚Äôs content remains relevant, authentic, and genuinely supportive for families navigating ALN.</p>
