<p data-end="983" data-start="731">Across Wales, thousands of unpaid carers shoulder the emotional, practical and financial responsibility of supporting children with <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+Additional+Learning+Needs&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=5946062721820493673" target="_blank">Additional Learning Needs</a>. <p data-end="1126" data-start="985">üìå <strong data-end="1126" data-start="988">Most of the system‚Äôs problems happen not because people do not care, but because the people who do care are not talking to each other.</strong></p>
<p data-end="1325" data-start="1128">When communication breaks down between schools, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+teams&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=5946062721820493673" target="_blank">ALN teams</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=CAMHS&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=5946062721820493673" target="_blank">CAMHS</a>, social care, transport, therapies, health boards, and local authorities, unpaid carers become the only ones holding it all together.</p>
<p data-end="1354" data-start="1327">And the weight is enormous.</p>

<h1 data-end="2030" data-start="1982"><strong data-end="2030" data-start="1984">How Communication Failures Impact Families</strong></h1>
<h2 data-end="2083" data-start="2032"><strong data-end="2081" data-start="2035">Parents become the ‚Äúkey worker‚Äù by default</strong></h2>
<p data-end="2130" data-start="2084">Families tell us the same thing over and over:</p>
<blockquote data-end="2174" data-start="2132">
<p data-end="2174" data-start="2134">‚ÄúIf I do not chase it, nothing happens.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote>
<p data-end="2325" data-start="2176">Parents are left tracking:<br data-end="2205" data-start="2202" />
üß© who said what<br data-end="2224" data-start="2221" />
üß© when it was said<br data-end="2246" data-start="2243" />

üß© who is missing from the chain</p>
<p data-end="2367" data-start="2327">This is unpaid labour, and a lot of it.</p>
<h2 data-end="2420" data-start="2369"><strong data-end="2418" data-start="2372">Decisions are made without parents present</strong></h2>
<p data-end="2453" data-start="2421">Carers frequently discover that:</p>
<ul data-end="2667" data-start="2455">
<li data-end="2514" data-start="2455">
<p data-end="2514" data-start="2457">decisions were made at meetings they weren‚Äôt invited to</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2559" data-start="2515">
<p data-end="2559" data-start="2517">schools assumed health would update them</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2613" data-start="2560">
<p data-end="2613" data-start="2562">health assumed education had spoken to the family</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2667" data-start="2614">
<p data-end="2667" data-start="2616">the LA assumed school had communicated everything</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="2707" data-start="2669">This deepens anxiety and erodes trust.</p>
<h2 data-end="2739" data-start="2709"><strong data-end="2737" data-start="2712">Crisis becomes normal</strong></h2>
<p data-end="2890" data-start="2740">When nothing is coordinated until the child is already in distress, families live permanently braced for the next emergency appointment or phone call.</p>
<p data-end="2926" data-start="2892">Chronic stress becomes normalised.</p>

<h1 data-end="2990" data-start="2933">‚≠ê <strong data-end="2990" data-start="2937">A Practical Toolkit for Parents and Unpaid Carers</strong></h1>

<h2 data-end="3155" data-start="3098"><strong data-end="3153" data-start="3101">1. Never make a phone call when an email will do</strong></h2>
<p data-end="3193" data-start="3156">Phone calls disappear. Emails do not.</p>
<p data-end="3233" data-start="3195">If you <em data-end="3208" data-start="3202">must</em> phone, follow up with:</p>
<blockquote data-end="3290" data-start="3234">

</blockquote>

<ul data-end="7071" data-start="6846">
<li data-end="6873" data-start="6846">

<li data-end="6923" data-start="6894">

<li data-end="6945" data-start="6924">

</li>
<li data-end="6970" data-start="6946">

<li data-end="6997" data-start="6971">

</li>
<li data-end="7018" data-start="6998">

<h2 data-end="7806" data-start="7747"><strong data-end="7806" data-start="7750">3. When transition happens (Child to Adult Services)</strong></h2>
<p data-end="7865" data-start="7808">In Wales, transition planning should begin at age <strong data-end="7864" data-start="7858">14</strong>.</p>
<p data-end="7878" data-start="7867">Key rights:</p>
<ul data-end="8205" data-start="7880">
<li data-end="7930" data-start="7880">
<p data-end="7930" data-start="7882">A young person can keep their IDP until <strong data-end="7928" data-start="7922">25</strong></p>
</li>
<li data-end="7997" data-start="7931">
<p data-end="7997" data-start="7933">Education, health and social care must plan transition jointly</p>
</li>
<li data-end="8053" data-start="7998">
<p data-end="8053" data-start="8000">Parents and the young person must be fully involved</p>
</li>
<li data-end="8138" data-start="8054">
<p data-end="8138" data-start="8056">Plans must consider education, health, housing, employment, and community support</p>
</li>
<li data-end="8205" data-start="8139">
<p data-end="8205" data-start="8141">No young person should be left without a plan between services</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="8328" data-start="8207">Many families do not know transition planning should start this early, which is why things feel rushed or chaotic later.</p>
