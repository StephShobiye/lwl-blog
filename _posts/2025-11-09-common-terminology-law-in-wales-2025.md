<p><strong><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Learn+Without+Limits+CIC&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">Learn Without Limits CIC</a></strong></p>
<p>This guide explains some of the most frequently used terms and legal concepts that ALN families in Wales may encounter. It has been updated to reflect the latest Welsh Government legislation, especially the <em><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Additional+Learning+Needs+and+Education+Tribunal+%28Wales%29+Act+2018&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">Additional Learning Needs and Education Tribunal (Wales) Act 2018</a></em> and the <em><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+Code+for+Wales+2021&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">ALN Code for Wales 2021</a></em>.</p>
<hr />
<h3>ALN ‚Äì Additional Learning Needs</h3>
<p>This is now the legal term in Wales (replacing <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+SEN+special+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">SEN</a>). Any child or young person with a significantly greater difficulty in learning than their peers or a disability that prevents or hinders access to education may have ALN. The ALN system has fully replaced the SEN framework in most settings under the phased rollout between 2021‚Äì2025.</p>
<p><strong><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+ALNCO&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">ALNCO</a></strong>: The new title for what was once <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+SENCO&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">SENCO</a> ‚Äî the <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+Additional+Learning+Needs+Coordinator&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">Additional Learning Needs Coordinator</a> in schools and colleges.</p>
<hr />
<h3>The ALN Code for Wales 2021</h3>
<p>The ALN Code sets out how Local Authorities, schools, colleges and health boards must support children and young people with ALN. It includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>The right to a <strong>person-centred Individual Development Plan (<a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+Individual+Development+Plan&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">IDP</a>)</strong> for eligible learners aged 0‚Äì25</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Clear duties for schools and local authorities to identify and meet ALN</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>A strong focus on inclusion, wellbeing and early intervention</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="https://gov.wales/additional-learning-needs-code">Welsh Government ALN Code 2021 (PDF)</a></p>
<hr />
<h3>EHE ‚Äì Elective Home Education</h3>
<p>This is when a parent chooses to take full responsibility for a child‚Äôs education. <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+EHE+elective+home+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">EHE</a> is legal in Wales and does <strong>not</strong> require approval to deregister from a mainstream school.</p>
<p>However, if your child attends a <strong><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=special+schools+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">special school</a></strong>, you must have permission from the <strong>Local Authority (<a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+Local+Authority&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">LA</a>)</strong> to deregister and begin EHE. If you are unsure whether your child‚Äôs current setting is classified as mainstream or special, we recommend checking directly with <strong><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Estyn+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">Estyn</a></strong>.</p>
<p>There is no funding or automatic support for EHE. Parents do not need to follow the national curriculum, but they must provide a suitable education.</p>
<p>We keep a list of local support groups and EHE networks in our <a href="https://www.facebook.com/groups/learnwithoutlimitscic">Facebook group</a> to help families make connections.</p>
<hr />
<h3>EOTAS ‚Äì Education Otherwise Than At School</h3>
<p>This is provision arranged by a local authority for a child or young person who cannot attend school. It includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>PRUs (Pupil Referral Units)</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=LA+funded+home+tuition+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">LA funded home tuition</a></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Specialist+outreach+programmes+ALN+Wales+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">Specialist outreach programmes</a></p>
</li>
<li>
<p><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Hospital+education+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">Hospital education</a></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+EOTAS&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">EOTAS</a> must meet a child‚Äôs identified ALN where applicable and is subject to monitoring and review. It is a legal entitlement, not a favour.</p>
<hr />
<h3>‚ÄúForced‚Äù Home Educators</h3>
<p>An increasing number of families are choosing EHE not out of preference, but because of unmet needs, bullying, anxiety, trauma, or unlawful exclusion. These are not truly elective cases ‚Äî families often feel pushed out of the system.</p>
<p>These learners may be eligible for EOTAS or support under the ALN Act but are often left without formal provision. Learn Without Limits supports families to explore their rights and reclaim their options.</p>
<hr />
<h3>Flexi-Schooling</h3>
<p>This is where a child attends school part-time and is home educated the rest of the week. It is entirely at the discretion of the Headteacher. The child remains on the school roll. There is no legal right to <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+flexi-school&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">flexi-school</a> in Wales, but it can be agreed as part of an IDP.</p>
<hr />
<h3>Medical Needs and Education</h3>
<p>The Welsh Government guidance <em><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Access+to+Education+and+Support+for+Children+and+Young+People+with+Medical+Needs+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">Access to Education and Support for Children and Young People with Medical Needs</a></em> sets out the responsibilities of local authorities to provide education when a child is unable to attend due to physical or mental health.</p>
<p>This includes:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Education at home</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Outreach support</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Hospital teaching</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><a href="https://gov.wales/access-education-and-support-children-and-young-people-medical-needs">Access to Education Guidance (PDF)</a></p>
<hr />
<h3>Legal Framework</h3>
<p><strong>ALNET Act 2018</strong>: Establishes the ALN system, IDPs and rights of appeal to the Education Tribunal for Wales.</p>
<p><strong><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Social+Services+and+Well-being+%28Wales%29+Act+2014&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">Social Services and Well-being (Wales) Act 2014</a></strong>: Introduced person-centred approaches and changed the statutory definition from ‚Äúwelfare‚Äù to ‚Äúwell-being‚Äù. This applies to disabled children and their families across education, health and social care.</p>
<p><strong><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Equality+Act+2010+UK&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=6366106718549182575" target="_blank">Equality Act 2010</a></strong>: Requires reasonable adjustments for disabled learners in education.</p>
<p><strong>Estyn</strong>: The Welsh education and training inspectorate, responsible for inspecting schools, PRUs and LA provision.</p>
