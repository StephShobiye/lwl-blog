<h2 data-end="433" data-start="367"><strong data-end="433" data-start="370">What Support Can Families Expect ‚Äî and What Are the Limits?</strong></h2>
<p data-end="723" data-start="435">Once a Local Authority agrees that a child cannot receive suitable education in a school setting, they become responsible for arranging <strong data-end="613" data-start="571">Education Other Than At School (EOTAS)</strong>.<br data-end="617" data-start="614" />
But what this looks like varies widely across <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Wales+general+information&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">Wales</a> ‚Äî and rarely matches the images parents first imagine.</p>
<p data-end="904" data-start="725">This guide explains the <em data-end="760" data-start="749">realistic</em> range of EOTAS provision, what your child should expect, what may not be included, and how to ensure the package remains meaningful and lawful.<br /><br /><br /></p><div class="separator" style="clear: both; text-align: center;"><a href="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEipuEA2UL25oyHayc_G7fe1lbtCYjiI93BlGIB9Fv-w50bAJA88zQdTWY1vaXYNxfglABy1VDHhBQ49K916xt-Wiq5PJ5FZPLitmesP6LD0QLbR1iZ8Il172mg94nZgpCiuxJs2aiJgNi4XOhovNx7oaIuAKf7eDa3gPdUVXKBtDfUyhnzOQY2p8WpuWFJh" style="margin-left: 1em; margin-right: 1em;"><img alt="" data-original-height="526" data-original-width="526" height="240" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEipuEA2UL25oyHayc_G7fe1lbtCYjiI93BlGIB9Fv-w50bAJA88zQdTWY1vaXYNxfglABy1VDHhBQ49K916xt-Wiq5PJ5FZPLitmesP6LD0QLbR1iZ8Il172mg94nZgpCiuxJs2aiJgNi4XOhovNx7oaIuAKf7eDa3gPdUVXKBtDfUyhnzOQY2p8WpuWFJh" width="240" /></a></div><br /><div>üìò This is Part 2 of a 5-part series on EOTAS in Wales.</div><div><br /></div><div>‚¨ÖÔ∏è Previous: Part 1 ‚Äì Introduction to EOTAS&nbsp;&nbsp;</div><div><a href="https://learnwithoutlimitscic.blogspot.com/2025/12/eotas-in-wales-part-1.html">https://learnwithoutlimitscic.blogspot.com/2025/12/eotas-in-wales-part-1.html</a></div><div><br /></div><div>‚û°Ô∏è Next: Part 3 ‚Äì What EOTAS Provision Looks Like&nbsp;&nbsp;</div><div><a href="https://learnwithoutlimitscic.blogspot.com/2025/12/eotas-in-wales-part-3.html">https://learnwithoutlimitscic.blogspot.com/2025/12/eotas-in-wales-part-3.html</a></div><div><br /></div><p></p>

<h1 data-end="977" data-start="911">‚≠ê What ‚ÄúSuitable Education‚Äù Really Means (The Legal Requirement)</h1>
<p data-end="1056" data-start="979">Under <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Section+19+Education+Act+1996&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">Section 19 of the Education Act 1996</a>, the Local Authority must provide:</p>
<h3 data-end="1091" data-start="1058">‚úî education suitable to age</h3>
<h3 data-end="1129" data-start="1092">‚úî education suitable to ability</h3>
<h3 data-end="1168" data-start="1130">‚úî education suitable to aptitude</h3>
<h3 data-end="1202" data-start="1169">‚úî education suitable to ALN</h3>
<h3 data-end="1279" data-start="1203">‚úî education for as many hours as the child‚Äôs health or capacity allows</h3>
<p data-end="1501" data-start="1281">‚ÄúFull time‚Äù does not always mean 25 hours a week.<br data-end="1333" data-start="1330" />
For children who cannot tolerate school or structured learning for extended periods, <strong data-end="1447" data-start="1418"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=part+time+Education+Other+Than+At+School+EOTAS&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">part time EOTAS</a> is lawful</strong>, as long as it reflects the child‚Äôs genuine capacity.</p>
<p data-end="1555" data-start="1503">But the education provided must still be meaningful.</p>
<hr data-end="1560" data-start="1557" />
<h1 data-end="1615" data-start="1562">‚≠ê The <em data-end="1581" data-start="1570">Realistic</em> Range of EOTAS Provision in Wales</h1>
<p data-end="1669" data-start="1617">Here is what most families actually see in practice.</p>
<h2 data-end="1712" data-start="1671">‚≠ê 1. <strong data-end="1710" data-start="1679">1:1 Tuition (Core Subjects)</strong></h2>
<p data-end="1801" data-start="1713">Usually <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Education+Other+Than+At+School+EOTAS+curriculum+English+Maths+Science&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">English, Maths, Science</a>.<br data-end="1748" data-start="1745" />
Delivered by qualified teachers or specialist tutors.</p>
<p data-end="1821" data-start="1803">Typical pattern:</p>
<ul data-end="1946" data-start="1822">
<li data-end="1844" data-start="1822">
<p data-end="1844" data-start="1824">2‚Äì5 hours per week</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1877" data-start="1845">
<p data-end="1877" data-start="1847">Sessions often 45‚Äì60 minutes</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1946" data-start="1878">
<p data-end="1946" data-start="1880">Delivered in the home, library, online or in a community setting</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="1994" data-start="1948">This is the foundation of most EOTAS packages.</p>
<hr data-end="1999" data-start="1996" />
<h2 data-end="2040" data-start="2001">‚≠ê 2. <strong data-end="2038" data-start="2009">Online Learning Platforms</strong></h2>
<p data-end="2093" data-start="2041">Often used to supplement limited tutor availability.</p>
<p data-end="2112" data-start="2095">This can include:</p>
<ul data-end="2233" data-start="2114">
<li data-end="2144" data-start="2114">
<p data-end="2144" data-start="2116">small group online lessons</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2168" data-start="2145">
<p data-end="2168" data-start="2147">live tutor sessions</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2193" data-start="2169">
<p data-end="2193" data-start="2171">recorded instruction</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2233" data-start="2194">
<p data-end="2233" data-start="2196">GCSE or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Functional+Skills+platforms+examples&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">Functional Skills platforms</a></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="2349" data-start="2235">These are helpful but should not be the <em data-end="2281" data-start="2275">only</em> form of education unless the child‚Äôs health prevents live teaching.</p>

<p data-end="2620" data-start="2553">These offer peer contact without the pressure of mainstream school.</p>

</li>

<p data-end="2909" data-start="2863">These are usually structured and time limited.</p>

<p data-end="3210" data-start="3142">But therapeutic input must be written into an IDP to be enforceable.</p>

<h1 data-end="3258" data-start="3217">‚≠ê What EOTAS <em data-end="3258" data-start="3232">Does Not Usually Include</em></h1>
<p data-end="3313" data-start="3260">EOTAS is <strong data-end="3282" data-start="3269">education</strong>, not childcare or social care.</p>
<p data-end="3347" data-start="3315">It does <em data-end="3328" data-start="3323">not</em> routinely include:</p>
<ul data-end="3620" data-start="3349">
<li data-end="3372" data-start="3349">
<p data-end="3372" data-start="3351">all day supervision</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3392" data-start="3373">
<p data-end="3392" data-start="3375">wraparound care</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3420" data-start="3393">
<p data-end="3420" data-start="3395">a tutor on site all day</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3467" data-start="3421">
<p data-end="3467" data-start="3423">multiple tutors providing continuous input</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3517" data-start="3468">
<p data-end="3517" data-start="3470">ongoing therapy unless assessed and specified</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3540" data-start="3518">
<p data-end="3540" data-start="3520">transport services</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3582" data-start="3541">
<p data-end="3582" data-start="3543">fully bespoke home-schooling packages</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3620" data-start="3583">
<p data-end="3620" data-start="3585">private therapeutic interventions</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="3733" data-start="3622">If a child needs care or supervision, this comes under <strong data-end="3721" data-start="3677"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Social+Services+and+Wellbeing+Act+duties&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">Social Services and Wellbeing Act duties</a></strong>, not EOTAS.</p>
<hr data-end="3738" data-start="3735" />
<h1 data-end="3810" data-start="3740">‚≠ê Specialist Interventions: Rare, Possible and Often Tribunal-Driven</h1>
<p data-end="4423" data-start="3812">Some families ask about specialist approaches such as <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=define+ABA&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">ABA</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Intensive+Interaction+definition&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">Intensive Interaction</a>, or full-time online schooling. These <strong data-end="3938" data-start="3931">can</strong> be provided under EOTAS in rare cases, but they are <strong data-end="4006" data-start="3991">not typical</strong>, and <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Welsh+Local+Authorities&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">Welsh Local Authorities</a> almost never agree to them without significant evidence. In most situations where specialist pedagogies have been secured, this has happened <strong data-end="4197" data-start="4177">through <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=EOTAS+Tribunal&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">Tribunal</a></strong>, supported by extensive professional reports showing that no other form of education would be suitable. These approaches may be appropriate for a very small number of children, but they are not offered routinely across Wales.</p>
<hr data-end="4428" data-start="4425" />
<h1 data-end="4472" data-start="4430">‚≠ê Typical Hours: What Parents Can Expect</h1>
<h3 data-end="4502" data-start="4474">‚≠ê Primary-age learners</h3>
<p data-end="4532" data-start="4503">3‚Äì6 hours per week is common.</p>
<h3 data-end="4564" data-start="4534">‚≠ê Secondary-age learners</h3>
<p data-end="4650" data-start="4565">4‚Äì10 hours per week is typical, sometimes rising to 12‚Äì15 hours in more generous LAs.</p>
<h3 data-end="4711" data-start="4652">‚≠ê Learners with Long Covid, ME/CFS or chronic illness</h3>
<p data-end="4786" data-start="4712">Often begin with 1‚Äì3 hours weekly, increasing gradually as stamina allows.</p>
<h3 data-end="4809" data-start="4788">‚≠ê In GCSE years</h3>
<p data-end="4859" data-start="4810">Some LAs add extra tuition if the child can cope.</p>
<p data-end="4971" data-start="4861">These numbers often surprise parents ‚Äî but reflect capacity, tutor availability and <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Welsh+Government&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">Welsh Government</a> guidance.</p>
<hr data-end="4976" data-start="4973" />
<h1 data-end="5040" data-start="4978">‚≠ê Why EOTAS Is Often Part Time ‚Äî Even When Parents Want More</h1>
<p data-end="5078" data-start="5042">EOTAS tends to be part time because:</p>
<ul data-end="5360" data-start="5080">
<li data-end="5127" data-start="5080">
<p data-end="5127" data-start="5082">many children cannot tolerate long sessions</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5181" data-start="5128">
<p data-end="5181" data-start="5130">fatigue, anxiety or dysregulation limits capacity</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5219" data-start="5182">
<p data-end="5219" data-start="5184">tutoring is intense and demanding</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5254" data-start="5220">
<p data-end="5254" data-start="5222">tutor shortages are widespread</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5306" data-start="5255">
<p data-end="5306" data-start="5257">Welsh guidance does not require full timetables</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5360" data-start="5307">
<p data-end="5360" data-start="5309">genuine learning capacity matters more than hours</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="5462" data-start="5362">A child cannot do 25 hours of EOTAS if they can only tolerate 45 minutes of learning before a crash.</p>
<p data-end="5515" data-start="5464">Part time is not failure ‚Äî it is <strong data-end="5514" data-start="5497"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=stabilisation+EOTAS&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=3490397753207923394" target="_blank">stabilisation</a></strong>.</p>
<hr data-end="5520" data-start="5517" />
<h1 data-end="5564" data-start="5522">‚≠ê The Local Authority‚Äôs Responsibilities</h1>
<p data-end="5579" data-start="5566">Your LA must:</p>
<h3 data-end="5617" data-start="5581">‚úî assess your child‚Äôs capacity</h3>
<h3 data-end="5667" data-start="5618">‚úî provide meaningful and suitable education</h3>
<h3 data-end="5712" data-start="5668">‚úî ensure tutors are vetted and trained</h3>
<h3 data-end="5747" data-start="5713">‚úî monitor progress regularly</h3>
<h3 data-end="5776" data-start="5748">‚úî keep the IDP updated</h3>
<h3 data-end="5822" data-start="5777">‚úî plan for transitions or reintegration</h3>
<h3 data-end="5858" data-start="5823">‚úî review provision frequently</h3>
<p data-end="5872" data-start="5860">They cannot:</p>
<p data-end="6081" data-start="5874">‚ùå leave you with no education<br data-end="5906" data-start="5903" />
‚ùå claim ‚Äúwe have no tutors‚Äù indefinitely<br data-end="5949" data-start="5946" />
‚ùå rely on worksheets alone<br data-end="5978" data-start="5975" />
‚ùå push you toward deregistration<br data-end="6013" data-start="6010" />
‚ùå use attendance penalties<br data-end="6042" data-start="6039" />
‚ùå delay reviews without justification</p>
<hr data-end="6086" data-start="6083" />
<h1 data-end="6131" data-start="6088">‚≠ê Integrating Social Care (When Relevant)</h1>
<p data-end="6170" data-start="6133">Some EOTAS learners also qualify for:</p>
<ul data-end="6322" data-start="6172">
<li data-end="6188" data-start="6172">
<p data-end="6188" data-start="6174">short breaks</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6208" data-start="6189">
<p data-end="6208" data-start="6191">direct payments</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6230" data-start="6209">
<p data-end="6230" data-start="6211">carer assessments</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6267" data-start="6231">
<p data-end="6267" data-start="6233">emotional or behavioural support</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6287" data-start="6268">
<p data-end="6287" data-start="6270">sibling support</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6322" data-start="6288">
<p data-end="6322" data-start="6290">home support for complex needs</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="6495" data-start="6324">Families often do not realise that <strong data-end="6385" data-start="6359">EOTAS plus Social Care</strong> is a legitimate combination.<br data-end="6417" data-start="6414" />
If a child‚Äôs needs span both education and care, both systems must be engaged.</p>
<hr data-end="6500" data-start="6497" />
<h1 data-end="6544" data-start="6502">‚≠ê What a Strong EOTAS Package Looks Like</h1>
<p data-end="6586" data-start="6546">A high quality package usually includes:</p>
<ul data-end="6835" data-start="6588">
<li data-end="6603" data-start="6588">
<p data-end="6603" data-start="6590">1:1 tuition</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6627" data-start="6604">
<p data-end="6627" data-start="6606">progress monitoring</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6676" data-start="6628">
<p data-end="6676" data-start="6630">appropriate therapeutic or emotional support</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6717" data-start="6677">
<p data-end="6717" data-start="6679">flexibility around health or anxiety</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6762" data-start="6718">
<p data-end="6762" data-start="6720">reintegration planning where appropriate</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6788" data-start="6763">
<p data-end="6788" data-start="6765">safeguarding measures</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6817" data-start="6789">
<p data-end="6817" data-start="6791">multi-agency involvement</p>
</li>
<li data-end="6835" data-start="6818">
<p data-end="6835" data-start="6820">clear reviews</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="6894" data-start="6837">The right package feels <strong data-end="6875" data-start="6861">supportive</strong>, not overwhelming.</p>
<hr data-end="6899" data-start="6896" />
<h1 data-end="6933" data-start="6901">‚≠ê What Happens if EOTAS Fails?</h1>
<p data-end="6968" data-start="6935">EOTAS can become unsuitable when:</p>
<ul data-end="7136" data-start="6970">
<li data-end="6997" data-start="6970">
<p data-end="6997" data-start="6972">the child cannot engage</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7021" data-start="6998">
<p data-end="7021" data-start="7000">health deteriorates</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7055" data-start="7022">
<p data-end="7055" data-start="7024">the tutor is not a good match</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7084" data-start="7056">
<p data-end="7084" data-start="7058">provision is too limited</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7104" data-start="7085">
<p data-end="7104" data-start="7087">progress stalls</p>
</li>
<li data-end="7136" data-start="7105">
<p data-end="7136" data-start="7107">family circumstances change</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="7169" data-start="7138">When this happens, the LA must:</p>
<h3 data-end="7193" data-start="7171">‚úî review the IDP</h3>
<h3 data-end="7230" data-start="7194">‚úî adjust or increase provision</h3>
<h3 data-end="7268" data-start="7231">‚úî explore specialist placements</h3>
<h3 data-end="7309" data-start="7269">‚úî involve Social Care where needed</h3>
<h3 data-end="7344" data-start="7310">‚úî ensure education continues</h3>
<p data-end="7390" data-start="7346">Parents should document concerns in writing.</p>
