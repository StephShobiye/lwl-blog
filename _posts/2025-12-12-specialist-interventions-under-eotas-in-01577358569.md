<h2 data-end="1267" data-start="1179"><strong data-end="1267" data-start="1182">Part 2: How to Access Support, What is State Provided, and What Requires Tribunal</strong></h2>
<p data-end="1367" data-start="1269">When parents discover the range of interventions that exist, the next question is always the same:</p>
<p data-end="1424" data-start="1369"><strong data-end="1424" data-start="1369">How do we actually get this for our child in Wales?</strong></p>
<p data-end="1677" data-start="1426">This section explains the practical routes to support, what the NHS and <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Local+Authorities+in+Wales+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">Local Authorities</a> can provide, what relies on the private sector, and how to gather evidence so that decisions are made fairly. It is honest, clear and rooted in lived experience.</p>
<hr data-end="1682" data-start="1679" />
<h1 data-end="1717" data-start="1684">‚≠ê 1. What the NHS can provide</h1>
<p data-end="1902" data-start="1718"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=NHS+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">NHS Wales</a> is responsible for health based interventions. These are usually delivered free of charge and can be written into a child‚Äôs <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=IDP+Wales+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">IDP</a> if they are essential for accessing education.</p>
<p data-end="1963" data-start="1904">NHS services commonly involved with <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=EOTAS+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">EOTAS</a> learners include:</p>
<h3 data-end="1993" data-start="1965">‚úî Occupational Therapy</h3>
<p data-end="2085" data-start="1994">Assessment and advice.<br data-end="2019" data-start="2016" />
Direct therapy is limited and sensory therapy is rarely delivered.</p>
<h3 data-end="2108" data-start="2087">‚úî Physiotherapy</h3>
<p data-end="2154" data-start="2109">Support for mobility and physical conditions.</p>
<h3 data-end="2191" data-start="2156">‚úî Speech and Language Therapy</h3>
<p data-end="2263" data-start="2192">Assessment, communication support, feeding advice, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=AAC+communication&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">AAC</a> recommendations.</p>
<h3 data-end="2294" data-start="2265">‚úî Community Paediatrics</h3>
<p data-end="2366" data-start="2295">Medical oversight, diagnosis, letters supporting educational decisions.</p>
<h3 data-end="2412" data-start="2368">‚úî CAMHS or Neurodevelopmental Services</h3>
<p data-end="2536" data-start="2413">Assessment of mental health and autism or ADHD.<br data-end="2463" data-start="2460" />
They do not provide education but their letters carry significant weight.</p>
<h3 data-end="2587" data-start="2538">‚úî Nurses specialising in chronic conditions</h3>
<p data-end="2644" data-start="2588">For example, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Long+Covid+symptoms&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">Long Covid</a>, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ME+condition+symptoms&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">ME</a> or fatigue based conditions.</p>
<h3 data-end="2707" data-start="2646">‚úî Vision or hearing support through health linked teams</h3>
<p data-end="2746" data-start="2708">Including audiology and ophthalmology.</p>
<p data-end="2967" data-start="2748"><strong data-end="2778" data-start="2748">What parents need to know:</strong><br data-end="2781" data-start="2778" />
NHS provision is free but often limited by capacity. If a child needs more frequent therapy than the NHS can deliver, this is where private practitioners or <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=education+tribunal+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">Tribunal</a> may become relevant.</p>
<hr data-end="2972" data-start="2969" />
<h1 data-end="3020" data-start="2974">‚≠ê 2. What Local Authorities can commission</h1>
<p data-end="3204" data-start="3021">Local Authorities are responsible for ensuring a child receives suitable education. This includes commissioning specialist interventions when they are required for educational access.</p>
<p data-end="3239" data-start="3206">Local Authorities may commission:</p>
<h3 data-end="3284" data-start="3241">‚úî EOTAS tutors with specialist skills</h3>
<p data-end="3362" data-start="3285">For anxiety informed teaching, literacy support, or autism informed practice.</p>
<h3 data-end="3400" data-start="3364">‚úî Outdoor therapeutic learning</h3>
<p data-end="3466" data-start="3401"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Woodland+programmes+for+education&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">Woodland programmes</a>, emotional wellbeing sessions, <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=forest+school+activities&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">forest school</a>.</p>
<h3 data-end="3505" data-start="3468">‚úî Alternative provision centres</h3>
<p data-end="3558" data-start="3506">Small therapeutic settings or vocational programmes.</p>
<h3 data-end="3594" data-start="3560">‚úî Third sector interventions</h3>
<p data-end="3653" data-start="3595"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Play+therapy+benefits+for+children&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">Play therapy</a>, mentoring, community education, life skills.</p>
<h3 data-end="3677" data-start="3655">‚úî Online tuition</h3>
<p data-end="3719" data-start="3678">Short sessions based on stamina and need.</p>
<h3 data-end="3781" data-start="3721">‚úî Specialist teachers for vision or hearing impairment</h3>
<p data-end="3816" data-start="3782">Provided through LA sensory teams.</p>
<h3 data-end="3879" data-start="3818">‚úî One to one SALT or OT sessions if Tribunal directs it</h3>
<p data-end="3915" data-start="3880">Rare unless ordered through appeal.</p>
<h3 data-end="3942" data-start="3917"><strong data-end="3940" data-start="3921">Important note:</strong></h3>
<p data-end="3993" data-start="3943">Local Authorities usually only commission what is:</p>
<ul data-end="4095" data-start="3995">
<li data-end="4016" data-start="3995">
<p data-end="4016" data-start="3997">available locally</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4031" data-start="4017">
<p data-end="4031" data-start="4019">affordable</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4055" data-start="4032">
<p data-end="4055" data-start="4034">demonstrably needed</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4095" data-start="4056">
<p data-end="4095" data-start="4058">recommended by professional reports</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="4184" data-start="4097">Parents do not get to choose the provider, but they can challenge unsuitable provision.</p>

<h1 data-end="4813" data-start="4768">‚≠ê 4. When Tribunal becomes the only route</h1>
<p data-end="4865" data-start="4814">Tribunal becomes relevant when the Local Authority:</p>
<ul data-end="5138" data-start="4867">
<li data-end="4911" data-start="4867">
<p data-end="4911" data-start="4869">refuses to provide a needed intervention</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4929" data-start="4912">
<p data-end="4929" data-start="4914">refuses EOTAS</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4961" data-start="4930">
<p data-end="4961" data-start="4932">provides too little therapy</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5007" data-start="4962">
<p data-end="5007" data-start="4964">insists on reintegration without evidence</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5039" data-start="5008">
<p data-end="5039" data-start="5010">ignores medical information</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5071" data-start="5040">
<p data-end="5071" data-start="5042">offers unsuitable provision</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5138" data-start="5072">
<p data-end="5138" data-start="5074">will not fund specialist support despite clear recommendations</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-end="5233" data-start="5140">Tribunal decisions are evidence based.<br data-end="5181" data-start="5178" />
The more precise the reports, the stronger the case.</p>

<h1 data-end="5300" data-start="5240">‚≠ê 5. How to gather evidence for specialist interventions</h1>
<p data-end="5407" data-start="5301">Families often worry that they do not know what ‚Äúcounts‚Äù as evidence. In Wales, the strongest sources are:</p>
<h3 data-end="5445" data-start="5409">‚úî Reports from NHS specialists</h3>
<p data-end="5478" data-start="5446">Even short letters are powerful.</p>
<h3 data-end="5522" data-start="5480">‚úî Educational psychology assessments</h3>
<p data-end="5589" data-start="5523">These explain how the child learns and what provision is required.</p>
<h3 data-end="5628" data-start="5591">‚úî Occupational therapy evidence</h3>
<p data-end="5685" data-start="5629">Especially when linked to functional access to learning.</p>
<h3 data-end="5730" data-start="5687">‚úî Speech and Language Therapy reports</h3>
<p data-end="5790" data-start="5731">Particularly where communication barriers affect education.</p>
<h3 data-end="5814" data-start="5792">‚úî Tutor feedback</h3>
<p data-end="5873" data-start="5815">Useful if the child is already engaging with home tuition.</p>
<h3 data-end="5897" data-start="5875">‚úî Parent diaries</h3>
<p data-end="5959" data-start="5898">These provide patterns that demonstrate difficulty over time.</p>
<h3 data-end="5980" data-start="5961">‚úî Child voice</h3>
<p data-end="6042" data-start="5981">Tribunal places real weight on the views of the young person.</p>
<p data-end="6120" data-start="6044">We will include parent friendly templates for evidence collection in Part 2.</p>

<li data-end="1994" data-start="1972">
<p data-end="1994" data-start="1974">reduced timetables</p>

<p data-end="3338" data-start="3318">tutor availability</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="3460" data-start="3340">This is why some families are directed toward blended or alternative options that meet the need but in a different form.</p><hr data-end="3465" data-start="3462" /><h1 data-end="3500" data-start="3467">‚≠ê 6. <li data-end="3925" data-start="3850">

<li data-end="4141" data-start="4110">

<li data-end="4162" data-start="4142">
<p data-end="4162" data-start="4144">outdoor learning</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4192" data-start="4163">
<p data-end="4192" data-start="4165">targeted SaLT or OT input</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4212" data-start="4193">
<p data-end="4212" data-start="4195">hybrid packages</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="4272" data-start="4214">Understanding this helps parents shape realistic requests.</p><hr data-end="4277" data-start="4274" /><h1 data-end="4305" data-start="4279">‚≠ê 7. The child‚Äôs voice</h1><p data-end="4422" data-start="4306">The <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+system+in+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=2822692276397501096" target="_blank">ALN system in Wales</a> requires that the child‚Äôs views are considered.<br data-end="4380" data-start="4377" />
Tribunal also places great weight on this.</p><p data-end="4440" data-start="4424">Panels look for:</p><ul data-end="4583" data-start="4442">
<li data-end="4476" data-start="4442">

</p><p data-end="4801" data-start="4733">Child voice does not need to be perfect. It just needs to be honest.<br /><br />___________________________________________________________________________________<br /><br /></p><h3 data-end="318" data-start="250"><strong data-end="318" data-start="254">Section 3: How to Request a Specific Intervention in the IDP</strong></h3><p data-end="553" data-start="320">Requesting a specialist intervention is not simply a matter of saying what you want. The ALN system in Wales requires Local Authorities to consider <strong data-end="482" data-start="468">need first</strong>, then decide what provision is necessary to secure suitable education.</p><p data-end="608" data-start="555">This section explains how to frame a request so that:</p><ul data-end="741" data-start="610">
<li data-end="631" data-start="610">
<p data-end="631" data-start="612">the need is clear</p>
</li>
<li data-end="667" data-start="632">
<p data-end="667" data-start="634">the provision logically follows</p>
</li>
<li data-end="706" data-start="668">
<p data-end="706" data-start="670">the Local Authority can approve it</p>
</li>
<li data-end="741" data-start="707">
<p data-end="741" data-start="709">you avoid common refusal traps</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="818" data-start="743">It also includes suggested wording you can use directly in your IDP review.</p><hr data-end="823" data-start="820" /><h1 data-end="875" data-start="825">‚≠ê 1. Start with the need, not the intervention</h1><p data-end="952" data-start="876">One of the most common mistakes parents make is to begin with a phrase like:</p><p data-end="1034" data-start="954"><strong data-end="996" data-start="954">‚ÄúI want my child to have play therapy‚Äù</strong><br data-end="999" data-start="996" />
or<br data-end="1004" data-start="1001" />
<strong data-end="1034" data-start="1004">‚ÄúWe want outdoor learning‚Äù</strong></p><p data-end="1141" data-start="1036">Panels tend to refuse these requests because the law requires them to look at <strong data-end="1123" data-start="1114">needs</strong>, not preferences.</p><p data-end="1172" data-start="1143">A more effective approach is:</p><blockquote data-end="1405" data-start="1174">
<p data-end="1405" data-start="1176">‚ÄúMy child needs support with communication and emotional regulation. The current environment is overwhelming and prevents them from engaging safely. A therapeutic intervention that supports regulation and connection is required.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote><p data-end="1442" data-start="1407">Then, once the need is established:</p><blockquote data-end="1578" data-start="1444">
<p data-end="1578" data-start="1446">‚ÄúOne option that would meet this need is play therapy. I would like the Local Authority to consider this as part of the IDP review.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote><p data-end="1678" data-start="1580">This moves the conversation from <strong data-end="1621" data-start="1613">want</strong> to <strong data-end="1633" data-start="1625">need</strong>, which is far harder for a panel to dismiss.</p><hr data-end="1683" data-start="1680" /><h1 data-end="1754" data-start="1685">‚≠ê 2. Show the link between the need and the proposed intervention</h1><p data-end="1894" data-start="1755">Local Authorities must be able to justify why they agree to a particular intervention.<br data-end="1844" data-start="1841" />
You can help them by drawing a clear line between:</p><h3 data-end="1915" data-start="1896">‚úî the barrier</h3><h3 data-end="1932" data-start="1916">‚úî the need</h3><h3 data-end="1954" data-start="1933">‚úî the provision</h3><p data-end="1965" data-start="1956">Examples:</p><p data-end="2199" data-start="1967"><strong data-end="1979" data-start="1967">Barrier:</strong> Child experiences sensory overload and becomes distressed in classrooms.<br data-end="2055" data-start="2052" />
<strong data-end="2064" data-start="2055">Need:</strong> Regulation strategies and sensory support.<br data-end="2110" data-start="2107" />
<strong data-end="2124" data-start="2110">Provision:</strong> OT designed regulation programme, tutor trained in sensory aware practice.</p><p data-end="2346" data-start="2201"><strong data-end="2213" data-start="2201">Barrier:</strong> Child cannot process spoken language under stress.<br data-end="2267" data-start="2264" />
<strong data-end="2276" data-start="2267">Need:</strong> Supported communication.<br data-end="2304" data-start="2301" />
<strong data-end="2318" data-start="2304">Provision:</strong> SaLT input, AAC strategies.</p><p data-end="2514" data-start="2348"><strong data-end="2360" data-start="2348">Barrier:</strong> Severe anxiety prevents attendance.<br data-end="2399" data-start="2396" />
<strong data-end="2408" data-start="2399">Need:</strong> Therapeutic learning in a safe setting.<br data-end="2451" data-start="2448" />
<strong data-end="2465" data-start="2451">Provision:</strong> Therapeutic tutor or outdoor learning programme.</p><p data-end="2572" data-start="2516">When the chain is logical, approval is much more likely.</p><hr data-end="2577" data-start="2574" /><h1 data-end="2620" data-start="2579">‚≠ê 3. Use neutral and factual language</h1><p data-end="2693" data-start="2621">Avoid emotional or adversarial statements, even when you feel desperate.</p><p data-end="2707" data-start="2695">Not helpful:</p><ul data-end="2798" data-start="2708">
<li data-end="2741" data-start="2708">
<p data-end="2741" data-start="2710">‚ÄúSchool has failed my child.‚Äù</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2768" data-start="2742">
<p data-end="2768" data-start="2744">‚ÄúNobody is listening.‚Äù</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2798" data-start="2769">
<p data-end="2798" data-start="2771">‚ÄúWe demand this therapy.‚Äù</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="2808" data-start="2800">Helpful:</p><ul data-end="3032" data-start="2809">
<li data-end="2905" data-start="2809">
<p data-end="2905" data-start="2811">‚ÄúDespite many attempts, my child remains unable to access education in the current setting.‚Äù</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2963" data-start="2906">
<p data-end="2963" data-start="2908">‚ÄúHere is the evidence that shows ongoing difficulty.‚Äù</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3032" data-start="2964">
<p data-end="3032" data-start="2966">‚ÄúThis intervention would directly address the barrier identified.‚Äù</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="3131" data-start="3034">Calm, factual wording keeps the focus on your child and reduces defensiveness in decision making.</p><hr data-end="3136" data-start="3133" /><h1 data-end="3190" data-start="3138">‚≠ê 4. Bring supporting evidence to the IDP review</h1><p data-end="3307" data-start="3191">Panels rely heavily on written recommendations.<br data-end="3241" data-start="3238" />
You do not need a twenty page report. A short statement is enough.</p><h3 data-end="3336" data-start="3309">Useful sources include:</h3><ul data-end="3521" data-start="3337">
<li data-end="3350" data-start="3337">
<p data-end="3350" data-start="3339">GP letter</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3367" data-start="3351">
<p data-end="3367" data-start="3353">CAMHS letter</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3381" data-start="3368">
<p data-end="3381" data-start="3370">OT advice</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3397" data-start="3382">
<p data-end="3397" data-start="3384">SaLT report</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3435" data-start="3398">
<p data-end="3435" data-start="3400">educational psychology assessment</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3454" data-start="3436">
<p data-end="3454" data-start="3438">tutor feedback</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3471" data-start="3455">
<p data-end="3471" data-start="3457">nurse letter</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3505" data-start="3472">
<p data-end="3505" data-start="3474">parent diary showing patterns</p>
</li>
<li data-end="3521" data-start="3506">
<p data-end="3521" data-start="3508">child voice</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="3633" data-start="3523">If evidence shows the child is not coping and an intervention is needed, the panel must consider it seriously.</p><hr data-end="3638" data-start="3635" /><h1 data-end="3713" data-start="3640">‚≠ê 5. <p data-end="3999" data-start="3837">‚ÄúI would like the intervention we discussed to be recorded in Section 2 of the IDP, along with who will deliver it, how often, and how progress will be reviewed.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote><p data-end="4041" data-start="4001">This prevents vague commitments such as:</p><ul data-end="4130" data-start="4043">
<li data-end="4072" data-start="4043">
<p data-end="4072" data-start="4045">‚ÄúWe will explore options‚Äù</p>

<li data-end="4099" data-start="4073">
<p data-end="4099" data-start="4075">‚ÄúWe will monitor this‚Äù</p>

</li>

<p data-end="4162" data-start="4145">clear provision</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4182" data-start="4163">
<p data-end="4182" data-start="4165">clear frequency</p>
</li>
<li data-end="4207" data-start="4183">
<p data-end="4207" data-start="4185">clear responsibility</p>
</li>
</ul><p data-end="4221" data-start="4209">For example:</p><blockquote data-end="4322" data-start="4223">
<p data-end="4322" data-start="4225">‚ÄúOne to one therapeutic tutoring for one hour per week delivered by an anxiety informed tutor.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote><p data-end="4326" data-start="4324">or</p><blockquote data-end="4412" data-start="4328">
<p data-end="4412" data-start="4330">‚ÄúOutdoor learning for two sessions per week delivered by a commissioned provider.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote><hr data-end="4417" data-start="4414" /><h1 data-end="4470" data-start="4419">‚≠ê 6. Helpful wording to include in your request</h1><p data-end="4564" data-start="4471">Parents often ask for exact phrases that avoid refusal traps.<br data-end="4535" data-start="4532" />
Here are options you can use:</p><p data-end="4595" data-start="4566"><strong data-end="4595" data-start="4566">To request consideration:</strong></p><blockquote data-end="4733" data-start="4597">

</blockquote><p data-end="4766" data-start="4735"><strong data-end="4766" data-start="4735">To link need and provision:</strong></p><blockquote data-end="4933" data-start="4768">
<p data-end="4933" data-start="4770">‚ÄúThe evidence shows that my child‚Äôs difficulty stems from sensory overload and emotional distress. A specialist intervention that supports regulation is required.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote><p data-end="4965" data-start="4935"><strong data-end="4965" data-start="4935">To strengthen the request:</strong></p><blockquote data-end="5085" data-start="4967">
<p data-end="5085" data-start="4969">‚ÄúThis intervention is recommended in professional advice and directly relates to the needs identified in Section 1.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote><p data-end="5109" data-start="5087"><strong data-end="5109" data-start="5087">To ensure clarity:</strong></p><blockquote data-end="5245" data-start="5111">
<p data-end="5245" data-start="5113">‚ÄúIf approved, please can this provision be written clearly in Section 2 with details of delivery, frequency and who is responsible.‚Äù</p>
</blockquote><hr data-end="5250" data-start="5247" /><h1 data-end="5294" data-start="5252">‚≠ê 7. What a refusal usually looks like</h1><p data-end="5342" data-start="5295">Local Authorities tend to refuse requests when:</p><ul data-end="5635" data-start="5344">
<li data-end="5381" data-start="5344">
<p data-end="5381" data-start="5346">the need is not clearly described</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5437" data-start="5382">
<p data-end="5437" data-start="5384">the intervention does not link directly to the need</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5486" data-start="5438">
<p data-end="5486" data-start="5440">school based adjustments have not been tried</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5510" data-start="5487">
<p data-end="5510" data-start="5489">evidence is missing</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5547" data-start="5511">

</li>
<li data-end="5580" data-start="5548">
<p data-end="5580" data-start="5550">the cost is disproportionate</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5635" data-start="5581">

</li>
</ul><p data-end="5699" data-start="5637">Knowing this helps you prepare a stronger case from the start.</p><hr data-end="5704" data-start="5701" /><h1 data-end="5742" data-start="5706">‚≠ê 8. <li data-end="5780" data-start="5772">

<p data-end="4221" data-start="4183">confirming agreements after meetings</p>

</li>
</ul><p data-end="4362" data-start="4269">The ALN Code expects the IDP to be a living document.<br data-end="4325" data-start="4322" />

<li data-end="4554" data-start="4535">

</li>

<li data-end="4756" data-start="4730">

<li data-end="4800" data-start="4757">

<li data-end="5135" data-start="5113">

</li>

<li data-end="5367" data-start="5344">

<li data-end="5393" data-start="5368">
<p data-end="5393" data-start="5370">medical needs tuition</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5413" data-start="5394">
<p data-end="5413" data-start="5396">sensory support</p>
</li>
<li data-end="5438" data-start="5414">
<p data-end="5438" data-start="5416">therapeutic tutoring</p>

<li data-end="5459" data-start="5439">
<p data-end="5459" data-start="5441">SaLT or OT hours</p>

<li data-end="5488" data-start="5460">
<p data-end="5488" data-start="5462">online learning packages</p>
