<p data-end="724" data-start="462">For many families across Wales, the transition from school to post-16 education can feel like taking a leap of faith. Support carefully built through the Additional Learning Needs (ALN) system can suddenly change or disappear once a young person turns 16.</p>
<p data-end="926" data-start="726">From delays in college assessments to uncertainty about Individual Development Plans (IDPs), parents and carers often find themselves lost between education, health, and social-care responsibilities.</p>
<p data-end="1136" data-start="928"><strong data-end="956" data-start="928">Learn Without Limits CIC</strong> understands this struggle â€” and our <strong data-end="1078" data-start="993">December App Release (v5.0) will include a brand-new <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Post-16+Parent+Carer+Toolkit+Wales&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1119267944045665752" target="_blank">Post-16 Parent/Carer Toolkit</a></strong> designed to make this journey clearer, calmer, and fairer.</p>

<h2 data-end="1216" data-start="1143"><strong data-end="1216" data-start="1146">The Changing Landscape: ALN Code 2021 and Post-16 Responsibilities</strong></h2>
<p data-end="1457" data-start="1218">The <strong data-end="1249" data-start="1222"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=ALN+Code+for+Wales+2021&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1119267944045665752" target="_blank">ALN Code for Wales 2021</a></strong> reshaped how post-16 learners are supported, replacing <a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Statements+and+Learning+and+Skills+Plans+Wales+ALN&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1119267944045665752" target="_blank">Statements and Learning and Skills Plans</a> with a single, unified <strong data-end="1406" data-start="1369">Individual Development Plan (IDP)</strong> that continues from school into further education.</p>
<p data-end="1738" data-start="1459">Under the <strong data-end="1538" data-start="1469"><a data-preview="" href="https://www.google.com/search?ved=1t:260882&amp;q=Additional+Learning+Needs+and+Education+Tribunal+Wales+Act+2018&amp;bbid=6470633950786405900&amp;bpid=1119267944045665752" target="_blank">Additional Learning Needs and Education Tribunal (Wales) Act 2018</a></strong>, local authorities now retain a duty to ensure continuity of support. However, implementation has varied across Wales, leaving many families unsure who is responsible once their child leaves school.</p>
<p data-end="1766" data-start="1740"><strong data-end="1766" data-start="1740">Key facts for parents:</strong></p>
<ul data-end="2188" data-start="1767">
<li data-end="1855" data-start="1767">
<p data-end="1855" data-start="1769">Colleges must identify ALN and collaborate with the local authority where necessary.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="1959" data-start="1856">
<p data-end="1959" data-start="1858">Local authorities must <em data-end="1886" data-start="1881">not</em> cease an IDP simply because a young person moves to further education.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2118" data-start="1960">
<p data-end="2118" data-start="1962">Every learner is entitled to an <em data-end="2007" data-start="1994">appropriate</em> education â€” whether in college, through EOTAS (Education Otherwise Than at School), or specialist provision.</p>
</li>
<li data-end="2188" data-start="2119">
<p data-end="2188" data-start="2121">Decisions should always be <em data-end="2164" data-start="2148">person-centered</em> and <em data-end="2185" data-start="2169">evidence-based</em>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
